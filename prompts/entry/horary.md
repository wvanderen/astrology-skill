# Horary reading — entry fragment

Applies when `reading_type` is `horary`. Append this framing to
`prompts/entry/_reading.md`. This fragment adds **framing only**; it never
casts the horary chart or assigns significators.

## Chart-data emphasis

- The **querent's question** — `user_question` is the primary input here, more
  decisive than in other reading types.
- **Significators** when supplied: querent significator (usually the Ascendant
  ruler), quesited significator (ruler of the house describing the matter), and
  the Moon as co-significator.
- **Radicality data**: chart time, timezone, location, house system, and
  whether the chart is cast for receipt of the question.

## Framing for the reading

- State the question and the chosen significators explicitly before judging.
- Flag missing radicality or significator data rather than assuming it; ask for
  it when essential to the question.
- Avoid fear-based or event-certain language; horary describes tendencies and
  movement in a matter.
- Load `references/reading_types/horary.md`. Selection is owned by `SKILL.md`;
  this fragment only names it.
