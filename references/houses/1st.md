# 1st House

Use this module when the 1st house is supplied as a house context. Combine it
with planets in the house, the ascendant sign, the ruler of the 1st, aspects,
sect, dignity, and the user's question.

## Core topics

- Body, vitality, appearance, temperament, identity, first impressions, personal
  agency, embodiment, and the way life is met directly.
- The native's immediate perspective: how experience is entered, initiated, and
  personalized.

## Angularity and condition

- Angular house.
- Planets here are prominent, active, embodied, and hard to ignore. They often
  describe visible traits, recurring life patterns, and the person's direct
  manner of engaging the world.

## Classical notes

- The 1st is the place of life and the helm of the chart. It is central for
  vitality, bodily condition, temperament, and the native's overall situation.
- The ascendant and its ruler are primary witnesses for the body's condition,
  agency, and the chart's practical orientation.
- Benefic support can strengthen ease, protection, health, and social reception;
  malefic pressure can indicate strain, scars, burdens, discipline, or
  difficulties that become part of embodied life.
- Planetary joy: Mercury is classically said to rejoice in the 1st, fitting the
  house of the body, perception, and the native's direct engagement.
- Turned houses: the 1st is the reference point from which derived houses are
  counted. Any house may be read "from" another house (for example, the 3rd
  from the 7th equals the 9th, for a partner's siblings) when the reading type
  is horary, synastry, or an explicit relational question and the supplied
  houses support it; state the derivation explicitly.

## Modern notes

- The 1st describes felt identity, self-presentation, instinctive posture, and
  the psychological experience of taking up space.
- It can show how someone learns to trust their presence, inhabit the body, and
  act without needing constant outside permission.
- Do not treat the 1st as a fixed personality label; it is the point where life
  is filtered through body, style, and immediacy.

## Common misreadings

- Reducing the whole chart to the rising sign or planets in the 1st.
- Reading appearance deterministically or making claims about health beyond the
  scope of astrological symbolism.
- Treating difficult planets here as a damaged identity rather than visible
  life pressures, strengths, and developmental tasks.

## Rulership interaction

- The ruler of the 1st shows where the person's attention, agency, vitality, and
  self-definition are directed.
- Planets ruling other houses and placed in the 1st bring those house topics
  into the body, identity, and personal initiative.
- Judge outcomes through the 1st ruler's condition, house placement, aspects,
  dignity, reception, and relationship to planets in the 1st.

## Source notes

- House topics, planetary joys, and turned-house doctrine paraphrase the
  classical tradition (Valens, Firmicus, Bonatti, Lilly; public-domain source
  texts, modern translations copyrighted — paraphrased, not quoted). The
  good/bad-place and aspect-to-ascendant rationale follows Ptolemy,
  *Tetrabiblos*. See `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md` for the source
  pass and provenance tags.
