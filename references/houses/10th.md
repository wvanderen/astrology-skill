# 10th House

Use this module when the 10th house is supplied as a house context. Combine it
with planets in the house, the midheaven if relevant, the ruler of the 10th,
aspects, sect, dignity, and the user's question.

## Core topics

- Vocation, career, public role, reputation, visibility, achievement, authority,
  leadership, status, honors, and life direction in the public world.
- What the native becomes known for and the work that carries public consequence.

## Angularity and condition

- Angular house.
- Planets here are highly visible, active, and consequential. Their topics tend
  to manifest publicly through role, responsibility, achievement, or reputation.

## Classical notes

- The 10th is the culminating angle and is associated with action, praxis,
  rank, offices, honors, rulers, authority figures, and public reputation.
- It is one of the strongest houses for concrete manifestation because planets
  here are elevated and visible.
- The 10th can describe career, but also any action by which the native is seen,
  judged, appointed, or made responsible.
- Place quality: the 10th is an angle (pivot), configured to the ascendant by
  square and culminating above it, which makes its topics the most visible and
  publicly consequential.

## Modern notes

- The 10th describes ambition, calling, public identity, contribution,
  leadership style, and the relationship to external standards of success.
- It can show how someone learns to be visible, accountable, and recognized for
  competent action.
- Vocation should be read as a developing pattern, not as a single job title.

## Common misreadings

- Reducing the 10th to career alone and ignoring reputation, authority, public
  action, and life direction.
- Assuming visibility is always desired or easy.
- Reading status as moral worth rather than a condition of public role and
  social recognition.

## Rulership interaction

- The ruler of the 10th shows where vocation, reputation, authority, and public
  action are directed.
- Planets ruling other houses and placed in the 10th bring those topics into
  career, visibility, rank, achievement, or public responsibility.
- Judge outcomes through the 10th ruler's condition, aspects, dignity,
  reception, and relationship to planets in the 10th.

## Source notes

- House topics, planetary joys, and turned-house doctrine paraphrase the
  classical tradition (Valens, Firmicus, Bonatti, Lilly; public-domain source
  texts, modern translations copyrighted — paraphrased, not quoted). The
  good/bad-place and aspect-to-ascendant rationale follows Ptolemy,
  *Tetrabiblos*. See `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md` for the source
  pass and provenance tags.
