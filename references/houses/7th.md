# 7th House

Use this module when the 7th house is supplied as a house context. Combine it
with planets in the house, the descendant sign, the ruler of the 7th, aspects,
sect, dignity, and the user's question.

## Core topics

- Partnerships, marriage, committed bonds, clients, contracts, open enemies,
  negotiation, projection, and encounters with significant others.
- The field of direct otherness: who meets the native face to face.

## Angularity and condition

- Angular house.
- Planets here are visible through relationships, agreements, conflict, and
  public-facing encounters with others.

## Classical notes

- The 7th is the setting angle and is associated with marriage, partners,
  contracts, opponents, lawsuits, and open enemies.
- Because it opposes the ascendant, it describes people and forces that confront,
  complement, attract, or challenge the native.
- Planets here can signify the condition of partners, the nature of agreements,
  and the quality of direct relational exchange.
- Turned houses: the 7th is the partnership pivot. Houses counted from the 7th
  describe the partner's world (for example, the 2nd from the 7th equals the
  8th, for the partner's resources; the 4th from the 7th equals the 10th, for
  the partner's family or private base). Use these only for horary, synastry,
  or explicit relational questions and when the supplied houses support the
  count.

## Modern notes

- The 7th describes committed relating, mirroring, projection, attachment
  patterns, collaboration, and the art of negotiating difference.
- It can show qualities sought in others or developed through partnership.
- Relationship readings should preserve agency for all people involved and
  avoid reducing partners to chart symbols.

## Common misreadings

- Treating the 7th as only romance and ignoring clients, contracts, allies, and
  opponents.
- Assuming every 7th-house planet belongs only to the partner rather than also
  describing relational patterns the native participates in.
- Reading conflict as failure; direct opposition can clarify boundaries and
  agreements.

## Rulership interaction

- The ruler of the 7th shows where partnership, contracts, clients, and open
  conflict are directed.
- Planets ruling other houses and placed in the 7th bring those topics into
  relationships, agreements, negotiation, or rivalry.
- Judge outcomes through the 7th ruler's condition, aspects, dignity, reception,
  and relationship to planets in the 7th.

## Source notes

- House topics, planetary joys, and turned-house doctrine paraphrase the
  classical tradition (Valens, Firmicus, Bonatti, Lilly; public-domain source
  texts, modern translations copyrighted — paraphrased, not quoted). The
  good/bad-place and aspect-to-ascendant rationale follows Ptolemy,
  *Tetrabiblos*. See `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md` for the source
