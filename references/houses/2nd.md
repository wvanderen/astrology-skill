# 2nd House

Use this module when the 2nd house is supplied as a house context. Combine it
with planets in the house, the ruler of the 2nd, aspects, sect, dignity, and the
user's question.

## Core topics

- Money, income, possessions, movable resources, livelihood support, values,
  sustenance, skills used for earning, and what helps maintain life.
- Personal assets and the practical means available to the native.

## Angularity and condition

- Succedent house.
- Planets here are steadying, accumulative, and resource-oriented. They may not
  be as publicly visible as angular planets, but they can show durable patterns
  of support, appetite, expenditure, and retention.

## Classical notes

- The 2nd is traditionally associated with substance, possessions, and
  livelihood because it follows the 1st and supports the life of the native.
- Its condition can describe wealth, scarcity, support systems, movable goods,
  food, and the capacity to gather or preserve resources.
- The 2nd does not see the ascendant by major Ptolemaic aspect, so resources may
  require deliberate management rather than being identical with identity.
- Place quality: the 2nd is a succedent place, rising immediately after the
  1st as the house of substance that sustains the life begun there.

## Modern notes

- The 2nd describes self-worth only when grounded in resources, values, and
  embodied security; avoid turning every 2nd-house topic into self-esteem.
- It can show how someone defines enough, builds material confidence, handles
  desire, and aligns earning with values.
- Psychological themes often involve trust in one's skills, comfort with
  receiving, and the difference between security and accumulation.

## Common misreadings

- Treating the 2nd as only money or only self-worth.
- Assuming benefics guarantee wealth or malefics guarantee poverty without
  checking ruler condition, sect, dignity, timing, and broader context.
- Ignoring spending, appetite, debt pressure, and maintenance when discussing
  income.

## Rulership interaction

- The ruler of the 2nd shows where resources come from, how they are managed,
  and which life topics affect earning and support.
- Planets ruling other houses and placed in the 2nd bring those topics into
  money, possessions, values, and livelihood.
- Judge financial and resource outcomes through the 2nd ruler's condition,
  aspects, dignity, reception, and relationship to planets in the 2nd.

## Source notes

- House topics, planetary joys, and turned-house doctrine paraphrase the
