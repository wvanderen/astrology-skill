# 11th House

Use this module when the 11th house is supplied as a house context. Combine it
with planets in the house, the ruler of the 11th, aspects, sect, dignity, and
the user's question.

## Core topics

- Friends, allies, groups, networks, patrons, benefactors, hopes, aspirations,
  audiences, communities, and support from people beyond the family.
- The social field that helps plans become possible.

## Angularity and condition

- Succedent house.
- Planets here can build durable networks, alliances, aspirations, and social
  resources. Their effects often accumulate through people and shared aims.

## Classical notes

- The 11th is the place of Good Spirit and is traditionally associated with
  friends, patrons, benefactors, hopes, praise, and support.
- It has affinity with Jupiterian topics of help, increase, favor, and social
  blessing, though the actual condition depends on ruler, occupants, and
  testimony.
- It is a favorable succedent house configured to the ascendant, making it
  important for assistance and future-oriented aims.
- Planetary joy: Jupiter is classically said to rejoice in the 11th, fitting
  the house of Good Spirit, allies, benefactors, and increase.

## Modern notes

- The 11th describes belonging in communities, collaborative ideals, networks,
  chosen affiliations, and the future someone wants to participate in building.
- It can show how friendship, audience, peer culture, and group identity shape
  opportunity.
- Hopes should be read as living aims connected to real alliances, not only as
  abstract wishes.

## Common misreadings

- Treating the 11th as only social media or casual friendship.
- Assuming groups are automatically supportive; networks can also create
  obligation, hierarchy, exclusion, or pressure.
- Confusing 11th-house public audience and allies with 10th-house reputation and
  authority.

## Rulership interaction

- The ruler of the 11th shows where friends, patrons, groups, hopes, and
  support networks are directed.
- Planets ruling other houses and placed in the 11th bring those topics into
  alliances, community, benefactors, audiences, or long-range aspirations.
- Judge outcomes through the 11th ruler's condition, aspects, dignity,
  reception, and relationship to planets in the 11th.

## Source notes

- House topics, planetary joys, and turned-house doctrine paraphrase the
  classical tradition (Valens, Firmicus, Bonatti, Lilly; public-domain source
  texts, modern translations copyrighted — paraphrased, not quoted). The
  good/bad-place and aspect-to-ascendant rationale follows Ptolemy,
  *Tetrabiblos*. See `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md` for the source
  pass and provenance tags.
