# Mars

Use this module when Mars is supplied as a core natal factor. Combine it with
sign, house, dignity, sect, aspects, motion, and ruler condition when those
factors are available.

## Core significations

- Mars signifies action, heat, courage, severing, conflict, desire, pursuit,
  defense, competition, pressure, and the will to act.
- It describes how a person asserts, protects, cuts away, takes risks, confronts
  resistance, and expends force.
- Mars can signify soldiers, athletes, rivals, surgeons, tools, weapons,
  fire, injuries, inflammations, and people or situations that demand decisive
  action.
- It often shows anger style, libido, stamina, boundary enforcement, and the
  capacity to move from impulse into effective effort.

## Classical notes

- Mars is the lesser malefic and the nocturnal malefic, generally more moderated
  in night charts.
- It rules Aries and Scorpio, is exalted in Capricorn, is in detriment in Taurus
  and Libra, and is in fall in Cancer.
- Mars can maltreat by hard aspect, enclosure, domination, or other difficult
  configurations when the tradition module supports that judgment and the
  needed chart factors are supplied.
- In concrete topics, Mars may signify conflict, cuts, burns, separations,
  weapons, surgery, labor, military matters, competition, brothers, and urgent
  action.
- Mars's condition depends on dignity, sect, house strength, aspects, reception,
  speed, retrogradation or stationing, and testimony from benefics or Saturn.

## Modern psychological notes

- Mars describes anger, agency, libido, courage, survival response, motivation,
  and the ability to say no.
- It can show where a person needs challenge, directness, exertion, and a clean
  relationship with desire.
- Development often involves separating healthy assertion from reactivity,
  cruelty, fear, or inherited conflict scripts.
- Mars works best when force is disciplined by purpose, timing, and respect for
  consequences.

## Constructive expression

- Courage, initiative, stamina, decisiveness, protection, and willingness to
  confront what must be confronted.
- Capacity to make clean cuts, defend boundaries, pursue goals, and act under
  pressure.
- Physical vitality and skill where effort, competition, tools, heat, strategy,
  or risk are required.
- Anger used as information and fuel for direct, accountable action.

## Shadow expression

- Aggression, impatience, cruelty, recklessness, domination, inflammatory
  conflict, or acting before consequences are considered.
- Suppressed anger that turns into resentment, self-sabotage, passive attack, or
  chronic agitation.
- Confusing conflict with intimacy, urgency with importance, or victory with
  actual resolution.
- Under stress, Mars can show burnout, injury-prone haste, combative posture,
  or fear of vulnerability disguised as force.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: connect Mars's action, conflict, desire, and cutting function
  to the topics of its house. Angular houses make Mars direct and visible;
  cadent houses may internalize, disperse, or specialize its effort.
- Sign and dignity: use Mars's sign to describe the style of assertion and
  available tools. Dignity can support decisive action; debility may require
  more skill around timing, frustration, or indirect expression.
- Sect: Mars is more moderated in night charts. In day charts, assess
  mitigation through dignity, reception, benefic aspects, and house condition.
- Aspects: supportive aspects can focus courage and discipline; hard aspects
  may intensify conflict, injury, severance, urgency, or anger. Benefic
  testimony can temper Mars, while Saturn can harden, delay, or weaponize it.
- Rulership: interpret the houses ruled by Aries and Scorpio as topics Mars
  carries into its placement. Mars's condition shows how those topics are
  pursued, defended, cut away, or made reactive.
- Motion and condition: when supplied, use retrograde motion, stationing,
  speed, combustion, or maltreatment to qualify directness, pressure, and
  reversals. Do not infer these modifiers without data.
- User question: for vocation, emphasize initiative and conflict management;
  for relationships, emphasize desire, boundaries, and anger; for health, stay
  non-diagnostic and frame heat, stress, exertion, and recovery.
