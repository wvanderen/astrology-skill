# Mercury

Use this module when Mercury is supplied as a core natal factor. Combine it
with sign, house, dignity, sect, aspects, visibility, motion, and ruler
condition when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- Mercury signifies mind, speech, writing, learning, exchange, interpretation,
  craft, calculation, commerce, messages, movement, and mediation.
- It describes how a person notices, connects, names, translates, negotiates,
  and adapts.
- Mercury can signify students, siblings, merchants, scribes, analysts,
  messengers, interpreters, administrators, tricksters, and technical skill.
- It often shows the style of thought and communication: quick or slow, concrete
  or abstract, precise or associative, playful or procedural.

## Classical notes

- Mercury is common or convertible in sect and tends to take on the condition
  of nearby planets, its phase relative to the Sun, and the chart's broader
  context.
- It rules Gemini and Virgo, is exalted in Virgo, and is in detriment in
  Sagittarius and Pisces.
- Mercury's condition is strongly modified by combustion, being under the beams,
  cazimi, morning or evening visibility, retrograde motion, stationing, and
  aspects from benefics or malefics when those details are supplied.
- In concrete topics, Mercury may signify trade, contracts, documents,
  accounting, education, travel, messengers, tools, dexterity, and technical
  knowledge.
- Mercury can join, separate, translate, or carry matters between planets and
  houses, especially when application, reception, or rulership supports it.

## Modern psychological notes

- Mercury describes cognitive style, language, curiosity, nervous attention,
  pattern recognition, humor, and the meaning-making function.
- It can show how a person learns, explains themselves, solves problems, and
  moves between perspectives.
- Development often involves refining attention, learning when to speak or
  listen, and using mental agility in service of clarity rather than avoidance.
- Mercury works best when flexibility is paired with discernment and words
  remain connected to experience.

## Constructive expression

- Clear communication, skillful learning, analysis, translation, humor,
  negotiation, and practical intelligence.
- Capacity to connect people, topics, systems, and data without losing the
  thread of meaning.
- Adaptability in changing circumstances, especially where information,
  commerce, education, writing, or craft is central.
- Ability to ask useful questions, revise assumptions, and make complexity
  communicable.

## Shadow expression

- Scattered attention, overthinking, verbal evasion, manipulation, gossip,
  intellectual vanity, or cleverness detached from ethics.
- Anxiety or restlessness when the mind has no reliable structure, embodied
  anchor, or truthful outlet.
- Confusing information with wisdom, motion with progress, or ambiguity with
  freedom from accountability.
- Under stress, Mercury can show miscommunication, nervous overload, fragmented
  focus, or using analysis to avoid feeling.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: connect Mercury's thinking, speech, exchange, and skill to
  the topics of its house. Angular houses make Mercury more visible and active;
  cadent houses can emphasize study, movement, service, or dispersed attention.
- Sign and dignity: use Mercury's sign to describe cognitive style and tools.
  Dignity can support precision and adaptability; debility may require more
  care around coherence, boundaries, or practical follow-through.
- Sect and solar phase: Mercury's sect status depends on context. When supplied,
  use morning or evening visibility, combustion, cazimi, retrograde motion, and
  stationing to qualify clarity, speed, confidence, and reversals.
- Aspects: supportive aspects can sharpen learning and communication; hard
  aspects may intensify anxiety, argument, distortion, or technical pressure.
- Rulership: interpret the houses ruled by Gemini and Virgo as topics Mercury
  carries into its placement. Mercury's condition shows how those topics are
  analyzed, exchanged, repaired, or made mobile.
- Relationship to other planets: Mercury often acts as translator or mediator.
  Give extra weight to planets it conjoins, aspects closely, receives, or is
  received by when those factors are supplied.
- User question: for vocation, emphasize writing, analysis, commerce, craft,
  and communication; for relationships, emphasize dialogue and interpretation;
  for timing questions, emphasize messages, documents, movement, and revisions.
