# Neptune

Use this module when Neptune is supplied as a natal, transit, synastry, or
timing factor and the requested tradition mode allows modern or blended
interpretation. Give Neptune strongest weight when it is angular, closely
configured to personal planets or angles, repeated through several chart
factors, tied to relevant house rulers, or specifically requested by the user.

## Core significations

- Neptune signifies imagination, longing, permeability, spiritual sensitivity,
  compassion, idealization, confusion, dissolution, and the loosening of rigid
  boundaries.
- It describes where life seeks meaning beyond ordinary control, where images
  and feelings become powerful, and where clarity may need deliberate care.
- Neptune can signify artists, mystics, healers, dreamers, musicians, poets,
  helpers, victims, saviors, intoxicants, oceans, fog, glamour, and collective
  fantasy.
- It often shows the style of receptivity, devotion, enchantment, yearning,
  surrender, and susceptibility to projection.

## Classical notes

- Neptune is not part of the classical seven-planet framework and should not
  replace traditional rulers, sect, dignity, planetary condition, or house
  strength in classical readings.
- In a strictly classical reading, omit Neptune unless the user explicitly asks
  for modern factors, or mention it only as a modern addendum after classical
  testimony has been established.
- Neptune does not receive essential dignity, sect status, bonification, or
  maltreatment judgments in the classical modules unless a specific blended
  method states otherwise.
- When blending, use Neptune as a modifier of topics already shown by the
  classical chart, especially when it contacts angles, luminaries, personal
  planets, or a relevant house ruler by close aspect.

## Modern psychological notes

- Neptune describes the imaginal and spiritual function: the longing for
  union, inspiration, mercy, beauty, and release from the hard edges of ego.
- It can show where a person is highly sensitive, porous, creative, empathic,
  elusive, or prone to idealizing what they cannot yet see clearly.
- Development often involves distinguishing intuition from fantasy, compassion
  from rescuing, and surrender from avoidance.
- Neptune works best when inspiration is grounded by discernment, practice,
  honest language, and appropriate boundaries.

## Constructive expression

- Empathy, forgiveness, symbolic imagination, artistic sensitivity, devotion,
  spiritual receptivity, and compassion for suffering.
- Capacity to perceive subtle atmospheres, create beauty, soften harshness,
  and honor mystery without needing premature certainty.
- Healing through art, music, prayer, contemplation, service, dreamwork, or
  practices that make meaning without denying reality.
- Openness that remains honest about limits, evidence, and consent.

## Shadow expression

- Confusion, projection, escapism, glamour, deception, vagueness, avoidance,
  porous boundaries, or difficulty naming what is happening.
- Over-identification with the victim, savior, martyr, muse, addict, or
  spiritually exceptional person.
- Rescuing fantasies, misplaced trust, disillusionment after idealization, or
  using mystery to avoid accountability.
- Under stress, Neptune can show fog, depletion, dependency, loss of direction,
  or the wish to disappear instead of engage reality.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Weight: do not let Neptune overpower the chart unless it is angular, exact,
  repeated, connected to personal planets or angles, tied to relevant rulers,
  or requested. Otherwise treat it as background generational symbolism.
- House placement: connect Neptune's imagination, permeability, confusion, and
  longing to the topics of its house. Angular houses make Neptune more visible;
  cadent houses may describe private sensitivity, service, study, or retreat.
- Sign: use Neptune's sign cautiously, especially for generational cohorts.
  Let house, aspects, and angularity personalize the symbolism before
  emphasizing sign meaning.
- Aspects: close aspects to the Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, or angles
  personalize Neptune strongly. Supportive aspects can describe imagination,
  empathy, and inspiration; hard aspects may intensify idealization,
  uncertainty, porousness, or disillusionment.
- Rulership links: in blended readings, if Neptune is tied to a relevant house
  ruler or modern rulership scheme, use it as a modifier rather than the sole
  significator.
- User question: for vocation, emphasize art, healing, service, spirituality,
  film, music, and symbolic work; for relationships, emphasize projection,
  compassion, boundaries, and clarity; for health, stay non-diagnostic and
  frame sensitivity, support, and grounding.

## Source notes

- Neptune is intentionally absent from `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md`:
  it is not part of the classical seven-planet framework, so this module makes
  no classical nature, sect, or dignity claims. Neptune's significations
  postdate the public-domain modern corpus catalogued in
  `references/modern_language_notes.md` (Leo uses Neptune in worked examples
  but excludes it from his seven-ray scheme), so this module paraphrases only
  adjacent PD vocabulary and treats the constructive/shadow framing as a
  contemporary `[Synth]` synthesis, not direct doctrine. See
  `references/traditions/modern/outer_planets.md` for the shared outer-planet
  provenance statement.
