# Moon

Use this module when the Moon is supplied as a core natal factor. Combine it
with sign, house, phase, sect, aspects, dignity, and ruler condition when those
factors are available.

## Core significations

- The Moon signifies body, emotion, instinct, memory, responsiveness, care,
  habit, fertility, fluctuation, belonging, and the changing conditions of life.
- It describes what is lived, felt, needed, protected, repeated, and metabolized
  through daily experience.
- The Moon can signify mothers or mother-like figures, family, home, food,
  bodily rhythm, public mood, common people, travel, and matters that wax and
  wane.
- It often shows how a person receives life: their needs, attachments,
  regulation patterns, and felt sense of safety.

## Classical notes

- Elemental nature: humidifying (moistening), with secondary heating from
  borrowed solar light.
- Sect membership: nocturnal; the Moon leads the night sect, with Venus and
  Mars as its sect partners.
- The Moon is the sect light in night charts and is especially important for
  body, fortune, change, timing, and the condition of the nocturnal sect.
- It rules Cancer, is exalted in Taurus, is in detriment in Capricorn, and is
  in fall in Scorpio.
- Lunar phase matters: waxing, waning, new, full, and quarter phases modify
  visibility, momentum, culmination, release, and crisis points.
- The Moon's speed, application, separation, voidness, aspects, and condition
  are especially important in timing-oriented techniques, though exact
  judgments require supplied data.
- In concrete topics, the Moon may signify mothers, caregivers, the body,
  nourishment, home, travel, the public, fluids, crops, fertility, and everyday
  maintenance.

## Modern psychological notes

- The Moon describes emotional patterning, attachment, memory, mood, somatic
  awareness, and the need to feel held by life.
- It can show what calms or unsettles the person, how they seek comfort, and
  what kind of care they instinctively offer others.
- Development often involves making unconscious habits conscious, distinguishing
  present need from remembered need, and learning reliable forms of self-care.
- The Moon works best when feeling becomes information and connection rather
  than a script that overrides discernment.

## Constructive expression

- Emotional intelligence, nurturance, adaptability, responsiveness, and
  sensitivity to timing, atmosphere, and bodily cues.
- Capacity to build belonging, protect vulnerable life, sustain routines, and
  honor the needs of the body.
- Memory used as wisdom rather than confinement, allowing the person to care
  without losing themselves in caretaking.
- Trust in cycles: knowing when to begin, tend, culminate, rest, and release.

## Shadow expression

- Mood dependence, reactivity, clinging, avoidance, projection, or difficulty
  separating current circumstances from old emotional memory.
- Over-accommodation, caretaking as control, or defining safety only through
  familiarity.
- Instability when routines, home, nourishment, or trusted attachments are
  chronically unsupported.
- Under stress, the Moon can show emotional flooding, withdrawal, dependency,
  or distrust of one's own needs.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: connect the Moon's needs, habits, and fluctuations to the
  topics of its house. Angular houses make lunar matters visible and active;
  cadent houses may internalize, disperse, or make them more adaptive.
- Sign and dignity: use the Moon's sign to describe emotional style and
  regulation. Dignity can support steadiness and receptivity; debility may
  show more complex work around trust, vulnerability, or containment.
- Sect: in night charts, the Moon carries extra weight as the sect light. In
  day charts, compare it with the Sun before making it the primary light.
- Lunar phase: use supplied phase to judge momentum, visibility, culmination,
  release, or transition. Do not invent phase from partial chart data.
- Aspects: supportive aspects can stabilize care and instinct; hard aspects may
  intensify reactivity, family patterning, bodily stress, or emotional urgency.
- Rulership: interpret the house ruled by Cancer as a topic carried by the
  Moon. The Moon's condition shows how that topic is protected, fed, changed,
  and emotionally prioritized.
- User question: for relationships, emphasize attachment and care; for home or
  family, emphasize belonging and memory; for vocation, emphasize responsiveness
  to public need, caregiving, rhythm, and change management.
