# Sun

Use this module when the Sun is supplied as a core natal factor. Combine it
with sign, house, dignity, sect, aspects, solar phase, and ruler condition when
those factors are available.

## Core significations

- The Sun signifies life force, visibility, authority, vitality, purpose,
  recognition, leadership, coherence, and the organizing center of identity.
- It describes what seeks to be seen, clarified, honored, directed, or made
  sovereign in a life.
- The Sun can signify rulers, leaders, fathers or father-like figures,
  reputation, honors, rank, and the capacity to act from an integrated center.
- It often shows the style of conscious orientation: how a person pursues
  meaning, distinction, creative authorship, and self-respect.

## Classical notes

- The Sun is the sect light in day charts and is especially important for
  vitality, authority, visibility, and the condition of the diurnal sect.
- It rules Leo, is exalted in Aries, is in detriment in Aquarius, and is in
  fall in Libra.
- The Sun can clarify and empower planets that are configured to it, but close
  proximity can also indicate combustion or invisibility when a planet is under
  the beams.
- Solar condition depends strongly on sign dignity, house strength, sect,
  aspects from benefics or malefics, and whether the Sun is angular or cadent.
- In concrete topics, the Sun may signify public office, honors, authority
  figures, fathers, patrons, command, rank, or matters requiring recognition.

## Modern psychological notes

- The Sun describes identity formation, conscious purpose, creative agency,
  self-expression, and the need to live from an inner center rather than merely
  react to circumstance.
- It can show where the person wants to feel alive, chosen, respected, and
  capable of directing their own life.
- Development often involves distinguishing authentic confidence from
  performance, approval seeking, inherited expectations, or defensive pride.
- The Sun works best when selfhood becomes a steady organizing principle rather
  than a demand to dominate the room.

## Constructive expression

- Clear purpose, creative authorship, generosity, vitality, confidence, and
  willingness to take responsibility for one's direction.
- Leadership that illuminates rather than overwhelms, giving others warmth,
  courage, and a coherent point of orientation.
- Capacity to act visibly, claim appropriate recognition, and make choices from
  self-respect.
- Integration of personal will with honorable conduct, reliability, and ethical
  use of influence.

## Shadow expression

- Arrogance, entitlement, brittle pride, domination, theatrical self-importance,
  or dependence on admiration.
- Loss of vitality when the person performs a role that is disconnected from
  genuine purpose.
- Difficulty sharing attention, receiving correction, or recognizing the needs
  of others when identity feels threatened.
- Under stress, the Sun can show burnout, shame around visibility, fear of
  insignificance, or over-identification with status and achievement.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: connect the Sun's visibility, purpose, and authority to the
  topics of its house. Angular houses make solar matters public and active;
  cadent houses may make purpose more developmental, private, or service-bound.
- Sign and dignity: use the Sun's sign to describe style and resources.
  Dignity can strengthen confidence and command; debility may require more
  conscious work around recognition, compromise, or self-definition.
- Sect: in day charts, the Sun carries extra weight as the sect light. In night
  charts, compare it with the Moon before making it the chart's primary light.
- Aspects: supportive aspects can stabilize purpose and confidence; hard
  aspects may intensify pressure around authority, visibility, vitality, or
  pride. Include benefic or malefic testimony when supplied.
- Solar phase and visibility: when available, consider whether planets near the
  Sun are under the beams, combust, cazimi, morning rising, or evening rising;
  do not infer exact visibility without supplied degrees.
- Rulership: interpret the house ruled by Leo as a topic carried by the Sun.
  The Sun's condition shows how that topic seeks recognition, direction, and
  coherent expression.
- User question: for vocation, emphasize leadership and visibility; for
  relationships, emphasize selfhood and mutual recognition; for health or
  vitality, stay non-diagnostic and frame energy, confidence, and life rhythm.
