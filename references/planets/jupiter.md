# Jupiter

Use this module when Jupiter is supplied as a core natal factor. Combine it
with sign, house, dignity, sect, aspects, motion, and ruler condition when
those factors are available.

## Core significations

- Jupiter signifies growth, wisdom, abundance, faith, law, protection,
  generosity, teaching, coherence, blessing, and meaningful expansion.
- It describes where life seeks increase, confidence, perspective, opportunity,
  guidance, and a larger horizon.
- Jupiter can signify teachers, priests, judges, advisors, patrons, benefactors,
  scholars, wealth, honors, children, and people of counsel or protection.
- It often shows the style of belief, ethics, hope, generosity, and trust in
  life's possibilities.

## Classical notes

- Jupiter is the greater benefic and the diurnal benefic, generally more
  moderated and effective in day charts.
- It rules Sagittarius and Pisces, is exalted in Cancer, is in detriment in
  Gemini and Virgo, and is in fall in Capricorn.
- Jupiter can bonify by aspect, enclosure, reception, or other supportive
  configurations when the tradition module supports that judgment and the
  needed chart factors are supplied.
- In concrete topics, Jupiter may signify law, religion, philosophy, education,
  wealth, children, patrons, counsel, honors, and protection from harm.
- Jupiter's condition depends on dignity, sect, house strength, aspects,
  reception, speed, retrogradation or stationing, and testimony from malefics
  or Venus.

## Modern psychological notes

- Jupiter describes worldview, optimism, trust, meaning-making, moral
  imagination, and the urge to grow beyond current limits.
- It can show how a person seeks guidance, interprets luck, teaches others, and
  places individual experience inside a larger story.
- Development often involves aligning hope with reality, belief with ethics,
  and generosity with discernment.
- Jupiter works best when expansion is guided by wisdom rather than appetite,
  entitlement, or avoidance of limits.

## Constructive expression

- Generosity, perspective, faith, ethical leadership, teaching, mentorship, and
  ability to recognize opportunity.
- Capacity to connect details to principles, make meaning, and cultivate trust
  without denying complexity.
- Growth through study, travel, counsel, spiritual practice, law, philosophy,
  publishing, or cross-cultural understanding.
- Protective abundance: using resources, influence, and optimism to increase
  wellbeing for more than oneself.

## Shadow expression

- Excess, grandiosity, moral superiority, overpromising, waste, escapism, or
  confidence unsupported by practice.
- Beliefs used to bypass grief, limits, accountability, or inconvenient facts.
- Generosity that becomes rescuing, indulgence, or avoidance of necessary
  boundaries.
- Under stress, Jupiter can show inflated expectations, disappointment after
  overreach, or dependence on luck instead of skill.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: connect Jupiter's growth, protection, faith, and opportunity
  to the topics of its house. Angular houses make Jupiter visible and active;
  cadent houses may emphasize study, counsel, service, travel, or inner belief.
- Sign and dignity: use Jupiter's sign to describe how expansion and judgment
  operate. Dignity can support wisdom and coherence; debility may require more
  care around excess, detail, humility, or practical limits.
- Sect: Jupiter is more favored in day charts. In night charts, assess
  mitigation through dignity, reception, house strength, and supportive
  testimony.
- Aspects: supportive aspects can increase opportunity, faith, teaching, and
  protection; hard aspects may intensify excess, judgment, inflation, or
  conflicts around belief. Malefic testimony can constrain or test Jupiter's
  promises.
- Rulership: interpret the houses ruled by Sagittarius and Pisces as topics
  Jupiter carries into its placement. Jupiter's condition shows how those
  topics grow, teach, protect, or overextend.
- Motion and condition: when supplied, use retrograde motion, stationing,
  speed, combustion, or bonification to qualify confidence, timing, and
  visibility. Do not infer these modifiers without data.
- User question: for vocation, emphasize teaching, law, advising, publishing,
  and leadership; for relationships, emphasize generosity and shared beliefs;
  for resources, emphasize opportunity, stewardship, and proportion.
