# Aspect Precision

Use this module when supplied aspect data includes partial precision, such as
an aspect type without an orb, an orb without applying or separating status, an
approximate aspect, or conflicting exactness notes.

## Core rule

Interpret only the precision that was supplied. Do not calculate, infer,
estimate, or silently fill in missing degrees, orbs, exactness, application,
separation, speed, retrograde repetition, or perfection dates.

When precision is unclear, keep the aspect in the reading only at the confidence
level supported by the source data.

## Exactness and weight

- Exact or near-exact aspects carry more interpretive weight than loose,
  approximate, or sign-based contacts, especially when they involve angles,
  luminaries, chart rulers, house rulers relevant to the question, timing
  triggers, or repeated testimony.
- Close aspects can shape the main synthesis when they answer the user's
  question or repeat a larger chart pattern.
- Moderate or wide aspects should usually qualify the reading rather than
  dominate it, unless other supplied factors make them central.
- Exactness strengthens relevance; it does not by itself determine whether the
  aspect is constructive, difficult, concrete, psychological, or event-oriented.
  Judge quality from aspect type, planetary condition, house topics, reception,
  sect, reading type, and the user's question.
- If the source marks an aspect as `exact: true`, treat it as exact but do not
  invent the degree or minute of exactitude unless supplied.

## Unknown orbs

- If an aspect is supplied without `orb_degrees`, acknowledge it as a supplied
  aspect but do not describe it as tight, close, moderate, wide, exact, or
  loose.
- Do not rank an unknown-orb aspect above an exact or close aspect merely
  because its symbolism is vivid.
- In a natal or synastry reading, an unknown-orb aspect can describe a valid
  relational or psychological pattern, but confidence about its strength should
  remain limited.
- In transit, horary, electional, solar return, or other timing work, missing
  orbs limit timing confidence. Use broader themes and avoid claims about peak
  activation, perfection, aftermath, or dates unless the source supplies them.
- If the reading depends on whether the aspect is close, ask for orb data or
  offer a narrower reading from the available factors.

## Applying and separating status

- Use applying or separating status only when supplied directly or when the
  chart source explicitly labels it.
- Applying aspects can describe building contact, developing pressure,
  incoming support, or the next stage of a matter, depending on reading type and
  aspect quality.
- Separating aspects can describe a contact already made, aftermath,
  integration, waning emphasis, or circumstances moving away from perfection.
- If neither applying nor separating status is supplied, do not say that the
  matter is building, peaking, waning, past, or about to perfect.
- If both applying and separating are absent, write scope language such as "the
  source does not specify whether this contact is applying or separating."
- If both applying and separating are marked true, treat the record as
  ambiguous or inconsistent unless source notes explain the convention.

## Synthesis use

When drafting the reading:

- Separate the existence of the aspect from its measured strength.
- Name uncertainty in proportion to its interpretive importance.
- Prefer phrases such as "with the supplied aspect," "if this contact is close,"
  "without orb data, this should be weighted cautiously," or "the source does
  not provide enough precision to time the peak."
- Record missing orb, exactness, or application data in the reading plan when it
  affects factor weighting or timing confidence.
- Do not use missing precision as permission to ignore a supplied aspect; use it
  as a reason to moderate confidence and avoid over-ranking it.
