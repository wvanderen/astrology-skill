# Synthesis Rules

Build an internal reading plan before writing the answer. Synthesis should answer the user's actual question by ranking evidence, identifying the strongest pattern, and using secondary factors to qualify that pattern.

## Weighting hierarchy

Give interpretive weight in this order:

1. Fit with the user's explicit question.
2. Reading type.
3. Angularity and angular rulers.
4. Relevant house rulers.
5. Exactness and relevance of aspects.
6. Sect, dignity, reception, and condition.
7. Repetition across chart factors.
8. Minor factors only when requested or clearly relevant.

The hierarchy is not a scoring system. A lower-ranked factor can sharpen, condition, or time a higher-ranked factor, but it should not overturn the main testimony unless multiple strong indicators repeat the same correction.

## Identify primary factors

Primary factors are the chart factors that most directly answer the request. Choose them from the user's focus, reading type, relevant houses, their rulers, angular planets, exact aspects, and timing triggers when supplied.

Use primary factors to define:

- Main topic.
- Main actors or functions.
- Concrete life area.
- Core tension, support, or pattern.
- Scope of confidence.

Do not lead with an isolated placement, minor point, or broad sign keyword when a more relevant ruler, angle, house topic, or exact aspect is available.

## Qualify with secondary factors

Secondary factors modify how the primary factors operate. Use them to qualify the main judgment rather than to create a separate parallel reading.

- Sign qualifies style, temperament, pace, and mode of expression.
- House qualifies where the topic is experienced or made visible.
- Rulership qualifies which topics are carried into each other.
- Sect and dignity qualify ease, constraint, resources, and functional strength.
- Reception qualifies cooperation, dependence, or lack of support between factors.
- Aspects qualify friction, flow, blockage, emphasis, or exchange between factors.
- Repetition qualifies confidence when several indicators point to the same theme.
- Timing qualifies activation windows; it should activate natal or base-chart testimony rather than invent a theme by itself.
- Outer planets, asteroids, lots, fixed stars, and minor points qualify only when supplied, requested, angular, exact, or repeatedly tied to the question.

Use secondary factors with explicit linking language: "because," "through," "while," "especially where," "less by X than by Y," or "this is moderated by." Avoid listing secondary factors as disconnected meanings.

## Resolve mixed testimony

Mixed testimony is normal. Preserve the tension and rank it.

- Lead with the strongest pattern.
- Use secondary factors to modify, nuance, or qualify the main pattern.
- Name tensions without forcing false resolution.
- Distinguish concrete topics from inner experience.
- Keep uncertainty visible when the supplied data is partial.
- Treat repeated testimony as stronger than a single striking symbol.
- Treat strong contrary testimony as a boundary on confidence, not as a reason to ignore the primary theme.

## Tradition mode

In classical mode, prioritize condition, rulership, house strength, sect, dignity, reception, concrete topics, and timing. In modern mode, prioritize lived experience, developmental patterning, archetypal symbolism, and integration while staying anchored to the supplied chart factors. In blended mode, use classical factors to judge condition and topic, then use modern language to describe subjective experience.

## Output shape

Prefer a concise synthesized reading with short sections based on the user's focus. Avoid exhaustive placement lists unless the user requests an inventory.
