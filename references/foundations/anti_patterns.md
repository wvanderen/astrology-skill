# Synthesis Anti-Patterns

Use this reference during validation, revision, or quality-control passes when a
reading sounds generic, list-like, overconfident, or disconnected from the
supplied chart data.

## Correction loop

1. Re-anchor the answer in the user's explicit question and reading type.
2. Verify which data is actually supplied and mark missing data as a limit.
3. Rank primary factors before secondary modifiers.
4. Connect each interpretation to a planet, house, ruler, aspect, condition,
   timing factor, or repeated testimony.
5. Rewrite disconnected meanings into a smaller number of synthesized claims.

## Over-weighting Sun sign

Anti-pattern: Treating the Sun sign as the native's whole character, the main
answer to every question, or the default lead even when the Ascendant, Moon,
house rulers, angular planets, exact aspects, or the user's topic point
elsewhere.

Why it weakens synthesis:

- Sun-sign reading is a simplified entry point, not full chart judgment.
- It blurs the distinction between identity, embodiment, circumstance,
  relationship, timing, vocation, and topic-specific testimony.
- It can make every reading sound like a twelve-sign column instead of a
  chart-specific interpretation.

Correction strategy:

- Use the Sun as one testimony: vitality, visibility, purpose, authority,
  conscious orientation, or solar house topics.
- Lead with the Sun only when the question, reading type, chart structure, sect,
  angularity, rulership, exact aspects, or repeated testimony makes it primary.
- If the Sun is relevant but not primary, use it to qualify the main pattern:
  "The Sun adds..." or "This becomes more visible through..."

## Flattening to placement lists

Anti-pattern: Giving a separate paragraph for each supplied placement without
showing how the factors modify one another.

Why it weakens synthesis:

- Placement lists preserve data but do not answer the user's actual question.
- They treat planet, sign, house, aspect, dignity, sect, and timing as equal
  keywords instead of distinct factor types.
- They hide mixed testimony because each item is interpreted in isolation.

Correction strategy:

- Group factors by topic, actor, tension, support, or timing window.
- Use linking language: "because," "through," "while," "especially where,"
  "less by X than by Y," and "this is moderated by."
- Turn three isolated meanings into one ranked claim with qualifications.
- Keep an inventory only when the user explicitly asks for a glossary or
  placement-by-placement explanation.

## Ignoring house rulers

Anti-pattern: Reading a house only by its natural topic, sign stereotype, or
planets inside it while skipping the condition and placement of the planet that
rules the relevant house.

Why it weakens synthesis:

- House rulers connect topics across the chart and show where a house's matters
  are carried, supported, constrained, or activated.
- Empty houses still have rulers; an empty topic house is not an absent topic.
- Natural house associations do not replace chart-specific rulership.

Correction strategy:

- For topic questions, identify the relevant house, planets in that house, and
  the house ruler when supplied.
- Read the ruler's actual sign, house, dignity, sect, reception, aspects, and
  angularity before making a topic judgment.
- Distinguish "this planet naturally signifies X" from "this planet rules the
  chart's X house."
- If birth time, house system, or house cusps are missing, say that house-ruler
  judgment cannot be made from the available data.

## Treating outer planets as always dominant

Anti-pattern: Letting Uranus, Neptune, or Pluto override the chart because they
sound dramatic, generational, psychological, or transformational.

Why it weakens synthesis:

- Outer planets move slowly, so sign placement alone is often collective rather
  than highly individual.
- In classical or blended work, they should not replace sect, dignity,
  condition, house rulers, traditional significators, or topic rulers.
- Dramatic symbolism can distort the actual weight of the supplied evidence.

Correction strategy:

- Give outer planets strong weight when they are angular, closely configured to
  personal planets or angles, tied to relevant house rulers, exact by aspect,
  central to the user's question, or repeated by other testimony.
- Use sign-only outer planet placements as background unless the user requests
  generational context.
- In blended readings, let outer planets describe experiential tone or
  developmental pressure while traditional factors carry concrete judgment.
- Avoid making every tension into crisis, awakening, dissolution, or
  transformation unless the chart repeats that theme.

## Inventing missing data

Anti-pattern: Filling in unknown birth time, houses, angles, house rulers,
aspects, dignity, sect, lots, timing periods, returns, or significators because
the reading would be more complete with them.

Why it weakens synthesis:

- It breaks the skill's input contract and makes the reading impossible to
  audit.
- It can produce confident claims about houses, angles, timing, or condition
  that the supplied data does not support.
- It hides uncertainty instead of helping the user understand what can and
  cannot be judged.

Correction strategy:

- Interpret only supplied factors.
- Ask for missing data when it is essential to the user's question.
- Offer a narrower reading when available factors can still answer part of the
  request.
- Label partial interpretations as provisional and state the exact judgment
  that cannot be made.
- Use scope language such as "from the supplied placements," "if this aspect is
  confirmed," or "house-based topics cannot be assessed without houses."

## Revision checklist

- Did the reading answer the question instead of summarizing the whole chart?
- Did the lead interpretation follow the weighting hierarchy?
- Did house topics include chart-specific rulers when supplied?
- Did outer planets stay proportional to angularity, exactness, ruler links, and
  repetition?
- Did every confident claim trace back to supplied chart data?
- Did the final answer synthesize factors into coherent judgments rather than
  stacking placement meanings?
