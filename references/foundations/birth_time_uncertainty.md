# Birth Time Uncertainty

Use this reference when a chart has no birth time, a rounded birth time, a
reported approximate time, conflicting records, or a low-confidence source note.
The goal is not to discard the reading, but to keep confidence proportional to
the factors that can still be trusted.

## First checks

- Identify the source: timed record, remembered time, rounded clock time,
  noon chart, unknown time, rectified time, or chart software default.
- Check whether the uncertainty could change the Ascendant, MC, house cusps,
  planets in houses, angularity, sect, or Moon sign and degree.
- If the user asks a house-, angle-, or timing-dependent question, name the
  birth-time limit before interpreting.
- Do not rectify, infer, or choose a likely birth time unless the user has
  explicitly supplied a rectified chart as source data.

## Factors That Become Unreliable

Treat these as unavailable or provisional when birth time is missing, rounded,
or low confidence:

- Ascendant, Descendant, MC, IC, chart ruler, angle rulers, and exact contacts
  to angles.
- House cusps, planets in houses, house rulers, intercepted houses, derivative
  houses, and house-based topic assignments.
- Angularity, cadency, succedency, planets rising, culminating, setting, or
  anti-culminating, and claims based on planets close to angles.
- Sect, if the birth time uncertainty crosses sunrise or sunset.
- The Moon's exact degree, aspects, sign, house, phase, and timing when the
  date or time range is wide enough for the Moon to move materially.
- Lots, annual profections by Ascendant or house, primary directions,
  progressions to angles, solar return angles and houses, transit contacts to
  angles, and other techniques that require exact angles or house cusps.
- Synastry house overlays and contacts to another person's angles when the
  receiving person's birth time is uncertain.

## Factors That Remain Usable

Prioritize factors that remain stable across the possible time range:

- Planetary sign placements when the planet does not change sign during the
  uncertainty window.
- Interplanetary aspects that remain within orb across the possible time range.
- Planetary dignity, debility, motion, speed, combustion, and retrograde status
  when calculable without the uncertain time or stable across the window.
- Sun sign, solar aspects, and broad solar themes.
- Slower-moving planets by sign, phase, aspect, and condition.
- Element, modality, polarity, and repeated aspect-pattern emphasis that does
  not depend on houses or angles.
- Topic significators that are not house-derived, such as Venus and Mars for
  desire and relational style, Mercury for thought and communication, Saturn
  for structure and limits, or Jupiter for growth and coherence.
- Timing by transits to confirmed natal planets, with caution around the natal
  Moon if its degree is uncertain.

## Confidence Levels

Use these labels in reading plans and final readings:

- `Timed`: A recorded or otherwise reliable birth time is supplied. Houses,
  angles, sect, and timing factors may be used normally, while still noting
  normal house-system limits.
- `Approximate`: The time is remembered, rounded, or reported as "around" a
  time. Use angles, houses, and angularity only if they remain stable across
  the uncertainty window; otherwise label them provisional.
- `Unknown`: No reliable birth time is supplied, or the chart is calculated for
  noon or another software default. Do not use houses, angles, chart ruler,
  angularity, or angle-based timing as evidence.
- `Rectified`: A rectified time is supplied by the user or source chart. Use it
  only as a stated source, and avoid presenting rectified angles as record-level
  certainty.

## Standard Language

For missing birth time:

> Because no reliable birth time is supplied, I would not treat the Ascendant,
> MC, houses, house rulers, angularity, or timing to the angles as usable
> evidence. This reading can still use planets by sign, stable interplanetary
> aspects, dignity or condition when supplied, and repeated whole-chart themes.

For a rounded or approximate time:

> Because the birth time is approximate, house and angle statements are
> provisional. I can use them where they remain stable across the possible time
> range, but I would avoid firm claims about the Ascendant, MC, house cusps,
> planets near cusps, angularity, and exact timing to angles.

For low-confidence houses:

> House-based topics may be emphasized if the house placement is accurate, but
> the chart does not support treating that house placement as certain.

For low-confidence angles:

> The Ascendant/MC axis is time-sensitive here, so I would treat identity,
> vocation, visibility, relationship, and home claims based on the angles as
> tentative rather than definitive.

For stable non-house factors:

> The more reliable part of the chart is the planetary pattern that does not
> depend on exact birth time: signs, stable aspects, planetary condition when
> supplied, and repeated themes among the planets.

For Moon uncertainty:

> The Moon moves quickly, so if the time range could change its degree, sign,
> house, or aspects, lunar interpretation should stay broad unless a narrower
> time is confirmed.

For timing work:

> Timing techniques that depend on houses or angles are not reliable with this
> birth-time uncertainty. Transits to confirmed natal planets can still be used,
> but transits to angles, house cusps, or an uncertain Moon should be avoided or
> clearly labeled provisional.
