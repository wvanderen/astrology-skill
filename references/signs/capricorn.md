# Capricorn

Use this module when Capricorn is supplied as a sign context. Combine it with
the planet, house, ruler condition, aspects, sect, dignity, and the user's
question.

## Core significations

- Element: earth.
- Modality: cardinal.
- Ruler: Saturn.
- Polarity: nocturnal, feminine, yin.
- Core motifs: structure, ambition, discipline, endurance, consequence,
  responsibility, strategy, hierarchy, material reality, and initiation through
  constraint.

## Interpretive cautions

- Capricorn describes a Saturnian and strategic style, not a complete placement
  by itself. Do not reduce placements here to coldness, career, pessimism,
  control, or status.
- Judge whether discipline becomes mastery, burden, authority, fear, or
  long-term accomplishment through Saturn's condition, the planet involved,
  house topics, and aspects.
- Avoid assuming restraint means lack of feeling; Capricorn may protect
  vulnerability through competence, timing, and responsibility.

## Classical sign-powers

- Category: solstitial (cardinal). Capricorn marks the winter solstice, where
  the Sun's southward decline reverses.
- Gender: feminine and nocturnal (even sign counted from Aries); distinct from
  planetary gender.
- Aspect-pair: obeys its opposite, Cancer, under the commanding/obeying doctrine
  (the winter solstice is the most obeying point).
- Temperament: cold by season (winter), hardened by the earth element toward
  cold and dryness.
- Bodily correspondence: the knees, bones, and skin (symbolic melothesia,
  non-diagnostic).

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Planet: describe how the planet expresses through structure, patience,
  accountability, ambition, and pragmatic effort.
- House placement: connect Capricorn's disciplined and consequential style to
  the house topics.
- Ruler condition: use Saturn's condition to assess limits, endurance,
  authority, delay, maturity, and the sustainability of effort.
- Aspects and dignity: distinguish earned mastery from rigidity, shame,
  over-control, or isolation.
