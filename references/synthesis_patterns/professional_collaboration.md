# Professional Collaboration Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when a synastry question is about colleagues, co-founders,
creative partners, clients, collaborators, professional allies, teachers,
students, advisory relationships, or shared work rather than romance.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. Each person's natal work and collaboration baseline: 7th house and ruler for
   one-to-one agreements, 10th house and ruler for public role, 6th house and
   ruler for labor and operations, 11th house and ruler for groups and shared
   aims, and 5th house factors when the work is creative.
2. Inter-chart contacts involving Mercury, Jupiter, Saturn, Venus, Mars,
   luminaries, chart rulers, angles, and rulers of the 5th, 6th, 7th, 10th, or
   11th houses.
3. House overlays involving the 3rd, 5th, 6th, 7th, 9th, 10th, or 11th houses
   when birth times are reliable enough to use overlays.
4. Repeated themes across both natal charts and the synastry: communication,
   taste, pace, standards, visibility, authority, labor, resources, conflict,
   and shared purpose.
5. Data quality: exact degrees, orbs, birth time reliability, house-overlay
   availability, and whether the user has supplied concrete collaboration
   context.

Do not translate collaboration testimony into romantic compatibility unless the
user explicitly asks for romance. The same Venus-Mars, Sun-Moon, 5th-house, or
8th-house contact may describe aesthetic charge, creative pace, visibility,
trust, resource sensitivity, or shared intensity in a work container.

## Core synthesis questions

- What is the purpose of the collaboration: making, teaching, advising,
  operating, selling, managing, researching, performing, or building an
  audience?
- Which person activates the other's communication, standards, courage,
  creativity, visibility, or sense of responsibility?
- Is the strongest testimony mutual, or does the synastry activate one person's
  natal pattern more strongly?
- Do Mercury and Jupiter support shared language, teaching, planning, meaning,
  and strategy, or do they inflate promises beyond execution?
- Do Venus and Mars support taste, momentum, design tension, and creative heat,
  or do they create friction about pace, ownership, pleasure, money, or style?
- Does Saturn provide commitment, accountability, standards, and durability, or
  does it feel like inhibition, hierarchy, criticism, delay, or unequal burden?
- Do 6th- and 10th-house factors show a realistic division between daily labor
  and public credit?
- Do 2nd- or 8th-house factors require explicit agreements about money,
  ownership, shared assets, confidentiality, or risk?

## Collaboration domains

For creative partnerships, emphasize the 5th house, Venus, Sun, Mercury, Mars,
and any 5th-10th links. Translate attraction-coded contacts into creative
charge, taste, authorship, momentum, feedback, and the ability to finish work.

For teaching, writing, consulting, or advisory work, emphasize Mercury,
Jupiter, the 3rd and 9th houses, and links to the 10th. Look for whether one
person expands the other's ideas, clarifies language, improves judgment, or
encourages overreach.

For operations, production, or management, emphasize Saturn, Mars, Mercury, the
6th house, and 6th-10th links. Look for evidence of reliable process, role
clarity, deadlines, accountability, and sustainable workload.

For public-facing partnerships, emphasize the 10th, MC, Sun, chart rulers, and
11th-house audience or network factors. Separate public chemistry from
behind-the-scenes capacity to coordinate.

## Friction and repair

- Mercury tension can show different vocabularies, planning assumptions,
  editing styles, decision speeds, or standards of evidence.
- Venus-Mars tension can be productive in design, performance, advocacy, or
  campaign work, but it needs explicit handling of authorship, pace, taste, and
  interpersonal charge.
- Moon-Mars tension can create urgency, responsiveness, and courage, but may
  also make feedback feel personal or reactive.
- Saturn contacts can stabilize a long project when responsibilities are named,
  but they can become heavy if one person is always evaluator, gatekeeper, or
  manager.
- Jupiter contacts can enlarge ambition, audience, teaching, and faith in the
  project; check whether Saturn, Mercury, or 6th-house testimony can ground the
  scope.

Name repair paths in practical terms: meeting rhythm, decision rights,
feedback agreements, authorship boundaries, money terms, workload limits,
confidentiality, and escalation processes. Keep the astrology reflective rather
than prescriptive.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve this module after `references/reading_types/synastry.md` when the
  user frames the relationship as professional, creative, advisory,
  educational, operational, client-based, or collaborative rather than romantic.
- Add `references/synthesis_patterns/creative_work.md` for artistic,
  performance, writing, design, or authorship questions.
- Add `references/synthesis_patterns/vocation.md` when the collaboration
  affects public role, career direction, reputation, or authority.
- Add `references/synthesis_patterns/resources.md` when compensation,
  ownership, pricing, shared assets, or business risk is central.
- Add `references/synthesis_patterns/conflict.md` when the question centers on
  friction, critique, accountability, or whether the partnership can handle
  pressure.
- Retrieve exact planet, house, aspect, rulership, and condition modules for
  the supplied factors. Do not infer overlays, roles, or agreement terms that
  are not supplied by the chart data or user context.

## Output guidance

Lead with the collaboration's strongest working pattern, then separate creative
chemistry, communication, labor structure, authority, resource questions, and
data limits. End with practical reflection points rather than a verdict about
whether the partnership should continue.
