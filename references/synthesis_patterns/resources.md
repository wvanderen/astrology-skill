# Resources Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when the user asks about money, income, possessions, assets,
security, spending, earning capacity, self-worth, material support, debts, or
shared resources.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 2nd house, planets in the 2nd, and the condition of the 2nd ruler.
2. The 8th house, planets in the 8th, and the 8th ruler for shared resources,
   debt, taxes, inheritance, risk, and financial entanglement.
3. Venus as a significator of value, attraction, pleasure, cohesion, and ease
   with material or relational exchange.
4. Jupiter as a significator of growth, patrons, confidence, opportunity,
   generosity, abundance, and scale.
5. Saturn as a significator of limits, preservation, scarcity, discipline,
   debt management, long-term planning, and durable structures.
6. Links between the 2nd, 8th, 10th, and 6th houses for livelihood, work,
   obligations, and income systems.
7. Repeated testimony across dignity, sect, reception, aspects, timing, and
   supplied chart factors.

Secondary factors include the Moon for felt safety and consumption habits,
Mercury for trade and accounting, Mars for expenditure or risk, and the Lot of
Fortune only when it is supplied by reliable chart data.

## Core synthesis questions

- What is most emphasized: the 2nd house, the 2nd ruler, Venus, Jupiter,
  Saturn, planets in the 2nd, or a repeated aspect pattern around value and
  security?
- Where is the 2nd ruler placed, and which life topics does it pull into
  earning, spending, saving, and material support?
- Does the 2nd ruler have the condition to build and hold resources, or does it
  require support, timing, restraint, or shared structure?
- Are the 2nd and 8th connected by rulership, aspect, reception, or repeated
  testimony? If so, personal resources and shared or entangled resources are
  linked.
- Are the 2nd and 10th connected (income tied to public role or career), or the
  2nd and 6th connected (income tied to labor and routine)?
- Do Venus, Jupiter, and Saturn describe a coherent resource style, or a
  tension between attraction, growth, and restraint?
- Is the testimony repeated by angularity, dignity, sect, exact aspects, or
  timing?

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Judge the 2nd as the house of movable resources, livelihood, substance, and
  what sustains the body and work.
- Treat the 2nd ruler as primary. Its condition shows the ease, strain, source,
  and direction of material support.
- Read the 8th for what belongs to others — inheritances, debts, a partner's
  resources, taxes, and shared or risky obligations — and judge its ruler
  similarly.
- Weigh dignity, sect, reception, combustion, retrograde motion, and
  bonification or maltreatment before judging how reliably the resource
  significators can act.
- Distinguish prominence from ease. Angular or strong malefics can describe
  visible responsibility, scarcity pressure, debt management, or durable but
  heavy accumulation; benefics can describe support, favor, or flow, but they
  do not guarantee wealth.
- Use repeated testimony before making a strong claim. A single Jupiter
  placement should not outweigh multiple strained 2nd or 8th rulers.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same chart structure into lived experience and
developmental language.

- Use the 2nd and 2nd ruler for values, self-worth, earning style, security
  needs, and the practical base that supports choices.
- Use Venus for what a person values, attracts, and finds worth cultivating;
  Jupiter for growth, generosity, confidence, and scale; Saturn for limits,
  discipline, delayed gratification, and long-term stewardship.
- Use the 8th for shared vulnerability, trust, merged resources, and the
  emotional weight of debt, inheritance, or entanglement.
- Treat financial tension as meaningful data. A strained 2nd, Venus-Saturn, or
  2nd-8th pattern may describe a person learning worth, pacing, boundary-setting
  with money, or repairing scarcity narratives rather than a fixed financial
  fate.

## Resolving mixed testimony

- If the 2nd ruler is strong but Jupiter is afflicted, distinguish reliable
  earning capacity from overextension, overpromising, or feast-and-famine
  cycles.
- If the 2nd is strong but the 8th is strained, separate personal earning from
  shared obligations, debt, or entangled resources.
- If the 8th is strong but the 2nd is quiet, describe shared resources,
  inheritance, or partnership material rather than independent earning.
- If Saturn dominates, emphasize stewardship, limits, and slow accumulation
  rather than interpreting restraint as poverty.
- If Venus or Jupiter dominates without Saturn, describe attraction and growth
  that needs structure, pricing discipline, or planning to become durable.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence directly: capacity for gain may
  coexist with uneven security or recurring obligation.

## Incomplete birth time

When the birth time is missing, approximate, or unreliable, reduce confidence in
house- and angle-based claims.

- Do not make firm judgments from the 2nd or 8th house, their cusps, planets in
  those houses, or house rulers unless the birth time is reliable enough to
  place them.
- If the birth time is approximate, say which parts are time-sensitive: 2nd and
  8th house planets, house rulers, angularity, and planets near house cusps.
- Emphasize factors less dependent on birth time: Venus, Jupiter, and Saturn
  sign and aspects, the Lot of Fortune only if it is supplied, and repeated
  sign-based testimony.
- Use the Moon cautiously if date or time uncertainty could change its sign.
- Frame resource guidance as thematic rather than definitive: likely earning
  style, value patterns, security needs, and material pressures.
- Recommend rectification or a timed chart when the question depends on the 2nd,
  8th, income houses, or the timing of financial events.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve the 2nd and 8th house modules first, then the modules for their
  rulers, planets placed there, and exact aspects to those rulers.
- Add vocation and health routines patterns when the question connects money
  to career, labor, service, time, or bodily capacity.
- Retrieve Venus, Jupiter, and Saturn modules when they rule, occupy, or
  strongly aspect the 2nd or 8th, or when the user asks about wealth patterns.
- Use timing modules only when the user asks about a period, decision window,
  or financial change.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not promise wealth, poverty, inheritance, debt, or financial rescue from a
  single placement.
- Distinguish earning capacity from savings behavior, inherited support,
  shared obligations, and subjective security.
- Treat financial guidance as reflective and symbolic, not legal, tax,
  investment, or debt-management advice.
- When birth time is unreliable, reduce confidence in house-based claims and
  emphasize planet aspects, sign-based testimony, and stated life context.

## Output guidance

Lead with the resource pattern: how value is built, protected, shared, strained,
or grown. Name the strongest evidence and keep recommendations practical,
non-deterministic, and proportional to data quality.
