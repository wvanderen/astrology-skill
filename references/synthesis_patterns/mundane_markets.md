# Mundane Markets and Resources Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when a mundane reading concerns a treasury, national or
institutional wealth, revenue, currency, trade, the economy, commodities,
markets, speculation, debt, taxation, or shared material resources across a
collective. Always load `references/reading_types/mundane.md` first; it governs
baseline factors, retrieval priorities, and the event-certainty and
fear-based-language limits this pattern must respect. Treat every factor here as
collective symbolic weather for a period — never as a prediction of a market
move, a crash or rally, a currency or commodity price, a default, or an economic
turn.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 2nd house, planets in the 2nd, and the condition of the 2nd ruler for
   the treasury, national or institutional wealth, revenue, currency, trade
   balance, and the entity's material resources.
2. The 8th house, planets in the 8th, and the 8th ruler for national debt,
   taxes, shared or inherited obligations, the partner's or counterpart's
   resources, mortality and death rates bearing on the collective economy, and
   financial entanglement between entities.
3. The 5th house and 5th ruler for speculative markets, exchanges, public
   entertainment and spectacle, and the entity's risk-taking or emissary
   activity, when the question touches speculation or market sentiment.
4. Jupiter as significator of growth, expansion, capital, confidence,
  generosity, abundance, and scale across the collective economy.
5. Saturn as significator of contraction, scarcity, limits, discipline, debt
   management, consolidation, and durable or heavy accumulation.
6. Venus as significator of value, currency, attraction, cohesion, exchange,
   and ease in material relations across the collective.
7. Mercury as significator of trade, commerce, accounting, contracts,
   messaging, and the flow of information through markets.
8. The Jupiter-Saturn great conjunction cycle for the longer arc of economic
   order: growth against constraint, expansion against consolidation.
9. Repeated testimony across chart type, sign emphasis, house rulers, ruler
   condition, angularity, dignity, sect, reception, and the entity's
   foundation-chart factors.

Secondary factors include the Moon for collective mood, public confidence,
consumption, and market sentiment; Mars for expenditure, risk, volatility, and
speculative pressure; the Sun for gold, central authority over the treasury, and
the vitality of the economy; sign and triplicity correspondences for commodities
and sectors (only when the source data or the stated tradition supplies them);
and Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto in modern or blended mode when repeated testimony
points to disruption of markets, contagion of mood or speculation, or
concentrations and transformations of capital.

## Core synthesis questions

- Is the market/resource theme centered on the treasury (2nd), shared
  obligations (8th), or speculation (5th)? Separate independent revenue from
  debt, taxation, and speculative sentiment.
- How does the 2nd ruler fare — its dignity or debility when used, sect, house,
  reception, motion, combustion, and aspects? A well-supported 2nd ruler can
  describe coherent revenue and material capacity; a stressed one can describe
  strain, exposure, or pressure on the treasury.
- Are the 2nd and 8th connected by rulership, aspect, reception, or repeated
  testimony? If so, national resources and shared or entangled obligations are
  linked.
- Are the 2nd and 10th connected (treasury tied to government and public
  policy), or the 2nd and 6th connected (revenue tied to labor and the working
  population)?
- Do Jupiter and Saturn describe a coherent economic style, or a tension between
  growth and restraint? Where is the great conjunction in its cycle?
- Are commodity or sector correspondences supplied by the source data or the
  stated tradition? Do not invent a sign-to-commodity mapping from memory.
- Is testimony repeated by angularity, dignity, sect, exact aspects, eclipse or
  lunation contacts, and the entity's foundation-chart factors?

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Read the 2nd as the house of movable resources, the treasury, livelihood, and
  what sustains the collective body and its work. Judge the 2nd ruler's
  condition for the ease, strain, source, and direction of collective material
  support.
- Read the 8th for what belongs to others — national debt, taxes, a partner
  entity's resources, inheritances, and shared or risky obligations — and judge
  its ruler similarly.
- Use commodity/sign correspondences only when the source data or the stated
  tradition supplies them. Classical lists vary across traditions and are
  geographically ancient; do not impose one or assert a region or sector belongs
  to a sign from memory.
- Weigh dignity, sect, reception, combustion, retrograde motion, and
  bonification or maltreatment before judging how reliably the resource
  significators can act.
- Distinguish prominence from ease. Angular or strong malefics can describe
  visible responsibility, scarcity pressure, debt management, or heavy
  accumulation; benefics can describe support, favor, or flow, but they do not
  guarantee prosperity.
- Use the Jupiter-Saturn great conjunction for the longer economic arc and the
  ingress for the annual framing. Use repeated testimony before making any
  strong claim.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same structure into collective, systemic, and
archetypal language.

- Read the 2nd as shared resources and economic mood: revenue, currency,
  valuation, and the practical base that supports collective choices.
- Use Jupiter for growth, confidence, and scale; Saturn for limits,
  consolidation, debt, and stewardship; Venus for value, exchange, and
  cohesion; Mercury for trade, accounting, and the flow of market information.
- Add Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto as long-arc collective forces when the
  tradition is modern or blended. Uranus contacts can describe technological or
  market disruption and sudden shifts in valuation; Neptune contacts can
  describe contagion of mood, speculative manias, oil and the sea, dissolved or
  blurred valuations, and collective idealism about resources; Pluto contacts
  can describe concentrations of capital, debt restructuring, and transformation
  of economic structures.
- Treat difficult market/resource configurations as meaningful collective
  material to integrate — correction, restructuring, redistribution — rather
  than as doom.
- Keep the modern layer non-deterministic and analytical. Describe collective
  process, tension, and possibility; do not predict market outcomes.

## Resolving mixed testimony

- If the 2nd ruler is strong but Jupiter is afflicted, distinguish reliable
  revenue capacity from overextension, overconfidence, or feast-and-famine
  cycles.
- If the 2nd is strong but the 8th is strained, separate national revenue from
  debt, taxation, and shared or entangled obligations.
- If the 8th is strong but the 2nd is quiet, describe debt restructuring, shared
  resources, or partnership material rather than independent revenue.
- If Saturn dominates, emphasize stewardship, limits, consolidation, and slow
  accumulation rather than reading restraint as collapse.
- If Jupiter or Venus dominates without Saturn, describe growth and attraction
  that needs structure, pricing discipline, or planning to become durable.
- If classical testimony (2nd ruler, commodity correspondences) and modern
  testimony (an outer-planet contact) point to different conditions, present
  both as concurrent pressures rather than forcing a single verdict.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence plainly: capacity for material
  support may coexist with uneven security, recurring obligation, or speculative
  strain.

## Missing location and entity context

- If the chart location is missing or approximate, you cannot judge the
  Ascendant, MC/IC, 2nd or 8th houses, house rulers, or planets near house
  cusps. State the limit, then fall back to location-stable testimony: Jupiter,
  Saturn, Venus, Mercury, interplanetary aspects, outer-planet positions, and
  the great conjunction's sign.
- If the entity is unspecified, do not assign one. Describe the market/resource
  symbolism generically and ask which nation, city, market, or institution it
  applies to before making entity-specific claims.
- If commodity or sector correspondences are not supplied, do not fabricate
  them. Offer the period's symbolism without pinning it to a specific commodity
  or market.
- If the entity has no known foundation chart, restrict the reading to
  period-chart symbolism; do not assert enduring economic structure.
- See `references/reading_types/mundane.md` (Missing location and entity
  context) for the full confidence-limit rules.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve the 2nd and 8th house modules first, then the modules for their
  rulers, planets placed there, and exact aspects to those rulers. Add the 5th
  house module when speculation is in view.
- Retrieve Jupiter, Saturn, Venus, and Mercury modules when they rule, occupy,
  or strongly aspect the 2nd, 8th, or 5th.
- For the great conjunction, retrieve the Jupiter and Saturn planet modules and
  the conjunction aspect module; for the modern path, add
  `references/traditions/modern/outer_planets.md` and the Uranus, Neptune, and
  Pluto modules when outer-planet contacts are supplied, restricted to modern or
  blended tradition modes.
- For a foundation chart, retrieve the natal module and read the entity's
  resource structure natal-like. For an ingress, retrieve the solar return
  module for the period-emphasis framing.
- Load `references/foundations/ethics_and_scope.md` for any high-stakes framing.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not predict market crashes or rallies, currency or commodity prices,
  defaults, interest-rate moves, recessions or booms, or the performance of any
  asset, sector, or institution from chart factors alone.
- Do not present Jupiter-Saturn testimony, outer-planet ingresses, eclipse
  contacts to the 2nd or 8th ruler, or great-conjunction arcs as omens of
  economic catastrophe or bonanza. Describe them as emphasis, pressure,
  exposure, or reorientation of collective attention around resources.
- Treat any market/resource reading as reflective and symbolic, never as
  investment, trading, tax, debt-management, or financial advice. Encourage
  real-world professional advice for material decisions.
- Defer to the user's supplied timing window (a fiscal year, a quarter, a
  budget cycle, a trading period). Do not assert when a market move will occur
  if the user has not supplied a timing frame; describe the period's themes
  instead.
- Keep agency intact for institutions, regulators, market participants, and the
  public. An economy under pressure is not fated to a specific outcome, and a
  reading should not be used to justify panic, speculation, or astrology-driven
  financial decisions.

## Output guidance

Lead with the market/resource pattern: how value, revenue, debt, exchange, and
confidence are foregrounded, supported, strained, or reorganized for the period.
Name the strongest evidence and keep the reading practical, non-deterministic,
and proportional to data quality. Prefer language such as "this ingress
foregrounds themes around collective resources," "one pressure on the treasury
in this period is," and "this is better treated as symbolic weather for the
period than as a market forecast."

## Source notes

- Classical topic routing for collective resources (2nd house as movable
  substance and treasury; 8th as others' resources, debt, and shared
  obligations) paraphrases Ptolemy, *Tetrabiblos* IV.2 (material fortune) and
  II.3 (familiarities of lands and their produce), and later topic practice in

  economic arc follows the medieval Arabic/Latin mundane tradition represented
