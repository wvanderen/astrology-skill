# Health Routines Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when the user asks about habits, daily work, maintenance,
stress, illness symbolism, burnout, service, skill practice, food, movement,
rest, or sustainable routines.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 6th house, planets in the 6th, and the condition of the 6th ruler.
2. The Ascendant, 1st house, chart ruler, and planets closely configured to the
   Ascendant for vitality, embodiment, and baseline temperament.
3. The Moon for bodily rhythms, sleep, appetite, emotional regulation, care,
   and habit memory.
4. Saturn for limits, chronicity, discipline, depletion, restraint, and
   long-term maintenance.
5. Mars for inflammation, exertion, urgency, injury, heat, courage, and
   physical activation.
6. The 12th house for isolation, retreat, hidden stressors, confinement,
   self-undoing, and recovery needs.
7. Links between the 6th, 1st, 10th, and 2nd houses for labor conditions,
   workload, resources, and vocational demands.
8. Repeated testimony across aspects, dignity, sect, timing, and supplied chart
   factors.

Secondary factors include Mercury for nervous system habits and scheduling,
Venus for ease and pleasure in routines, and Jupiter for excess, recovery,
support, or growth.

## Core synthesis questions

- What is most emphasized: the 6th house, the 6th ruler, the Ascendant and
  chart ruler, the Moon, Mars, Saturn, planets in the 6th, or a repeated
  aspect pattern around maintenance and stress?
- Where is the 6th ruler placed, and which life topics does it pull into daily
  work, service, routine, and bodily maintenance?
- Does the 6th ruler have the condition to sustain routines, or does it require
  pacing, support, delegation, recovery, or redesign of workload?
- Are the 6th and 1st connected (routine tied to vitality and embodiment), the
  6th and 10th connected (routine tied to vocation and responsibility), or the
  6th and 2nd connected (routine tied to earning)?
- Do Mars and Saturn describe a coherent pattern of activation and restraint,
  or a tension between exertion and depletion?
- Is the testimony repeated by angularity, dignity, sect, exact aspects, or
  timing, and does it echo the user's stated burnout, illness, or routine
  context?

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Judge the 6th as the house of illness, servants, small animals, daily labor,
  toil, and the conditions of service and maintenance.
- Treat the 6th ruler and planets in the 6th as primary. Their condition shows
  the ease, strain, source, and direction of routine, labor, and health-related
  matters.
- Read the Ascendant, 1st house, and chart ruler for vitality, temperament, and
  the body's baseline; use the Moon for rhythms of nourishment, sleep, and
  emotional regulation; Mars for heat, inflammation, exertion, and injury;
  Saturn for chronicity, depletion, and long-term limits.
- Weigh dignity, sect, reception, combustion, retrograde motion, and
  bonification or maltreatment before judging how reliably the routine and
  vitality significators can act.
- Distinguish prominence from ease. Angular or strong malefics can describe
  visible labor, demanding service, chronic strain, or serious responsibility;
  benefics can describe support, recovery, or ease, but they do not guarantee
  health.
- Use repeated testimony before making a strong claim. One striking 6th
  placement should not outweigh multiple relevant rulers and conditions pointing
  elsewhere. Do not predict illness outcomes from chart symbolism.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same chart structure into lived experience and
developmental language.

- Use the 6th and 6th ruler for daily systems, work habits, self-care routines,
  service orientation, and the maintenance the body and life require.
- Use the Ascendant and chart ruler for embodiment, vitality, and how the
  person meets physical experience; the Moon for rhythms of rest, appetite,
  mood, and recovery; Mars for drive, exertion, and reactivity; Saturn for
  discipline, limits, and sustainable structure.
- Use the 12th for retreat, hidden stressors, isolation, and recovery needs;
  use Mercury for nervous-system load, scheduling, and information processing.
- Treat routine tension as meaningful data. A strained 6th, Mars-Saturn, or
  6th-10th pattern may describe a person learning pacing, boundaries, recovery,
  or sustainable labor rather than a fixed health fate.

## Resolving mixed testimony

- If the 6th ruler is strong but Mars is strained, distinguish capable routine
  from vulnerability to overexertion, inflammation, or burnout cycles.
- If Saturn dominates, emphasize pacing, limits, and long-term maintenance
  rather than interpreting restraint as depletion or illness.
- If the 6th is heavy but the 1st is strong, describe demanding routines that a
  resilient baseline can sustain with support and pacing.
- If the 6th-10th axis is tense, name the workload tension directly:
  responsibility, ambition, or public role may pull against bodily limits and
  recovery.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence: capacity for sustained labor
  may coexist with real recovery needs, uneven pacing, or hidden stress.

## Incomplete birth time

When the birth time is missing, approximate, or unreliable, reduce confidence in
angle-based and house-based claims.

- Do not make firm judgments from the 1st, 6th, or 12th house, their cusps,
  planets in those houses, or house rulers unless the birth time is reliable
  enough to place them.
- If the birth time is approximate, say which parts are time-sensitive: 1st,
  6th, and 12th house planets, the chart ruler, house rulers, angularity, and
  planets near house cusps.
- Emphasize factors less dependent on birth time: the Moon, Mars, Saturn, and
  Mercury sign and aspects, and repeated sign-based testimony about pace,
  exertion, and limits.
- Use the Moon cautiously if date or time uncertainty could change its sign.
- Frame routine and health guidance as thematic rather than diagnostic: likely
  stress patterns, maintenance needs, pacing themes, and recovery tendencies.
- Recommend rectification or a timed chart when the question depends on the
  Ascendant, 6th house, or timing of routine or health events.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve the 6th and 1st house modules, the chart ruler, the 6th ruler,
  planets in the 6th, and exact aspects to the Moon, Mars, or Saturn.
- Add vocation when routine strain is tied to work, responsibility, employees,
  or professional ambition.
- Add spirituality when the question concerns retreat, ritual, meditation,
  grief, or meaning-making in recovery.
- Add timing when the user asks about a current flare, burnout period, or
  routine reset.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not diagnose, prescribe treatment, discourage medical care, or predict
  illness outcomes.
- Frame health indications as symbolic patterns around maintenance, stress,
  pacing, and support; recommend qualified medical help for medical concerns.
- Be especially cautious with mental health, eating, fertility, disability,
  addiction, and chronic illness topics.
- When birth time is unreliable, avoid firm 1st, 6th, 12th, and house-ruler
  claims.

## Output guidance

Lead with the maintenance pattern: what drains, stabilizes, activates, or
restores the native. Emphasize practical self-observation, pacing, support, and
professional care where appropriate.

## Source notes

- Classical topic routing for health and routines (6th house as illness,
  servants, daily labor, toil; 1st as vitality) follows the Hellenistic and
  Lilly topic traditions, with Ptolemy's bodily and length-of-life analysis
  (*Tetrabiblos* III.10) as a cross-check; see
  `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md`. Modern developmental language
  (routines, self-care, burnout, pacing) draws on the public-domain modern
  corpus in `references/modern_language_notes.md`. Health testimony is treated
  symbolically, never diagnostically. No period tables or translator examples
  are reproduced.
