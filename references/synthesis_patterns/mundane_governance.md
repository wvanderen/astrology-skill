# Mundane Governance and Leadership Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when a mundane reading concerns government, the executive,
leadership, heads of state, the administration in power, public legitimacy,
institutions of authority, or the collective posture of a nation, city, or
political body. Always load `references/reading_types/mundane.md` first; it
governs baseline factors, retrieval priorities, and the event-certainty and
fear-based-language limits this pattern must respect. Treat every factor here as
collective symbolic weather for a period — never as a prediction of an election,
a succession, the fate of a leader, a policy outcome, or a regime change.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 10th house, planets in the 10th, and the condition of the 10th ruler for
   the government, the executive, the leadership or head of state, the
   administration, and the entity's public standing.
2. The chart ruler and Ascendant of the period chart (ingress, lunation, great
   conjunction) for the collective entity's posture and operating capacity for
   the period.
3. The Sun as the classical significator of the ruler, the center of authority,
   and the vitality-and-purpose center of the polity.
4. Saturn as significator of authority, structure, institutions, consolidation,
   limits, consequences, and durable collective organization.
5. Jupiter as significator of legitimacy, law, belief, expansion of authority,
   and public confidence in governance.
6. The 11th house and 11th ruler for the legislature, parliament, institutional
   allies, organized civic groups, and the entity's supporters.
7. The 4th house and 4th ruler for the land, the homeland, the roots of the
   polity, and (in some traditions) the opposition or foundational base against
   which the government stands.
8. Angular planets close to the Ascendant, MC, Descendant, or IC; they become
   prominent forces or figures for the period and belong in the headline.
9. Repeated testimony across chart type, sign emphasis, house rulers, ruler
   condition, angularity, dignity, sect, reception, and the entity's
   foundation-chart factors.

Secondary factors include Mercury for decrees, law, treaties, official speech,
and the press; the 9th house and ruler for the judiciary, ideology, religion of
state, and legitimacy grounded in belief or law; the 12th house for covert,
secret, or hidden institutions; the Moon for public mood and the people's
relationship to authority; and Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto in modern or blended
mode when repeated testimony points to disruption of governance, mass
idealism or confusion, or concentrations and transformations of power.

## Core synthesis questions

- Is governance the strongest theme, or is it secondary to markets, conflict,
  land, or ideology? Read this pattern alongside the others when the topics
  overlap.
- How does the 10th ruler fare — its dignity or debility when used, sect,
  house, reception, motion, combustion, and aspects? A well-supported 10th
  ruler can describe coherent, capable, or legitimate administration; a
  stressed one can describe exposure, strain, or pressure to navigate.
- Does the Sun, as significator of the ruler, support or strain the 10th
  testimony? Note combustion, sect, house, and close aspects.
- Are the 10th and 4th connected by rulership, aspect, reception, or repeated
  testimony? If so, government and homeland (or government and opposition)
  themes are linked.
- Are the 10th and 11th connected, linking the executive to the legislature or
  to organized supporters and allies?
- Do Saturn and Jupiter describe a coherent balance of authority and
  legitimacy, or a tension between consolidation and expansion?
- Is testimony repeated by angularity, dignity, sect, exact aspects, eclipse or
  lunation contacts, and the entity's foundation-chart factors?

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Read the 10th as the house of the king, the government, the executive, and
  public reputation. Judge the 10th ruler's condition for the quality of the
  administration through the period.
- Read the Sun as the ruler or head of state in the classical universal
  doctrine: its sign, house, sect, dignity or debility when used, reception,
  combustion, and aspects describe the condition of the leadership.
- Where the entity has no known foundation chart, the classical doctrine allows
  the horoscope of the office-holder or ruler to stand in, with particular
  attention to the Midheaven. Note this fallback explicitly rather than
  presenting it as the entity's own chart.
- Weigh sect, dignity, reception, combustion, bonification, and maltreatment of
  the 10th ruler, the Sun, and Saturn before judging how reliably the period's
  governing forces can act.
- Distinguish prominence from ease. Angular or strong malefics can describe
  visible responsibility, heavy institutional burden, or durable but pressured
  authority; benefics can describe support, favor, or legitimacy, but they do
  not guarantee competence or survival in office.
- Use region/sign correspondences only when the source data or the stated
  tradition supplies them; do not invent a sign-to-nation mapping.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same structure into collective, systemic, and
archetypal language.

- Read the 10th as how authority and governance are experienced collectively —
  coherence, legitimacy, exposure, overhaul, or strain in the administration.
- Use Saturn for institutional structure, limits, consolidation, and the weight
  of authority; Jupiter for legitimacy, belief, law, and public confidence.
- Add Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto as long-arc collective forces when the
  tradition is modern or blended. Uranus contacts can describe disruption,
  revolt, or technological and institutional reorganization; Neptune contacts
  can describe mass idealism, charisma, confusion, or dissolved legitimacy;
  Pluto contacts can describe concentrations and transformations of power,
  collapse and renewal of authority.
- Treat difficult governance configurations as meaningful collective material to
  integrate — correction, reorganization, exposure, transformation — rather
  than as doom.
- Keep the modern layer non-deterministic and analytical. Describe collective
  process, tension, and possibility; do not predict political outcomes.

## Resolving mixed testimony

- If the 10th ruler is strong but an angular malefic is afflicted, distinguish
  the administration's underlying capacity from the foregrounded pressure of the
  period.
- If the Sun is well-placed but the 10th ruler is stressed, separate the
  symbolism of the office-holder from the machinery of government.
- If the entity's foundation chart is strong but the period chart is stressed,
  frame the period as a capable polity meeting temporary pressure. If the
  foundation is stressed and the period is supportive, frame it as an opening to
  work with difficult structural material.
- If classical testimony (10th ruler, Sun) and modern testimony (an outer-planet
  contact to the MC or 10th ruler) point to different conditions, present both
  as concurrent pressures rather than forcing a single verdict.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence plainly. Governance periods often
  carry mixed pressures simultaneously; a reading that flattens them into a
  single prediction has overreached.

## Missing location and entity context

- If the chart location is missing or approximate, you cannot judge the
  Ascendant, MC/IC, 10th house, house rulers, or planets near the MC. State the
  limit, then fall back to location-stable testimony: the Sun, Saturn, Jupiter,
  interplanetary aspects, and outer-planet positions.
- If the entity is unspecified, do not assign one. Describe the governance
  symbolism generically and ask which nation, city, or institution it applies to
  before making entity-specific claims.
- If the entity has no known foundation chart, restrict the reading to
  period-chart symbolism; do not assert enduring structural patterns. Note the
  classical office-holder fallback only when the user supplies that chart and
  the tradition supports it.
- See `references/reading_types/mundane.md` (Missing location and entity
  context) for the full confidence-limit rules.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve the 10th house module and the module for the 10th ruler first, then
  planets placed in the 10th and exact aspects to the 10th ruler and the MC.
- Retrieve the Sun, Saturn, and Jupiter modules when they rule, occupy, or
  strongly aspect the 10th, the MC, or the chart ruler.
- Add the 11th house module for legislature, parliament, and allies; the 4th
  for homeland and the roots of the polity; the 9th for judiciary, ideology, and
  religion of state.
- For a foundation chart, retrieve the natal module and read the entity's
  governance structure natal-like. For an ingress, retrieve the solar return
  module for the period-emphasis framing.
- For the modern path, retrieve `references/traditions/modern/outer_planets.md`
  and the Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto modules when outer-planet contacts are
  supplied; restrict this layer to modern or blended tradition modes.
- Load `references/foundations/ethics_and_scope.md` for any high-stakes framing.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not predict election results, success or failure of a candidate, the fall,
  death, or survival of a leader, regime change, revolution, coup, policy
  outcomes, or the legitimacy of any government from chart factors alone.
- Do not present Saturn-Pluto, Uranus-Pluto, Uranus contacts to the MC, eclipse
  contacts to the 10th ruler, or great-conjunction testimony as omens of
  political crisis. Describe them as emphasis, pressure, exposure, or
  reorientation of collective attention around governance.
- Defer to the user's supplied timing window (an electoral cycle, a session, a
  term, a fiscal year). Do not assert when a political development will occur if
  the user has not supplied a timing frame; describe the period's themes instead.
- Keep agency intact for voters, officials, institutions, and the public. A
  polity under pressure is not fated to a specific outcome, and a reading should
  not be used to justify fatalism, polarization, or astrology-driven political
  decisions.

## Output guidance

Lead with the governance pattern: how authority, legitimacy, structure, and
leadership capacity are foregrounded, supported, strained, or reorganized for
the period. Name the strongest evidence and keep the reading practical,
non-deterministic, and proportional to data quality. Prefer language such as
"this ingress foregrounds themes around governance and legitimacy," "one
collective pressure in this period is," and "this is better treated as symbolic
weather for the period than as a forecast."
