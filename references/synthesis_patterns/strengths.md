# Strengths Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when the user asks about gifts, talents, advantages,
resilience, what comes naturally, where to focus, confidence, support, or the
most constructive use of the chart.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The Ascendant, chart ruler, 1st house, and planets closely configured to the
   Ascendant for core agency, temperament, embodiment, and self-direction.
2. The Sun for purpose, vitality, confidence, leadership, authorship, and
   coherent self-expression.
3. The Moon for adaptability, care, memory, intuition, responsiveness, and
   emotional intelligence.
4. Planets in domicile, exaltation, triplicity, strong sect condition,
   angularity, reception, or bonification.
5. Venus and Jupiter as benefic significators of ease, support, pleasure,
   coherence, growth, trust, and social or material help.
6. Trines and sextiles involving the chart ruler, luminaries, benefics, or
   relevant house rulers.
7. Strong houses related to the user's question, especially the 2nd, 5th, 9th,
   10th, and 11th for skills, creativity, learning, vocation, and allies.
8. Repeated testimony across dignity, sect, aspects, reception, timing, and
   supplied chart factors.

Secondary factors include Mercury for learning and adaptation, Mars for courage
and initiative, and Saturn for mastery, endurance, standards, and reliability.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve the chart ruler, Sun, Moon, Venus, Jupiter, any dignified or angular
  planet modules, and exact supportive aspect modules.
- Add topic patterns matching the user's context, such as vocation, creative
  work, resources, spirituality, or relationships.
- Retrieve planet condition modules for dignity, sect, angularity, reception,
  bonification, and maltreatment when supplied.
- Use timing only to describe periods when a strength is activated, tested, or
  ready for development.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not imply that only easy placements are strengths; difficult testimony can
  describe earned skill, resilience, courage, and precision.
- Avoid ranking people, promising success, or using strength language to bypass
  pain, disability, structural barriers, or real constraints.
- Distinguish natural ease from developed mastery and from external support.
- When birth time is unreliable, avoid firm Ascendant, angularity, and house
  claims.

## Output guidance

Lead with the most repeatable strength pattern and name how it can be used with
agency. Include both innate ease and cultivated capacity when the chart shows
both.
