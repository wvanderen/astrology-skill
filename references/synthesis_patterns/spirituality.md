# Spirituality Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when the user asks about faith, meaning, devotion, ritual,
belief, study, dreams, intuition, teachers, pilgrimage, philosophy, divination,
ancestral practice, or spiritual development.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 9th house, planets in the 9th, and the condition of the 9th ruler for
   religion, philosophy, doctrine, higher learning, pilgrimage, and teachers.
2. Jupiter as a significator of faith, wisdom, blessing, coherence, ethics,
   protection, and spiritual generosity.
3. The Sun for purpose, devotion, illumination, authority, and the organizing
   principle of meaning.
4. The Moon for ritual rhythm, intuition, memory, dreams, receptivity, and
   devotional habit.
5. The 12th house for retreat, solitude, surrender, hidden fears, confinement,
   contemplation, and spiritual undoing.
6. Neptune in modern mode for imagination, mysticism, permeability, longing,
   inspiration, and discernment challenges.
7. The 3rd house for daily practice, prayer, study, divination, writing,
   siblings, local teachers, and ordinary sacred attention.
8. Repeated testimony across aspects, dignity, sect, reception, timing, and
   supplied chart factors.

Secondary factors include Saturn for vows and discipline, Mercury for study and
interpretation, Venus for devotion through beauty, and Mars for zeal or
spiritual conflict.

## Core synthesis questions

- What is most emphasized: the 9th house, the 9th ruler, Jupiter, the Sun, the
  Moon, planets in the 9th or 12th, or a repeated aspect pattern around meaning
  and practice?
- Where is the 9th ruler placed, and which life topics does it pull into faith,
  study, pilgrimage, teaching, philosophy, or divination?
- Does the 9th ruler have the condition to support a coherent path, or does it
  require discernment, grounding, study, or caution around idealization?
- Are the 9th and 3rd connected, describing a link between higher doctrine and
  daily practice, local teachers, or ordinary sacred attention?
- Are the 9th and 12th connected, linking belief with retreat, contemplation,
  hidden fears, or surrender?
- Do Jupiter, the Sun, and the Moon describe a coherent path of meaning, or a
  tension between faith, illumination, and devotional habit?
- Is the testimony repeated by angularity, dignity, sect, exact aspects, or
  timing?

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Judge the 9th as the house of the gods, religion, philosophy, higher
  learning, long journeys, dreams, divination, and the search for meaning that
  exceeds the local and familiar.
- Treat the 9th ruler and planets in the 9th as primary. Their condition shows
  the ease, strain, source, and direction of spiritual and philosophical
  matters.
- Use Jupiter as the natural significator of faith, wisdom, blessing, and
  coherence; the Sun for illumination, purpose, and authority; the Moon for
  ritual rhythm, dream, and receptivity; Saturn for vows, discipline, and
  renunciation; Mercury for study, interpretation, and scripture.
- Weigh dignity, sect, reception, combustion, retrograde motion, and
  bonification or maltreatment before judging how reliably the spiritual
  significators can act.
- Distinguish prominence from ease. Angular or strong malefics can describe
  demanding practice, visible religious responsibility, ascetic strain, or
  conflict around belief; benefics can describe support, favor, or inspiration,
  but they do not guarantee spiritual attainment or truth.
- Use repeated testimony before making a strong claim about path, teachers, or
  outcome. One striking 9th placement should not outweigh multiple relevant
  rulers and conditions pointing elsewhere.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same chart structure into lived experience and
developmental language.

- Use the 9th and 9th ruler for the search for meaning, worldview, higher
  education, travel, and the frameworks through which a person interprets life.
- Use Jupiter for faith, generosity, and the drive for coherence; the Sun for
  purpose and the organizing principle of meaning; the Moon for intuition,
  devotional habit, and receptivity to guidance or dream.
- Use the 12th for retreat, contemplation, surrender, and the hidden or
  liminal; use Neptune in modern mode for mysticism, permeability, and
  imagination, with care around discernment and grounding.
- Treat spiritual difficulty as meaningful data. A strained 9th, Jupiter-Saturn,
  or 12th-house pattern may describe a person forging faith after doubt,
  disillusionment, or difficult encounters with religious authority rather than
  a fixed spiritual fate.

## Resolving mixed testimony

- If the 9th ruler is strong but Jupiter is afflicted, distinguish a capable
  search for meaning from overreach, dogmatism, inflation, or unstable
  idealization.
- If Jupiter is strong but the 9th is quiet, describe faith, generosity, and
  coherence without overstating formal religion, pilgrimage, or teaching.
- If Saturn dominates, emphasize vows, discipline, and long-term study rather
  than interpreting restraint as spiritual dryness.
- If the 9th and 12th are both emphasized, name the contemplative tension:
  active belief may coexist with retreat, uncertainty, or the pull of the
  hidden.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence: genuine longing for meaning
  may coexist with doubt, disillusionment, or caution around authority.

## Incomplete birth time

When the birth time is missing, approximate, or unreliable, reduce confidence in
house-based claims.

- Do not make firm judgments from the 9th, 12th, or 3rd house, their cusps,
  planets in those houses, or house rulers unless the birth time is reliable
  enough to place them.
- If the birth time is approximate, say which parts are time-sensitive: 9th,
  12th, and 3rd house planets, house rulers, angularity, and planets near
  house cusps.
- Emphasize factors less dependent on birth time: Jupiter, Sun, Moon, Mercury,
  Saturn, and Neptune (in modern mode) sign and aspects, plus repeated
  sign-based testimony.
- Use the Moon cautiously if date or time uncertainty could change its sign.
- Frame spiritual guidance as thematic rather than definitive: likely paths,
  meaning-making style, devotional tendencies, and discernment needs.
- Recommend rectification or a timed chart when the question depends on the 9th,
  12th, or timing of spiritual cycles.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve the 9th, 12th, and 3rd house modules according to the user's
  question, then the modules for Jupiter, Sun, Moon, and relevant rulers.
- Add timing when the user asks about spiritual periods, initiations, study
  cycles, retreats, or crises of faith.
- Add home when the question involves ancestry, household ritual, land, or
  inherited tradition.
- Add conflict when the question concerns religious disputes, disillusionment,
  coercion, or separation from a community.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not claim spiritual authority over the user, prescribe belief, or present
  chart symbolism as proof of metaphysical truth.
- Distinguish intuition from certainty and inspiration from practical reality.
- Use care around cult dynamics, psychosis, coercive religious environments,
  grief, and trauma; support discernment and grounded help.
- When birth time is unreliable, reduce confidence in house-based claims and
  emphasize Jupiter, Sun, Moon, Mercury, and sign/aspect testimony.

## Output guidance

Lead with the meaning pattern: how the native seeks coherence, guidance,
devotion, or mystery. Keep the language invitational, grounded, and respectful
of the user's stated tradition or lack of one.

## Source notes

- Classical topic routing for spirituality (9th house as the gods, religion,
  philosophy, long journeys, dreams, divination; 12th as retreat and the
  hidden) follows the Hellenistic and Lilly topic traditions, which extend the
  Ptolemaic topic structure without a dedicated Ptolemy chapter on religion;
  see `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md`. Modern developmental language
  (meaning-making, developmental faith, discernment) draws on the public-domain
  modern corpus in `references/modern_language_notes.md`. No period tables or
  translator examples are reproduced.
