# Relationships Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when the user asks about relationship patterns, partnership,
marriage, dating, attraction, intimacy, conflict, commitment, repair,
collaboration, clients, one-to-one bonds, or recurring relational themes in a
natal chart. For comparison between two charts, use the synastry section only
after each person's natal relationship pattern has been assessed.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 7th house, Descendant, planets in the 7th, and planets closely
   configured to the Descendant.
2. The ruler of the 7th and its house, sign, condition, aspects, reception, and
   relationship to planets in the 7th.
3. Venus as a significator of attraction, pleasure, affection, harmony,
   reciprocity, desire to connect, and relational values.
4. Mars as a significator of pursuit, libido, conflict, assertion, passion,
   boundaries, and how difference is handled.
5. The Moon as a significator of attachment, emotional safety, responsiveness,
   habits of care, bodily needs, and the need to belong.
6. Relevant aspects to the 7th ruler, Venus, Mars, Moon, chart ruler,
   luminaries, and angular planets.
7. The 5th house for romance, dating, pleasure, and creative play; the 8th for
   trust, vulnerability, shared resources, grief, fear, and entanglement; and
   the 11th for friendship, community, support, and shared aims.
8. Repeated testimony across rulers, planets, aspects, sect, dignity, reception,
   timing, and supplied chart factors.

Do not reduce relationships to a single placement, partner type, or compatibility
verdict. Describe the strongest relational pattern, desire style, emotional
need, conflict pattern, repair capacity, and developmental path.

## Core synthesis questions

- What is most emphasized: the 7th house, the 7th ruler, Venus, Mars, the Moon,
  an angular planet, or a repeated aspect pattern?
- Where is the 7th ruler placed, and which life topics does it pull into
  partnership, negotiation, or direct encounter?
- Does the 7th ruler have the condition to act directly, or does it require
  support, patience, clearer agreements, timing, or repair work?
- Do Venus, Mars, and the Moon describe compatible needs, or do they show
  different rhythms for affection, desire, safety, and conflict?
- Are the 5th, 7th, 8th, or 11th connected by rulership, aspect, reception, or
  repeated planetary testimony?
- Are the same relational themes repeated by house, ruler, aspect, dignity,
  sect, angularity, or timing?
- Does the chart distinguish between romance, commitment, intimacy, friendship,
  collaboration, clients, and open conflict?

## Natal relationship patterns

In natal relationship synthesis, describe the native's recurring patterns and
needs without making claims about a specific partner's private motives or the
inevitable outcome of any relationship.

- Use the 7th house and 7th ruler for committed bonds, marriage, direct
  partnership, contracts, clients, negotiation, projection, and open conflict.
- Use Venus for what feels attractive, pleasant, receptive, connective, valued,
  and worth maintaining.
- Use Mars for pursuit, heat, initiative, libido, separation, anger, courage,
  and how direct friction is handled.
- Use the Moon for emotional bonding, attachment needs, daily care, mood,
  safety, memory, and the conditions under which the person can soften.
- Use the chart ruler, Ascendant, and 1st house to keep the native's own agency
  in view. Relationship indicators do not erase temperament, self-direction, or
  stated boundaries.
- Use the 5th, 8th, and 11th only when the question points to romance,
  vulnerability, shared resources, friendship, or community support.
- Treat difficult testimony as information about tension, need, pacing, or
  repair work, not as proof that the person is doomed in love or unable to
  sustain relationship.

## Synastry comparison

Synastry compares two natal charts. It should not replace natal relationship
assessment, and it should not be used to declare a relationship compatible,
incompatible, destined, doomed, safe, unsafe, or morally required.

- Assess each person's natal relationship pattern first: 7th house, 7th ruler,
  Venus, Mars, Moon, relevant 5th/8th/11th factors, and repeated themes.
- Then compare inter-chart contacts involving the Sun, Moon, Ascendant,
  Descendant, chart rulers, Venus, Mars, Saturn, and relationship house rulers.
- Read house overlays only when the receiving person's birth time is reliable
  enough to use houses and angles.
- Interpret asymmetry. One person's planet in another person's 7th or aspecting
  their Moon may not feel the same to both people.
- Distinguish attraction from compatibility, intensity from care, familiarity
  from safety, and commitment indicators from mutual emotional fulfillment.
- Keep conclusions qualitative: points of ease, points of work, repeated
  themes, likely sensitivities, practical reflection questions, and data limits.

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Judge the 7th as the house of marriage, partners, contracts, lawsuits, open
  enemies, and those who meet the native face to face.
- Treat the Descendant, planets in the 7th, and the 7th ruler as primary. The
  ruler's condition shows the strength, ease, constraint, and direction of
  partnership matters.
- Weigh dignity, sect, reception, combustion, retrograde motion, and
  maltreatment or bonification before judging how reliably relationship
  significators can act.
- Use Venus and Mars according to the chart context rather than as fixed gender
  rules. They can describe desire, union, separation, pleasure, passion,
  conflict, or the management of difference.
- Use the Moon for bodily/emotional responsiveness, need, changeability, and
  the ongoing maintenance of attachment.
- Distinguish prominence from ease. Angular or strongly configured malefics can
  describe visible conflict, pressure, serious commitments, boundaries, or
  opponents; benefics can describe support, concord, help, or pleasure, but do
  not guarantee relational success.
- Use repeated testimony before making a strong claim. One striking placement
  should not outweigh multiple relevant rulers and conditions pointing
  elsewhere.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same chart structure into lived experience and
developmental language.

- Use the 7th and 7th ruler for partnership expectations, mirroring,
  projection, collaboration, negotiation, and how the native meets difference.
- Use Venus for attachment to pleasure, beauty, affection, relational values,
  consent to closeness, and the style of giving and receiving.
- Use Mars for agency, desire, directness, anger, conflict, pursuit, sexual
  vitality, and boundaries.
- Use the Moon for emotional availability, regulation, habits of care, comfort,
  memory, vulnerability, and what helps the person feel safe enough to connect.
- Treat relational conflict as meaningful data. Tense testimony may describe
  the work of differentiating self from other, asking for care directly,
  repairing rupture, or setting boundaries.
- Preserve agency. A chart can describe patterns and sensitivities, but lived
  behavior, consent, communication, values, safety, and repair determine what a
  relationship becomes.

## Resolving mixed testimony

Rank the strongest pattern first, then qualify it.

- If the 7th ruler is strong but Venus or the Moon is strained, emphasize real
  partnership capacity that may still require emotional pacing, reassurance, or
  more explicit affection.
- If Venus is strong but the 7th is quiet, describe attraction, pleasure, and
  relational values without overstating commitment or marriage themes.
- If Mars is dominant or strained, distinguish passion and initiative from
  conflict style, urgency, defensiveness, or the need for clear boundaries.
- If the Moon is central, emphasize emotional safety, responsiveness, care
  patterns, and attachment needs as major relationship factors.
- If the 5th is strong but the 7th is quiet, describe romance, play, desire, or
  creative chemistry without assuming long-term partnership.
- If the 8th is emphasized, use careful language around vulnerability, trust,
  shared resources, fear, grief, and entanglement. Do not romanticize intensity.
- If the 11th is emphasized, describe friendship, networks, shared ideals, and
  community support as part of the relational pattern.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence directly: for example, desire for
  closeness may coexist with a strong need for autonomy or slower trust.

## Incomplete birth time

When the birth time is missing, approximate, or unreliable, reduce confidence in
angle-based claims.

- Do not make firm judgments from houses, the Descendant, angularity, or house
  rulers unless the birth time is reliable enough to place them.
- If the birth time is approximate, say which parts are time-sensitive:
  Descendant sign, 7th house planets, house rulers, angularity, and planets near
  house cusps.
- Emphasize factors less dependent on birth time: Venus sign and aspects, Mars
  sign and aspects, Moon sign and aspects when stable, luminary contacts,
  planetary configurations, and repeated sign-based testimony.
- Use the Moon cautiously if the date or time uncertainty could change its sign,
  aspects, or phase.
- Frame relationship guidance as thematic rather than definitive: likely needs,
  desire styles, conflict patterns, repair themes, and boundaries.
- Recommend rectification or a timed chart when the question depends on the
  7th house, Descendant, house overlays, angular planets, or relationship
  timing.

## Output guidance

Lead with the relational through-line, then name the supporting evidence.
Separate natal relationship patterns from synastry comparison. Avoid
compatibility scores, soul-mate verdicts, deterministic success/failure
language, and claims that someone must pursue, endure, leave, or formalize a
relationship because of chart factors alone. Keep confidence proportional to
repeated testimony, data quality, and birth-time reliability.
