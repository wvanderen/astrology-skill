# Vocation Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when the user asks about vocation, career direction, public
work, calling, reputation, livelihood, professional development, or the kind of
work through which the native becomes visible.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 10th house, Midheaven, planets in the 10th, and planets closely
   configured to the MC.
2. The ruler of the 10th and its house, sign, condition, aspects, reception, and
   relationship to planets in the 10th.
3. The Sun as a significator of purpose, visibility, authority, creative
   direction, and the will to be recognized.
4. Saturn as a significator of mastery, responsibility, durability,
   professional boundaries, institutions, and long-term achievement.
5. The 2nd house, its ruler, and links between the 2nd and 10th for income,
   resources, marketable skills, and livelihood support.
6. The 6th house, its ruler, and links between the 6th and 10th for labor,
   service, routines, employees, apprenticeship, obligations, and work
   conditions.
7. Angularity, especially planets angular by house or closely configured to the
   Ascendant, MC, Descendant, or IC.
8. Repeated testimony across rulers, planets, aspects, sect, dignity, timing,
   and supplied chart factors.

Do not reduce vocation to a single job title. Describe the strongest public
function, environment, responsibility, skill pattern, and developmental path.

## Core synthesis questions

- What is most visible: a planet in the 10th, the MC sign and ruler, the Sun,
  Saturn, or another angular planet?
- Where is the 10th ruler placed, and which life topics does it pull into public
  work?
- Does the 10th ruler have the condition to act directly, or does it require
  support, apprenticeship, revision, or timing?
- Are the 2nd and 10th connected by rulership, aspect, reception, or repeated
  planetary testimony? If so, career and livelihood are closely linked.
- Are the 6th and 10th connected? If so, public role grows through labor,
  service, craft, maintenance, health-related work, employees, or daily
  obligations.
- Do the Sun and Saturn support the same vocational pattern, or do they describe
  a tension between visibility and responsibility?
- Is the testimony repeated by angularity, exact aspects, dignity, sect, or
  timing?

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Judge the 10th as the house of action, praxis, offices, honors, reputation,
  rank, authority, and public consequence.
- Treat the MC and 10th ruler as primary. The ruler's condition shows the
  strength, ease, constraint, and direction of vocational action.
- Weigh angularity heavily, but distinguish prominence from ease. Angular
  malefics can show serious responsibility, pressure, command, conflict, or
  visible difficulty; angular benefics can show support, favor, patrons, honors,
  or public trust.
- Use sect, dignity, reception, combustion, retrograde motion, and maltreatment
  or bonification to judge how reliably the vocational significators can act.
- Read the 2nd as livelihood support and movable resources. A 2nd-10th link can
  show earning through public role, status through wealth, or career choices
  shaped by financial obligation.
- Read the 6th as labor, service, subordinates, illness, repair, and toil. A
  6th-10th link can show work in service fields, management of workers, public
  responsibility for maintenance, or advancement through demanding routines.
- Use repeated testimony before making a strong claim. One striking placement
  should not outweigh multiple relevant rulers and conditions pointing
  elsewhere.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same chart structure into lived experience and
developmental language.

- Use the 10th and MC for public identity, ambition, contribution, reputation,
  leadership style, and the conditions under which the native becomes visible.
- Use the 10th ruler to describe where vocational energy goes and what kind of
  life material must be integrated into career direction.
- Use the Sun for vocation as purpose, creative authorship, confidence, and the
  wish to be recognized for coherent action.
- Use Saturn for vocation as craft, discipline, standards, commitment, delayed
  mastery, authority, and the work of becoming reliable.
- Use the 2nd to describe values, earning style, skill monetization, security
  needs, and the practical resource base that supports career choices.
- Use the 6th to describe work habits, craft, service ethics, daily systems,
  burnout risks, and the kind of maintenance required for sustainable work.
- Treat vocational conflict as meaningful data. A tense Sun-Saturn or 10th-6th
  pattern may describe a person learning to reconcile calling with competence,
  visibility with duty, or ambition with bodily limits.

## Resolving mixed testimony

Rank the strongest pattern first, then qualify it.

- If the 10th ruler is strong but the 6th is difficult, emphasize vocation with
  real public capacity that depends on improved routines, boundaries, labor
  conditions, or health maintenance.
- If the 10th is emphasized but the 2nd is weak or strained, distinguish public
  recognition from financial ease.
- If the 2nd is strong but the 10th is quiet, describe skill, earning, and
  resource-building without overstating public fame or status.
- If the 6th is strong but the 10th is quiet, describe craft, service, support
  roles, technical skill, or useful labor rather than assuming conventional
  prestige.
- If angular planets contradict the 10th ruler, treat them as visible modifiers
  of the vocational story, not automatic replacements for the 10th.
- If the Sun and Saturn point in different directions, describe both the desired
  expression and the discipline, institution, delay, or standard that shapes
  its realization.

## Incomplete birth time

When the birth time is missing, approximate, or unreliable, reduce confidence in
angle-based claims.

- Do not make firm judgments from houses, the MC, angularity, or house rulers
  unless the birth time is reliable enough to place them.
- If the birth time is approximate, say which parts are time-sensitive: MC sign,
  10th house planets, house rulers, angularity, and planets near house cusps.
- Emphasize factors less dependent on birth time: Sun sign and aspects, Saturn
  sign and aspects, planetary configurations, sect only when the chart is known
  to be day or night, and repeated sign-based testimony.
- Use the Moon cautiously if the date or time uncertainty could change its sign,
  aspects, or phase.
- Frame vocational guidance as thematic rather than definitive: likely
  motivations, work styles, developmental pressures, skills, and constraints.
- Recommend rectification or a timed chart when the question depends on career
  houses, MC, angular planets, or timing of professional events.

## Output guidance

Lead with the vocational through-line, then name the supporting evidence. Use
the 2nd and 6th as livelihood and labor qualifiers rather than as substitutes
for the 10th. Keep confidence proportional to repeated testimony and birth-time
reliability.
