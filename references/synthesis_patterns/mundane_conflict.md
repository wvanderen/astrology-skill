# Mundane Conflict and Nations Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when a mundane reading concerns foreign relations, diplomacy,
treaties and alliances, open adversaries, war and organized conflict, border
tensions, covert or hidden hostility, uprisings, or relations between nations,
cities, or institutions. Always load `references/reading_types/mundane.md`
first; it governs baseline factors, retrieval priorities, and the
event-certainty and fear-based-language limits this pattern must respect. Treat
every factor here as collective symbolic weather for a period — never as a
prediction of war, revolution, attack, terror, the fate of a leader, a
ceasefire, a victory, or any conflict outcome.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 7th house, planets in the 7th, and the condition of the 7th ruler for
   foreign relations, diplomacy, treaties, alliances, international trade, open
   adversaries, and face-to-face confrontation between entities.
2. The 1st house and chart ruler for the collective entity's own posture,
   agency, and how it enters the period's tensions.
3. Mars by sign, house, condition, sect, aspects, and relationship to the chart
   ruler, the 7th ruler, the angles, and relevant mundane house rulers — for
   force, aggression, confrontation, the military, expenditure of energy, and
   confrontation.
4. Saturn for endurance, defense, consequences, attrition, long-term pressure,
   refusal, control, and the weight of sustained hostility.
5. The 12th house and 12th ruler for hidden enemies, covert operations, secret
   or covert institutions, espionage, and matters conducted out of public view.
6. The 4th house and 4th ruler for the land, the homeland, the territory, and
   the roots of the polity being defended or contested.
7. Eclipse and lunation contacts to the chart's angles, the 1st or 7th ruler,
   or relevant mundane house rulers; eclipses are the strongest universal
   trigger in the classical mundane doctrine.
8. Angular planets close to the Ascendant, MC, Descendant, or IC; planets near
   the Descendant especially foreground the 7th-house themes of open conflict
   and foreign relations.
9. Repeated testimony across chart type, sign emphasis, house rulers, ruler
   condition, angularity, dignity, sect, reception, and the entity's
   foundation-chart factors.

Secondary factors include Mercury for negotiation, treaties, messaging,
propaganda, and the press; Jupiter for peace, law, legitimacy, and ethical
framing of alliances; Venus for diplomacy, conciliation, and treaties; the Moon
for public mood and collective morale; the 9th house for ideological, religious,
or legal conflict and foreign affairs conducted through belief or law; the 11th
house for allies, coalitions, and organized supporters; and Uranus, Neptune, and
Pluto in modern or blended mode when repeated testimony points to revolt,
uprising, rupture, covert transformation, or concentrations of coercive power.

## Core synthesis questions

- Is the conflict theme centered on open foreign relations (7th), covert
  hostility (12th), territory and homeland (4th), or internal rupture and
  revolt? Separate open adversaries from hidden enemies and from internal
  pressure.
- How does the 7th ruler fare — its dignity or debility when used, sect, house,
  reception, motion, combustion, and aspects? A well-supported 7th ruler can
  describe coherent diplomacy and manageable foreign relations; a stressed one
  can describe exposure, friction, or open pressure to navigate.
- How does the 1st/chart ruler relate to the 7th ruler by aspect, reception,
  and dignity? Their relationship describes the entity's posture toward its
  counterparts.
- Is Mars angular, dignified or debilitated, and closely aspected to the angles,
  the 1st or 7th ruler, or the luminaries? Angular Mars near the Descendant is
  classic foregrounding of open conflict symbolism.
- Do eclipses or lunations contact the angles, the 1st or 7th ruler, or the
  entity's foundation-chart factors? Treat such contacts as activation of
  existing symbolism, not as omens of a dated event.
- Are the 7th and 4th connected (foreign pressure on the homeland), the 7th and
  11th (alliances and coalitions), or the 7th and 9th (ideological or religious
  conflict)?
- Is testimony repeated by angularity, dignity, sect, exact aspects, eclipse or
  lunation contacts, and the entity's foundation-chart factors?

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Read the 7th as the house of open adversaries, foreign relations, war, and
  treaties. Judge the 7th ruler's condition for the quality of the entity's
  relations and confrontations through the period.
- Read Mars for the conduct, energy, and severity of confrontation: its sign,
  house, sect, dignity or debility when used, reception, combustion, and aspects
  to the angles, the 1st and 7th rulers, and the luminaries.
- Treat eclipses as the strongest universal trigger. Read the eclipse sign, the
  regions associated with that sign when supplied by the source data, the ruling
  planet of the eclipse sign, the mundane houses contacted, and the quality of
  the period's pressures through that ruling planet's condition.
- Use region/sign correspondences only when the source data or the stated
  tradition supplies them; classical lists are geographically ancient and vary
  across traditions. Do not invent a sign-to-nation mapping or assert a region
  belongs to a sign from memory.
- Weigh sect, dignity, reception, combustion, bonification, and maltreatment of
  the 1st ruler, 7th ruler, Mars, and Saturn before judging how reliably the
  period's forces can act.
- Distinguish prominence from severity. Angular or strong malefics can describe
  visible responsibility, sustained pressure, or heavy confrontation to
  navigate; they do not guarantee that violence will occur.
- Use the ingress for the annual framing and the great conjunction for the
  longer arc. Use repeated testimony before making any strong claim.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same structure into collective, systemic, and
archetypal language.

- Read the 7th as relations across borders — how the entity meets its
  counterparts, where diplomacy and friction collect, and how foreign and open
  adversary themes are experienced collectively.
- Use Mars for the activation of force and confrontation; Saturn for sustained
  pressure, defense, and consequences; the Moon for collective morale and public
  mood under tension.
- Add Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto as long-arc collective forces when the
  tradition is modern or blended. Uranus contacts (and Uranus-Pluto, Mars-Uranus
  patterns) can describe revolt, uprising, sudden rupture, or disruptive
  confrontation; Neptune contacts can describe contagion of hostility, dissolved
  or blurred boundaries, and mass idealism or grievance; Pluto contacts (and
  Saturn-Pluto, Mars-Pluto patterns) can describe concentrations of coercive
  power, covert transformation, collapse and renewal of collective structures.
- Treat difficult conflict configurations as meaningful collective material to
  integrate — de-escalation, boundary-setting, repair, transformation — rather
  than as doom.
- Keep the modern layer non-deterministic and analytical. Describe collective
  process, tension, and possibility; do not predict conflict outcomes.

## Resolving mixed testimony

- If the 7th ruler is strong but Mars is afflicted, distinguish manageable
  foreign relations from foregrounded friction or confrontation.
- If the 1st/chart ruler is strong but the 7th ruler is stressed, separate the
  entity's underlying capacity from external pressure through the period.
- If classical testimony (eclipse contacts, 7th ruler condition) and modern
  testimony (an outer-planet contact to the Descendant or 7th ruler) point to
  different conditions, present both as concurrent pressures rather than
  forcing a single verdict.
- If the entity's foundation chart is strong but the period chart is stressed,
  frame the period as a capable entity meeting temporary external pressure. If
  the foundation is stressed and the period is supportive, frame it as an
  opening to work with difficult structural material.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence plainly. Collective periods often
  carry mixed diplomatic and confrontational pressures simultaneously; a reading
  that flattens them into a single prediction has overreached.

## Missing location and entity context

- If the chart location is missing or approximate, you cannot judge the
  Ascendant, MC/IC, Descendant, 7th or 12th houses, house rulers, or planets
  near the angles. State the limit, then fall back to location-stable testimony:
  Mars, Saturn, the luminaries, interplanetary aspects, the eclipse or lunation
  sign and its ruling planet, outer-planet positions, and the great conjunction's
  sign.
- If the entity is unspecified, do not assign one, and do not name an adversary.
  Describe the conflict symbolism generically and ask which entities it applies
  to before making entity-specific claims.
- If region/sign correspondences are not supplied, do not fabricate them or pin
  the symbolism to a specific nation or region.
- If the entity has no known foundation chart, restrict the reading to
  period-chart symbolism; do not assert enduring patterns of rivalry or
  vulnerability.
- See `references/reading_types/mundane.md` (Missing location and entity
  context) for the full confidence-limit rules.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve the 7th house module and the module for the 7th ruler first, then
  planets placed in the 7th and exact aspects to the 7th ruler and the
  Descendant. Add the 1st house and chart-ruler testimony for the entity's own
  posture.
- Retrieve Mars, Saturn, and the relevant aspect modules (especially
  `references/aspects/opposition.md`, `square.md`, and `conjunction.md`) when
  they rule, occupy, or strongly aspect the 7th, 12th, 4th, or the angles.
- Add the 12th house module for hidden enemies and covert matters; the 4th for
  homeland and territory; the 9th for ideological or religious conflict; the
  11th for allies and coalitions.
- For eclipses and lunations, retrieve the transit module and the sign module
  for the eclipse or lunation sign; read the ruling planet of the sign and the
  houses contacted.
- For the modern path, retrieve `references/traditions/modern/outer_planets.md`
  and the Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto modules when outer-planet contacts are
  supplied; restrict this layer to modern or blended tradition modes.
- Load `references/foundations/ethics_and_scope.md` for any high-stakes framing.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not predict war, revolution, terror, attack, invasion, ceasefire, victory
  or defeat, the fate of leaders, the targeting of a specific place, or any
  conflict outcome from chart factors alone.
- Do not present eclipses, Saturn-Pluto or Uranus-Pluto contacts, Mars on the
  Descendant, outer-planet ingresses, or fixed-star alignments as omens of
  catastrophe, violence, or attack. These are among the most fear-prone mundane
  symbols; describe them as emphasis, pressure, exposure, or reorientation of
  collective attention around conflict and foreign relations.
- Do not name a specific nation, city, group, or leader as a target, aggressor,
  or victim from chart factors or from region/sign correspondences. Use
  correspondences only as traditional symbolism supplied by the source data.
- Defer to the user's supplied timing window (a deployment cycle, a session, a
  season, a diplomatic period). Do not assert when a conflict event will occur
  if the user has not supplied a timing frame; describe the period's themes
  instead.
- Keep agency intact for diplomats, institutions, combatants, civilians, and the
  public. A collective under pressure is not fated to violence, and a reading
  should not be used to justify fear, hostility, polarization, or
  astrology-driven political or security decisions.

## Output guidance

Lead with the conflict-and-nations pattern: how foreign relations, diplomacy,
open and covert pressure, defense, and collective morale are foregrounded,
supported, strained, or reoriented for the period. Name the strongest evidence
and keep the reading practical, non-deterministic, and proportional to data
quality. Prefer language such as "this ingress foregrounds themes around foreign
relations and open pressure," "one collective tension in this period is," and
"this is better treated as symbolic weather for the period than as a forecast of
conflict."
