# Consulting and Advisory Vocation Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when the user asks about consulting, advising, coaching,
teaching, writing, analysis, facilitation, strategy, independent practice,
client work, operations guidance, or choosing between employment and an
advisory path.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 10th house, MC, 10th ruler, planets in the 10th, and planets configured
   to the MC for public role, authority, reputation, and career direction.
2. Mercury for analysis, language, writing, teaching, mediation, commerce,
   technical craft, systems thinking, and translation between domains.
3. Jupiter for counsel, synthesis, wisdom, teaching, law, publishing, ethics,
   strategy, and the ability to frame meaning or opportunity.
4. Saturn for standards, expertise, boundaries, specialization, credibility,
   accountability, institutions, and long-term professional trust.
5. The 2nd house and ruler for pricing, income model, skill monetization, and
   material support.
6. The 6th house and ruler for service delivery, operations, routines, client
   maintenance, workload, apprenticeships, and burnout risk.
7. The 3rd and 9th houses for writing, teaching, publishing, curriculum,
   training, law, philosophy, research, and knowledge circulation.
8. The 7th house and ruler for clients, contracts, one-to-one work, negotiation,
   referrals, and open professional counterparts.
9. Repeated testimony across aspects, dignity, sect, angularity, reception, and
   supplied timing factors.

Do not treat "consulting" as a single astrological signature. The chart may
support advisory work through different functions: technical analysis,
teaching, facilitation, operations design, strategic counsel, public authority,
research, mediation, writing, or client stewardship.

## Core synthesis questions

- Is the chart stronger for independent authority, institutional authority,
  service expertise, teaching, writing, operational command, or client-facing
  counsel?
- Do Mercury, Jupiter, and Saturn cooperate? Consulting is strongest when
  language and analysis, big-picture judgment, and credible standards all have
  support.
- Does the 10th connect to the 2nd or 6th, showing a path from public role to
  income and delivery?
- Does the 7th support one-to-one agreements, client relationships, negotiation,
  or referral-based work?
- Does the 3rd or 9th show a knowledge channel: writing, teaching, training,
  publishing, law, research, or interpretation?
- Is the 6th heavy or strained, suggesting that delivery systems, scope control,
  support, or recovery time are essential?
- Is Saturn prominent enough for expertise and trust, or too heavy without
  enough Mercury/Jupiter flexibility?
- Are Mars or the Sun prominent, indicating leadership, entrepreneurship,
  advocacy, executive presence, or willingness to take public responsibility?

## Comparing options

When the user is choosing between a stable operations role, teaching and
writing, consulting, advisory work, or another professional option, rank the
options from supplied evidence rather than from generic preference.

- Stable operations is supported by strong 6th-house, Saturn, Mercury, Mars, or
  10th-6th testimony, especially when the chart favors systems, maintenance,
  accountability, process, or management.
- Teaching and writing are supported by Mercury, Jupiter, the 3rd, the 9th, the
  Sun, and 10th links to communication or publication.
- Consulting and advisory work are supported when 10th authority, 7th client
  dynamics, 2nd income capacity, 6th delivery systems, Mercury analysis,
  Jupiter synthesis, and Saturn credibility repeat the same theme.
- Independent practice requires more than vocational symbolism. Look for
  testimony for income, workload, client relationships, boundaries, and public
  visibility before encouraging a leap away from stable employment.

Name the strongest path, the secondary path, and the practical conditions that
would make each path more viable. Do not tell the user to quit, accept, invest,
or make a major financial decision solely from astrology.

## Delivery risks

- Mercury without Saturn may describe skill and language without enough
  structure, pricing discipline, or scope control.
- Jupiter without Mercury may describe vision without enough operational
  clarity or evidence.
- Saturn without Jupiter may describe expertise that becomes too narrow,
  cautious, credential-bound, or slow to market.
- A strong 10th with a strained 6th can show public capacity that depends on
  workload design, delegation, pacing, and recovery.
- A strong 7th with strained 2nd or 8th factors can show client demand or
  partnership activity that needs clearer money, contracts, and boundaries.
- Heavy 12th-house or Neptune testimony in modern mode may require special
  care around unclear scope, rescue dynamics, invisible labor, and exhaustion.

  business model, security, or leaving employment is part of the question.

  burnout, sustainability, or service delivery pressure is part of the question.
- Retrieve Mercury, Jupiter, Saturn, Sun, Mars, 2nd, 3rd, 6th, 7th, 9th, and
  10th modules only as they match supplied factors. Include rulership and
  condition modules when the chart data supplies them.
- When birth time is unreliable, do not claim client, income, operations, or
  career-house strength from houses or angles. Use Mercury/Jupiter/Saturn/Sun
  aspects and sign-based testimony as lower-confidence vocational style.

## Output guidance

Lead with the professional function the chart most consistently supports:
analyst, teacher, strategist, operator, counselor, writer, facilitator,
manager, advocate, or builder of systems. Then explain what makes consulting or
advisory work viable, what would need structure, and which alternatives appear
stronger or safer from the supplied testimony.
