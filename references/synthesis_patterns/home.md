# Home Synthesis Pattern

Use this pattern when the user asks about home, family, roots, ancestry,
parents, domestic life, privacy, belonging, relocation, land, endings, or the
inner foundation of life.

## Primary testimony

Prioritize evidence in this order:

1. The 4th house, IC, planets in the 4th, and planets closely configured to the
   IC.
2. The ruler of the 4th and its house, sign, condition, aspects, reception, and
   relationship to planets in the 4th.
3. The Moon as a significator of family memory, care, attachment, shelter,
   bodily comfort, and emotional belonging.
4. Saturn for ancestry, duty, boundaries, age, inheritance, land, family
   burdens, and the weight of the past.
5. The 10th house as the public/private counterpoint to the 4th, especially
   when family life and public role are linked.
6. The 2nd and 8th houses for property, household resources, inheritance, and
   shared obligations.
7. Repeated testimony across rulers, aspects, dignity, sect, angularity,
   timing, and supplied chart factors.

Secondary factors include Cancer, the Moon's ruler, planets aspecting the Moon,
and benefic or malefic testimony to the 4th ruler.

## Core synthesis questions

- What is most emphasized: the 4th house, the IC, the 4th ruler, the Moon,
  Saturn, planets in the 4th, or a repeated aspect pattern around shelter and
  belonging?
- Where is the 4th ruler placed, and which life topics does it pull into home,
  family, land, privacy, and endings?
- Does the 4th ruler have the condition to provide a stable foundation, or does
  it require support, repair, boundary work, relocation, or time?
- Are the 4th and 10th connected, describing tension or exchange between
  private foundations and public role?
- Are the 4th and 2nd or 8th connected, linking home to property, household
  resources, inheritance, or shared obligations?
- Do the Moon and Saturn describe a coherent pattern of belonging, or a tension
  between emotional shelter and the weight of ancestry, duty, or the past?
- Is the testimony repeated by angularity, dignity, sect, exact aspects, or
  timing?

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with concrete chart structure.

- Judge the 4th as the house of parents, ancestry, roots, land, hidden
  foundations, treasure, and the endings of life.
- Treat the IC, planets in the 4th, and the 4th ruler as primary. The ruler's
  condition shows the ease, strain, source, and direction of domestic and
  ancestral matters.
- Read the 10th as the public counterpoint to the 4th; the 4th-10th axis maps
  private roots against public visibility and authority.
- Weigh dignity, sect, reception, combustion, retrograde motion, and
  bonification or maltreatment before judging how reliably the home
  significators can act.
- Distinguish prominence from ease. Angular or strong malefics can describe
  visible responsibility, heavy ancestry, endurance, or early separation;
  benefics can describe support, comfort, or favor, but they do not guarantee a
  trouble-free home.
- Use repeated testimony before making a strong claim about parents, land, or
  endings. One striking 4th placement should not outweigh multiple relevant
  rulers and conditions pointing elsewhere.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same chart structure into lived experience and
developmental language.

- Use the 4th and IC for the inner foundation: emotional grounding, private
  life, family patterns, belonging, and the conditions under which the person
  can rest.
- Use the Moon for family memory, care, attachment, shelter, and bodily
  comfort; use Saturn for the weight of ancestry, duty, inheritance, and the
  structures a person builds or carries.
- Use the 4th ruler to describe where the search for home, privacy, or roots
  tends to operate and what life material it draws into domestic life.
- Treat domestic tension as meaningful data. A strained 4th, Moon-Saturn, or
  4th-10th pattern may describe a person building belonging after displacement,
  estrangement, caregiving load, or unclear roots rather than a fixed domestic
  fate.

## Resolving mixed testimony

- If the 4th ruler is strong but the Moon is strained, distinguish a capable
  outer foundation from emotional unrest or unmet attachment needs at home.
- If the Moon is strong but the 4th is quiet, describe emotional belonging and
  care patterns without overstating property, ancestry, or literal housing.
- If Saturn dominates, emphasize structures, duty, endurance, and slow
  consolidation rather than interpreting restraint as deprivation or loss.
- If the 4th-10th axis is tense, name the public-private tension directly:
  visibility and career may pull against privacy, rest, or family presence.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence: a strong drive to build a home
  may coexist with recurring uprooting, caregiving, or a complicated
  relationship to ancestry.

## Incomplete birth time

When the birth time is missing, approximate, or unreliable, reduce confidence in
angle-based and house-based claims.

- Do not make firm judgments from the IC, the 4th house, planets in the 4th, or
  the 4th ruler unless the birth time is reliable enough to place them.
- If the birth time is approximate, say which parts are time-sensitive: IC sign,
  4th house planets, house rulers, angularity, and planets near house cusps.
- Emphasize factors less dependent on birth time: the Moon sign and aspects,
  Saturn sign and aspects, Cancer testimony, and repeated sign-based themes.
- Use the Moon cautiously if date or time uncertainty could change its sign.
- Frame home guidance as thematic rather than definitive: likely attachment
  style, belonging needs, ancestral themes, and shelter patterns.
- Recommend rectification or a timed chart when the question depends on the IC,
  4th house, relocation, property timing, or family events.

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve the 4th house module, the 4th ruler's planet module, any planet in
  the 4th, and exact aspects involving the Moon or 4th ruler.
- Add resources when the question concerns property, inheritance, rent,
  household money, or material security.
- Add relationships when the question centers on family dynamics,
  cohabitation, marriage, or domestic agreements.
- Add timing when the user asks about moving, buying, selling, endings, or
  family events during a specific period.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not diagnose family members, assign blame, or claim certain events from
  chart symbolism alone.
- Distinguish the native's inner foundation from literal family circumstances
  unless the chart testimony and user context both support the link.
- Use care around trauma, estrangement, grief, inheritance, and housing
  insecurity; offer symbolic framing and agency rather than certainty.
- When birth time is unreliable, avoid firm IC, 4th house, angularity, and
  house-ruler claims.

## Output guidance

Lead with the home through-line: what creates shelter, continuity, privacy, and
belonging. Name whether the pattern is primarily emotional, ancestral,
material, relational, or locational.

## Source notes

- Classical topic routing for home (4th house as parents, land, foundations,
  endings; 4th-10th as private-public axis) paraphrases Ptolemy, *Tetrabiblos*
  III.4 (parents) and the Hellenistic and Lilly topic traditions; see
  `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md`. Modern developmental language
  (inner foundation, belonging, attachment) draws on the public-domain modern
  corpus in `references/modern_language_notes.md`. No period tables or
  translator examples are reproduced.
