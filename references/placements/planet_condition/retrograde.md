# Retrograde

Use this module when a planet is explicitly supplied as retrograde.

## Core significations

- Retrograde motion describes a planet whose expression turns back, revises,
  reconsiders, repeats, or works against a simple forward rhythm.
- The planet may act internally, irregularly, retrospectively, or through
  returns to unfinished material.
- Retrograde does not mean broken. It often marks a function that develops
  through review, delay, repetition, or nonstandard timing.

## Interpretive effect

- Emphasize reflection, revision, reversals, hesitation, recovery, or a need to
  renegotiate the planet's topics.
- The planet may be strong but less linear, especially when angular or otherwise
  emphasized.
- Retrograde can describe skepticism toward inherited scripts around the
  planet's topic and a need to build authority from lived experience.

## Required cautions

- Do not treat retrograde as an automatic weakness or failure.
- Do not over-weight retrograde in isolation. Judge the planet's sign, house,
  ruler, sect, speed, visibility, aspects, and whether the reading question is
  actually about timing or expression.

## Synthesis examples

- "Retrograde Mercury suggests the voice becomes more effective through revision
  and second thoughts, not because the mind is weak but because it refuses easy
  assumptions."
- "Retrograde Saturn may make responsibility feel delayed or self-questioning at
  first, then increasingly authoritative through tested experience."
- "Because Mars is retrograde and angular, desire and anger may be highly
  visible but require conscious pacing and repair after false starts."

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Planet function: describe what is reviewed, repeated, internalized, or
  reworked.
- House placement: name the life area where timing may be nonlinear.
- Aspects and ruler condition: distinguish productive revision from inhibition,
  conflict, or lack of support.
- Reading type: in timing work, connect retrograde to returns and reversals only
  when the chart technique supports that claim.
