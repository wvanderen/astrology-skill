# Face

Use this module when a planet is explicitly supplied as having face dignity,
decan dignity, or occupying a named decan.

## Core significations

- Face, also called decan, describes a planet's ability to maintain a role,
  image, station, or local coherence within a small division of a sign.
- It is a minor dignity that can give the planet enough identity or footing to
  hold itself together.
- Face dignity is not moral goodness, popularity, or guaranteed success. It is a
  modest condition of recognition and role maintenance.

## Interpretive effect

- Emphasize presentation, resilience of identity, craft, and the ability to keep
  a recognizable function under pressure.
- Face can matter when a planet otherwise lacks essential dignity, showing a
  small but real place to stand.
- The effect is usually subtle and should not outweigh domicile, exaltation,
  detriment, fall, house strength, sect, or major aspects.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: name where the planet maintains a role, image, or local
  coherence.
- Decan or face lord condition: use the named face lord when supplied, especially
  for reception or dignity nuance.
- Other condition: treat face as minor support that can qualify peregrine,
  debilitated, cadent, or stressed placements without erasing stronger
  testimonies.
- User question: include face when the question concerns identity, visibility,
  craft, reputation, or the planet's ability to sustain a specific role.
