# Domicile

Use this module when a planet is explicitly supplied as in domicile, rulership,
or its own sign.

## Core significations

- Domicile describes a planet acting from its own territory, priorities, and
  rules.
- The planet has direct access to its significations and usually needs less
  mediation from another planet's agenda.
- This is a condition of authority and coherence, not a guarantee of pleasant
  outcomes or moral virtue.

## Interpretive effect

- Emphasize agency, steadiness, and the ability to organize the house topics the
  planet occupies or rules.
- The planet may set terms, define standards, and act with recognizable
  consistency.
- Strong domicile can intensify the planet's nature. A dignified malefic is not
  automatically gentle; it may be more capable, disciplined, or effective in its
  own kind of action.

## Classical notes

- Domicile (the classical "houses of the planets") is the strongest essential
  dignity: the planet acts from its own sign and on its own terms.
- Ptolemy rationalizes the scheme outward from the luminaries — the Sun takes Leo
  and the Moon Cancer (the northernmost, hottest signs), and the remaining
  planets are allocated by sphere order and nature, with Saturn taking Capricorn
  and Aquarius opposite the luminaries.
- Domicile is essential (sign-based) authority, distinct from accidental strength
  such as angularity, motion, or visibility. A dignified planet still needs
  accidental support to act visibly and on schedule.
- Read dignity as a planet more fully present in its function, not as morally
  superior. A dignified malefic is not softened; it is more capable, disciplined,
  and effective in its own kind of action.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: locate where the planet has room to act directly.
- Rulership: connect the houses ruled by the planet to the planet's own agenda
  and condition.
- Aspects: supportive aspects can make the planet's authority easier to share;
  hard aspects can show where its confidence becomes pressure or conflict.
- Sect and other condition: dignity describes capacity, while sect, angularity,
  reception, combustion, maltreatment, and benefic testimony describe how cleanly
  that capacity operates.
