# Under Beams

Use this module when a planet is explicitly supplied as under the beams or
hidden by solar light, but not cazimi.

## Core significations

- Under beams describes a planet close enough to the Sun to lose visibility, but
  not necessarily close enough to be combust.
- The planet's topics may be hidden, private, emerging, protected from public
  view, or difficult to separate from solar concerns.
- This condition often speaks to visibility and disclosure more than absolute
  strength or weakness.

## Interpretive effect

- Emphasize concealment, incubation, low visibility, or a planet operating
  behind the scenes.
- The planet may need timing, distance, or supportive testimony before its
  significations become clear to others.
- Under beams can describe privacy and gestation as well as obscurity, fatigue,
  or uncertainty.

## Required cautions

- Do not treat under beams as identical to combustion. Use the exact condition
  supplied by the chart data.
- Do not over-weight under beams in isolation. Balance it with dignity,
  angularity, house placement, phase, sect, aspects, reception, and the planet's
  role in the question.

## Synthesis examples

- "Venus under beams may keep desire and affection private at first; in the 11th
  house this can show social bonds developing outside obvious display."
- "Jupiter under beams can still offer help, but its support may arrive through
  quieter channels or become visible only after the timing opens."
- "Because Mercury is under beams but angular, the voice is both central and
  partially obscured, making careful timing around disclosure important."

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Solar visibility: describe what is hidden, incubating, or difficult to see
  clearly.
- House placement: name where topics may operate privately or behind the scenes.
- Comparison: distinguish under beams from combustion and cazimi when exact
  proximity is supplied.
- Supporting testimony: use angularity, dignity, reception, and aspects to judge
  whether hiddenness is protective, frustrating, strategic, or temporary.
