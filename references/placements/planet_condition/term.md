# Term

Use this module when a planet is explicitly supplied as having term dignity,
bound dignity, or occupying a named bound.

## Core significations

- Term, also called bound, describes a planet operating within the portion of a
  sign governed by a bound lord.
- The planet has a local set of rules, permissions, constraints, and practical
  management conditions.
- Term dignity is not moral endorsement. It shows local jurisdiction and
  operational leverage within a narrower range than domicile or exaltation.

## Interpretive effect

- Emphasize management, procedure, limits, contracts, and the planet's ability
  to get something done through a defined channel.
- If the planet is in its own bound, it has a modest but concrete form of local
  authority.
- If another planet rules the bound, that bound lord can describe who or what
  manages the planet's expression.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Bound lord condition: assess the named bound lord's house, sect, dignity,
  aspects, and relationship to the planet in question when supplied.
- House placement: describe where the planet works through local rules or
  procedural leverage.
- Reception: term-based reception can provide welcome or practical assistance,
  especially in otherwise difficult configurations.
- Other dignity or debility: term can mitigate but rarely dominates stronger
  dignity, debility, house condition, or aspect testimony.
