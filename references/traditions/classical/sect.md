# Classical Sect

Use this module when chart data supplies sect, day or night chart status, or asks for classical benefic and malefic condition.

## Core rule

Sect describes whether the chart is diurnal or nocturnal and how comfortably planets operate within that light condition. It modifies planetary behavior, especially the benefics and malefics.

## Chart sect

- Day chart: the Sun is above the horizon. The diurnal sect is emphasized.
- Night chart: the Sun is below the horizon. The nocturnal sect is emphasized.
- Do not infer sect unless the source data explicitly gives the Sun's horizon status or chart sect.

## Planets by sect

- Diurnal sect: Sun, Jupiter, and Saturn.
- Nocturnal sect: Moon, Venus, and Mars.
- Mercury is variable and is judged by additional condition when supplied, such as phase, placement, speed, visibility, or association.

## Benefics and malefics

- Jupiter is the greater benefic and tends to operate more constructively in a day chart.
- Venus is the lesser benefic and tends to operate more constructively in a night chart.
- Saturn is the greater malefic and is more moderated in a day chart.
- Mars is the lesser malefic and is more moderated in a night chart.
- The malefic contrary to sect often describes sharper friction, pressure, severing, delay, heat, cold, or conflict.

## Sect lights and solar versus lunar sect

- Each sect has a leading light: the Sun leads the day sect and the Moon leads
  the night sect. Weight the sect light's condition first when judging how the
  chart's overall ease and pressure are distributed.
- A day chart leans on solar testimony (vitality, authority, visibility, life
  direction); a night chart leans on lunar testimony (body, need, caretaking,
  circumstance, habit).
- The benefic of sect (Jupiter by day, Venus by night) is the more reliably
  constructive support; the benefic contrary to sect helps less consistently.

## Triplicity lords and sect-adjacent timing

- In the later Dorothean triplicity system, each elemental triangle has day,
  night, and participating lords, so sect becomes a sequence of ruling planets
  over time rather than a single fit judgment.
- Use triplicity-lord timing only when the supplied data names the system and the
  active lord; otherwise treat sect as a static condition modifier and leave
  time-lord reasoning to `annual_profection` or other supplied timing factors.

## Translation notes

- "Diurnal/nocturnal" and "day/night chart" mean the same thing; prefer
  "day/night chart" for plain-language readings and keep "diurnal/nocturnal"
  for classical mode.
- "Benefic/malefic of sect" and "in sect" mean a planet operating in its own
  light condition; "contrary to sect" or "out of sect" means the opposite. None
  of these rank the planet morally.

## Synthesis guidance

- Use sect to qualify dignity, aspects, house topics, and condition. Sect rarely overrides all other testimony by itself.
- A malefic of the sect can still indicate hardship, but its action may be more contained, purposeful, or manageable.
- A benefic contrary to sect can still help, but its support may be less consistent, less available, or mixed with complications.
- Prioritize the sect light, benefic of sect, and malefic contrary to sect when the reading asks about ease, pressure, timing, or topic condition.

## Required cautions

Do not reduce planets to good or bad. Sect describes fit, moderation, and temperature of expression, not moral value or guaranteed outcomes.

  paraphrased at the tradition level, with Valens as the principal Hellenistic
  witness for practical sect/time-lord use. See
