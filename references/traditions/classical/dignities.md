# Classical Dignities

Use this module when classical or blended interpretation includes supplied dignity, debility, rulership, exaltation, triplicity, bound, face, reception, or peregrine condition.

## Core distinction

- Dignity describes a planet's capacity to act according to its own nature, resources, and authority.
- Debility describes difficulty acting cleanly, consistently, or with access to expected support.
- Dignity is not moral goodness, and debility is not doom. Interpret both as condition, agency, stability, and access to resources.

## Essential dignity

- Domicile: the planet has strong authority and can act from its own terms.
- Exaltation: the planet has elevated visibility, honor, or emphasis, though it may act with less rootedness than domicile.
- Triplicity: the planet has support from a compatible elemental environment, often through allies, context, or cooperative resources.
- Bound or term: the planet operates under the rules or management of the bound lord.
- Face or decan: a minor dignity that can describe a planet's ability to maintain a role, image, or local coherence.
- Peregrine: the planet lacks essential dignity and may depend heavily on other testimonies, reception, house condition, or aspect support.

## Debility

- Detriment: the planet acts in a sign opposite its domicile, often needing to function through unfamiliar priorities or external negotiation.
- Fall: the planet acts in a sign opposite its exaltation, often describing lowered confidence, diminished recognition, or awkward expression.
- Debility can be mitigated by sect, angularity, reception, benefic testimony, or being configured to its ruler.
- Debility can be intensified by contrary sect, cadency, poor reception, maltreatment, combustion, or isolation from supportive testimony.

## Reception

- Reception shows whether a planet receives support from the ruler of the sign, exaltation, triplicity, bound, or face it occupies.
- Strong reception can make difficult aspects more workable by showing access, welcome, or negotiated cooperation.
- Lack of reception can make otherwise strong testimony feel less coordinated or less able to deliver constructively.
- Mutual reception links two planets' topics and can show exchange, dependency, alliance, or mixed obligations.

## Reception mechanics and mutual reception

- A planet is received when the ruler of the sign (or of its exaltation,
  triplicity, bound, or face) it occupies attends to it, especially by aspect.
  Reception shows welcome, access, and negotiated cooperation.
- Strong reception can make a difficult aspect more workable; lack of reception
  in a hard configuration intensifies friction.
- Mutual reception: two planets occupy each other's dignities (for example, the
  Moon in Capricorn while Saturn is in Cancer). It links their topics and can
  show exchange, alliance, dependency, or shared obligation. Read which planet has
  the stronger overall condition, because that planet tends to set the tone of
  the exchange.
- In horary, mutual reception between the querent's and quesited's
  significators can perfect a matter that an applying aspect alone would not
  (Lilly). Apply that only when the reading type is horary and the question
  concerns whether a matter perfects; do not import horary perfection into natal
  readings.
- Reception modifies the *delivery* of a planet's testimony, not its existence.
  A received malefic still signifies its topics; reception changes how
  constructively those topics can be expressed.

## Translation notes

- "Domicile," "rulership," and "own sign" are interchangeable here; the
  classical term is the "house of the planet," which predates the modern
  house-and-ruler usage.
- "Essential" dignity (by sign) is distinct from "accidental" strength (by
  house, motion, visibility). Keep the two layers separate so a
  debilitated-but-angular planet is read correctly.

## Synthesis guidance

- Read dignity together with house placement, sect, angularity, and aspects. A dignified planet in a hidden or cadent house may have resources that are less visible or slower to express.
- Separate a planet's ability to act from whether the topics it signifies are pleasant, easy, public, or personally satisfying.
- When dignity tables or exact minor dignities are not supplied, do not derive them. State that the dignity judgment is limited to the provided data.

## Required cautions

Avoid ranking a life or person by dignities. Use dignity language to describe condition, capacity, support, and friction within the supplied chart factors.
