# Bonification and Maltreatment

Use this module when supplied chart data includes benefic or malefic aspects, enclosure, overcoming, reception, combustion, rays, or explicit bonification and maltreatment terms.

## Core distinction

- Bonification describes conditions that improve, protect, stabilize, or make a planet's significations more constructive.
- Maltreatment describes conditions that pressure, obstruct, inflame, isolate, or destabilize a planet's significations.
- These are modifiers of condition and delivery, not absolute promises of good or bad events.

## Bonification indicators

- Configuration to a benefic, especially by whole-sign or close aspect when supplied.
- Benefic enclosure, where a planet is bounded or supported by benefic testimony.
- Reception from a benefic or from the planet's ruler in a constructive configuration.
- Being overcome by a benefic, when the tradition and aspect doctrine in use support that judgment.
- Support from the benefic of sect, especially when the benefic is itself in good condition.

## Maltreatment indicators

- Configuration to a malefic, especially close hard aspects when supplied.
- Malefic enclosure, where a planet is hemmed in by malefic testimony.
- Being overcome by a malefic, especially the malefic contrary to sect.
- Lack of reception in a harsh configuration.
- Combustion, severe invisibility, or other destructive solar condition when explicitly supplied.
- Testimony from a malefic in poor condition, contrary to sect, or strongly placed to act.

## Synthesis guidance

- Assess the planet being modified, the modifying planet, sect, reception, dignity, house topics, and aspect closeness when provided.
- Bonification can mitigate difficulty without erasing the underlying problem.
- Maltreatment can complicate strong placements without cancelling all capacity or agency.
- Mixed testimony should be named as mixed: support may arrive with pressure, and pressure may force discipline, separation, or realism.

## Required cautions

Avoid fear-based language. Describe maltreatment as strain, exposure, blockage, volatility, or difficulty in the relevant topics, while preserving uncertainty and possible mitigation.
