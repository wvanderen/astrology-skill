# Archetypal Language

Use this module when modern or blended interpretation calls for mythic, symbolic, poetic, or archetypal language.

## Core significations

- Archetypal language treats planets, signs, houses, and aspects as living symbolic patterns that can describe recurring stories, images, roles, and tensions.
- Use archetypes to deepen interpretation, not to obscure concrete chart testimony or the user's actual question.
- Keep symbolic language grounded enough that the user can recognize choices, behaviors, circumstances, or felt experience.

## Constructive expression

- Use metaphor to clarify a pattern when literal language would flatten the symbolism.
- Name multiple possible expressions of an archetype so the reading does not trap the user in one role.
- Let archetypal language open reflection, agency, and nuance.

## Shadow expression

- Avoid grandiose claims, spiritual certainty, or mythic language that makes ordinary life sound fated or inflated.
- Do not use archetypes to bypass practical concerns, ethical limits, or missing chart data.
- Avoid presenting symbols as universal in a way that ignores culture, context, or the user's stated experience.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Tone: use more archetypal language for poetic or psychological readings, and less for practical or technical readings.
- Reading type: keep horary, electional, timing, and concrete question work especially clear and operational.
- Chart structure: anchor each image in a supplied planet, house, aspect, rulership, or repeated theme.
- Tension: when factors conflict, describe the archetypal dialogue rather than forcing one symbol to dominate.
- Scope: label symbolic possibilities as interpretive images, not confirmed facts about the user's life.

## Source notes

- Archetypal framing is a contemporary synthesis choice rather than a quote
  from a single source. Where it draws on modern psychological and symbolic
  vocabulary, it paraphrases the public-domain modern corpus catalogued in
  `references/modern_language_notes.md` (Alan Leo and adjacent modern-popular
  witnesses) into the skill's non-deterministic, agency-preserving ethics
  style. Avoid Theosophical, mythically deterministic, or universalizing
  phrasing from those sources.
