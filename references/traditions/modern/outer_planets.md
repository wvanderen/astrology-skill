# Outer Planets

Use this module when Uranus, Neptune, or Pluto are supplied and modern or blended interpretation includes them. Give them strongest weight when they are angular, closely configured to personal planets or angles, tied to relevant house rulers, or central to the user's question.

## Core significations

- Uranus describes disruption, liberation, awakening, individuation, instability, experimentation, and the need to break stale patterns.
- Neptune describes imagination, porousness, longing, spiritual or artistic sensitivity, idealization, confusion, compassion, and dissolution of rigid boundaries.
- Pluto describes intensity, compulsion, deep change, survival patterns, power dynamics, exposure, loss of control, and regeneration.

## Constructive expression

- Uranus can support innovation, honesty, independence, and new room for aliveness.
- Neptune can support empathy, symbolic imagination, devotion, forgiveness, and creative or spiritual receptivity.
- Pluto can support depth work, resilience, truth-telling, recovery of agency, and transformation after confronting what has been avoided.

## Shadow expression

- Uranus can describe volatility, alienation, reflexive rebellion, or disruption without integration.
- Neptune can describe projection, escapism, vagueness, rescuing fantasies, or difficulty distinguishing inspiration from avoidance.
- Pluto can describe obsession, control struggles, crisis orientation, or interpreting every pressure as total transformation.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Angularity: angular outer planets are more visible and personally emphasized than cadent or weakly connected ones.
- Personal planet contact: close aspects to the Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, or chart angles personalize the symbolism.
- House placement: read the outer planet through the topics of its house rather than as a generalized life theme.
- Rulership links: if an outer planet is tied to the ruler of a relevant house, use it as a modifier of that topic.
- Classical or blended mode: do not let outer planets replace sect, dignity, rulership, house condition, or traditional significators.

## Source notes

- Outer-planet significations and the constructive/shadow framing here are a
  contemporary synthesis of twentieth-century modern astrology rather than a
  quote from a single source. The public-domain modern corpus catalogued in
  `references/modern_language_notes.md` (Alan Leo, Raphael/Cross, Sepharial)
  supplies adjacent psychological and character language; outer-planet
  significations themselves postdate those authors and are paraphrased at a
  high level only. Do not treat this module as a quote of any modern
  translator, commentator, or living teacher.
