# Psychological Framing

Use this module when modern or blended interpretation emphasizes inner experience, developmental themes, coping strategies, self-awareness, or integration.

## Core significations

- Psychological framing describes how chart factors may be experienced internally: motivation, attachment to familiar patterns, emotional defenses, growth edges, and ways of making meaning.
- Treat placements and aspects as symbolic maps of tendencies and tensions, not as diagnoses, fixed traits, or explanations that override lived context.
- Translate difficult symbolism into workable language: pressure, sensitivity, avoidance, overcompensation, adaptation, repair, and integration.

## Constructive expression

- Name capacities that can be practiced, such as reflection, emotional regulation, boundary-setting, creative expression, honest communication, or sustained attention.
- Frame growth as increased choice around a pattern rather than the elimination of the pattern.
- Let the user's explicit question determine which psychological themes matter most.

## Shadow expression

- Avoid reducing the whole reading to personality keywords or therapeutic language detached from chart structure.
- Do not diagnose mental health conditions, trauma histories, attachment styles, or personality disorders from astrology.
- Do not present hardship as spiritually necessary, deserved, or chosen by the soul.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: connect inner patterns to the life topics named by the house.
- Aspects: describe the felt tension, reinforcement, or dialogue between planetary functions.
- Ruler condition: ground psychological themes in the planet's available support, constraints, and context.
- Repetition: give more weight to themes repeated by multiple supplied factors.
- User question: prioritize psychological framing only where it clarifies the question being asked.
