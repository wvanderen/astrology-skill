# Classical Doctrine Notes

Distilled classical doctrine for enriching interpretation modules in Phases 5, 6,
and 7. This is a **working research extraction**, not a retrieved interpretation
module: read it during module authoring, then paraphrase the doctrine into the
target module. Do not load this file during a normal reading pass.

## Purpose and scope

- Capture practical interpretive mechanics from the vetted classical source spine
  (`references/source_canon_and_rights_ledger.md`) so that planet, sign, house,
  aspect, condition, rulership, and reception modules can be deepened without
  each task re-doing the source pass.
- Emphasize doctrine that fits the retrieval-first, non-calculating skill
  contract in `SKILL.md`: judgment of *condition, capacity, support, and
  friction* from supplied chart factors, never calculation, rectification, or
  derivation of missing data.
- Route each doctrine point back to a source family and a book/chapter or page
  range so later enrichment can be audited and corrected.

This document does not supersede the existing `traditions/classical/` modules
(`dignities`, `sect`, `bonification_maltreatment`). It supplies the underlying
source material and terminology those modules summarize.

## How to use

- Before enriching a module, read the matching section here, then the
  `reference_gap_matrix.md` row for that category, then the rights-ledger
  extraction rules for the source family.
- Carry only what the module needs. Do not turn a compact module into a
  monograph by importing an entire doctrine section.
- Preserve the provenance tags. When doctrine is paraphrased into a module, the
  module may keep a one-line `## Source notes` pointer rather than repeating the
  full citation.
- Respect the "do not derive missing data" rule from `SKILL.md` and the
  `interpretive_principles` / `ethics_and_scope` anchors.

## Interpretive cautions (read first)

These apply to every section and should be respected when doctrine is moved
into modules.

- Classical sources are often event-predictive, fatalistic, medicalized,
  status-conscious, gendered, or moralizing. Import the *mechanism of
  judgment*, then adapt the tone to the skill's non-deterministic ethics
  (`ethics_and_scope.md`).
- Dignity, sect, and reception describe **condition and capacity**, not moral
  worth or guaranteed outcomes. A debilitated planet still signifies; a
  dignified one can still signify difficulty `[Synth]`.
- Ptolemy is systematic and selective and is **not** a complete record of
  Hellenistic working practice `[P]`. Valens and Firmicus preserve practical
  doctrine Ptolemy omits or simplifies `[V]`, `[F]`.
- Do not flatten tradition layers. Ptolemy's two-lord triplicity system
  (`[P I.18]`) is not the later three-lord Dorothean system; Lilly's horary
  reception (`[L]`) is not identical to medieval `receptio` `[M]`. Keep the
  terminology distinct in modules and name the source family.
- Never calculate or derive sect, houses, dignities, lots, orbs, applying/
  separating status, or timing factors from partial data. Interpret only what
  the supplied chart data states (`SKILL.md` input contract).

## Planets

### Planetary natures (hot / cold / moist / dry)

- Sun: heating and (moderately) drying `[P I.4]`.
- Moon: humidifying (moistening), with secondary heating from borrowed solar
  light `[P I.4]`.
- Saturn: cooling and (moderately) drying, being farthest from solar heat and
  terrestrial moisture `[P I.4]`.
- Mars: drying and burning (fiery), being near the Sun's sphere `[P I.4]`.
- Jupiter: temperate, heating and humidifying, situated between Saturn's cold
  and Mars's heat; fertile `[P I.4]`.
- Venus: temperate, humidifying and (moderately) heating; fertile `[P I.4]`.
- Mercury: variable — drying and moistening alike, taking on the nature of its
  companions and condition `[P I.4]`, `[P I.5]`.
- `[Synth]` These natures are the classical basis for benefic/malefic and sect
  judgments; in modules, use them to explain *why* a planet tends a certain way
  in a given condition rather than as standalone character claims.

### Benefics and malefics

- Jupiter and Venus (with the Moon) are benefic because they abound in the
  fertile hot/moist pair `[P I.5]`.
- Saturn (excess cold) and Mars (excess dryness) produce effects of the
  opposite nature `[P I.5]`.
- Sun and Mercury have both powers and join whichever planet they are
  associated with `[P I.5]`.
- `[Synth]` "Benefic" and "malefic" name a planet's baseline tendency under
  condition, not a fixed moral value — already stated in `sect.md`; preserve
  that framing.

### Masculine / feminine and diurnal / nocturnal (sect membership)

- Feminine by greater moisture: Moon and Venus `[P I.6]`.
- Masculine: Sun, Saturn, Jupiter, Mars `[P I.6]`.
- Mercury common to both genders `[P I.6]`.
- Nocturnal: Moon and Venus; Diurnal: Sun and Jupiter; Mercury common
  (diurnal as morning star, nocturnal as evening star) `[P I.7]`.
- The two malefics are assigned to sects on the principle of *opposite* nature,
  not similar: Saturn (cold) joins the diurnal/day sect, Mars (dry/hot) joins
  the nocturnal/night sect, so that each malefic is moderated by the sect's
  temperament `[P I.7]`. This is the classical root of the sect-reversal rule
  already summarized in `sect.md`.
- Masculine/feminine can also vary by solar phase: morning stars preceding the
  Sun become more masculine; evening stars following it more feminine `[P I.6]`,
  `[P I.8]`.

### Oriental / occidental and solar-phase power

- Planets are most powerful when oriental (rising before the Sun) and adding to
  proper motion; weaker when occidental and diminishing in speed `[P I.24]`.
- By phase, oriental planets run: moist (rising to first station), hot (first
  station to evening rising), dry (evening rising to second station), cold
  (second station to setting) `[P I.8]`.
- The Moon's phases modify her active quality similarly: waxing-to-first-quarter
  moist, first-quarter-to-full hot, full-to-last-quarter dry, last-quarter-to-
  dark cold `[P I.8]`.
- `[Synth]` Use oriental/occidental and lunar phase only when the supplied data
  states them; they are classic condition modifiers for solar-phase-sensitive
  readings (e.g., Venus or Mercury as morning/evening star).

## Signs

Ptolemy Book I (§§10-16) is the strongest public-domain anchor for sign-powers
doctrine, which the gap matrix confirms is absent from the current sign modules.

### Solstitial, equinoctial, solid, bicorporeal

- Solstitial: Cancer and Capricorn — the Sun reverses latitudinal progress at
  their beginnings `[P I.11]`.
- Equinoctial: Aries and Libra — day and night are equal at their beginnings
  `[P I.11]`.
- Solid (fixed): Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius — they follow the
  solstitial/equinoctial signs and stabilize the season's temperament `[P I.11]`.
- Bicorporeal (double-bodied): Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces — they
  complete the season and transition toward the next `[P I.11]`.
- `[Synth]` These map onto but are not identical to the modern
  cardinal/fixed/mutable modality already in the sign modules; name the
  classical category distinctly when enriching.

### Masculine and feminine signs

- Six masculine/diurnal and six feminine/nocturnal signs, in alternating order
  beginning with Aries (masculine) `[P I.12]`.
- Alternate tradition begins masculine signs from the horoscope (Ascendant)
  rather than from Aries; Ptolemy reports both `[P I.12]`.
- `[Synth]` This is the classical root of the odd-sign/even-sign gender
  assignment; keep it distinct from planetary gender (`[P I.6]`).

### Commanding and obeying signs

- Signs at equal distance from the same equinoctial sign, because they ascend in
  equal times and lie on equal parallels `[P I.14]`.
- Those in the summer hemisphere are "commanding"; those in the winter
  hemisphere "obeying" (longer vs. shorter days) `[P I.14]`.
- Pairs: Aries commands Libra-equivalent axis; in practice the commanding sign
  of each equinoctial pair is the one nearer the summer solstice `[P I.14]`.

### Signs that behold each other / of equal power; disjunct signs

- Signs in aspect (opposition, trine, quartile/square, sextile) are "familiar"
  and configure each other `[P I.13]`, `[P I.15]`.
- Opposition = two right angles, six signs, 180°; trine = 120°; quartile =
  90°; sextile = 60° `[P I.13]`.
- Signs of equal power are those equidistant from the same tropical/equinoctial
  sign `[P I.15]`.
- Disjunct (inconjunct/aversion) signs share none of these aspect relations and
  are unfamilial to one another `[P I.16]`.
- `[Synth]` "Aversion" is the practical doctrine behind the modern inconjunct
  and the classical idea that a cadent or aversed house has weaker access to the
  Ascendant — useful for house and aspect modules.

### Humoral / seasonal temperament of signs

- Each sign's general temperament is analogous to the season in which it falls,
  with further qualities from kinship to Sun, Moon, and planets `[P I.11]`.
- `[Synth]` Use sparingly in modules; the element + modality already in the
  sign modules covers most retrieval needs. Add humoral notes only where a
  classical-condition reading explicitly benefits.

## Houses

Ptolemy gives **limited** house doctrine; practical house material comes from
Valens, Firmicus, Lilly, and Bonatti. Treat the topic lists below as
source-family doctrine, paraphrased.

### Topic assignments (standard across the tradition)

- 1st: life, body, head, the native `[V]`, `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 2nd: substance, livelihood, movable goods `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 3rd: siblings, short journeys, neighbors `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 4th: parents (especially father in some traditions; mother in Lilly's later
  usage), land, foundations, hidden treasures `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 5th: children, embassy, messengers, pleasure `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 6th: illness, servants, small animals `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 7th: marriage, partnership, open enemies, commerce `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 8th: death, inheritance, the partner's substance `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 9th: long journeys, religion, dreams, divination, science `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 10th: profession, honor, authority, mothers (in some traditions) `[F]`,
  `[L]`.
- 11th: friends, hopes, the Good Daemon `[F]`, `[L]`.
- 12th: enemies, sorrow, imprisonment, large animals `[F]`, `[L]`.
- `[Synth]` These are *tradition-standard topic lists*, not assertions about a
  specific chart. Map them to the supplied house system only when the data
  provides houses.

### Angular, succedent, cadent; good and bad houses

- Angular (1, 4, 7, 10): strongest for action and visibility; succedent (2, 5,
  8, 11): moderate; cadent (3, 6, 9, 12): weakest `[F]`, `[L]`, `[M]`.
- "Good" and "bad" houses were judged partly by aspect to the Ascendant:
  houses configured to the Ascendant by a classical aspect (1, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9,
  10, 11) were considered more effective; aversed houses (2, 6, 12, and
  depending on system 8) weaker `[V]`, `[M]`.
- `[Synth]` Already partially present in the house modules; the classical
  aspect-to-Ascendant rationale is the missing piece named in the gap matrix.

### Planetary joys

- A classical assignment of each of the seven traditional planets to a house
  where it is said to "rejoice": Mercury in the 1st, Moon in the 3rd, Venus in
  the 5th, Mars in the 6th, Jupiter in the 11th, Saturn in the 12th, with the
  Sun in the 9th and 10th by variant traditions `[V]`, `[M]`.
- `[Synth]` Use as a *secondary* testimonial layer when the supplied data
  matches a joy; do not derive outcomes from joys alone.

### Derived / turned houses

- A house counted from another house (e.g., the 7th from the 7th = the 1st, for
  the partner's partner; the 2nd from the 7th = the 8th, for the partner's
  money) is the basis of horary and synastry-adjacent turned-house judgment
  `[L]`, `[M]`.
- `[Synth]` Use turned houses only when the reading type (`horary`,
  `synastry`, or an explicit relational question) and the supplied houses
  support it. State the derivation explicitly in the reading.

## Aspects

### Aspect geometry and configuring signs

- Four aspect relations between signs: opposition (180°), trine (120°),
  quartile/square (90°), sextile (60°) `[P I.13]`.
- Only signs sharing one of these relations are "familiar" / configured; all
  other sign pairs are disjunct `[P I.13]`, `[P I.16]`.
- Aspects can be judged *by body* (exact degree) or *by sign* (whole-sign);
  Ptolemy treats sign-based familiarity as primary for sign doctrine `[P I.13]`,
  `[P I.24]`.

### Dexter and sinister; hurling rays; witnessing

- A dexter (right-hand) aspect is thrown backward through the sign order (e.g.,
  from Aries to Aquarius); a sinister (left-hand) aspect is thrown forward
  through the sign order (e.g., from Aries to Gemini) `[M]`. Ptolemy describes
  the directional geometry without the later Latin labels `[P I.13]`.
- "Witnessing" (or testimony) is a benefic's constructive configuration to a
  planet; "hurling rays" is a planet casting an aspect across intervening signs
  `[V]`, `[M]`. These underlie the bonification/maltreatment module.
- `[Synth]` Use dexter/sinister language only in `classical` or `blended`
  readings; keep it tied to the supplied aspect data and do not over-claim
  strength differences.

### Application and separation

- Planets that *precede* (are in earlier degree/sign) are said to apply to
  those that follow; those that follow separate from those that precede, when
  the interval is not great `[P I.24]`.
- Application/separation can occur by bodily conjunction or by aspect; for
  bodily applications, latitude matters (same side of the ecliptic preferred)
  `[P I.24]`.
- Lilly classifies horary application in three ways: (1) a faster planet direct
  applying to a slower direct one; (2) both retrograde (prohibition/frustration
  risk); (3) one direct, one retrograde, producing a mutually applying or a
  breaking-off situation `[L Book II, Application; ~p.123-125]`.
- `[Synth]` Applying aspects carry ongoing/future force; separating aspects
  carry completed/past force. State applying/separating status only when the
  supplied data provides it (see `aspect_precision.md`).

## Dignities and debilities

### Domicile ("houses of the planets")

- Cancer and Leo (the northernmost, hottest signs) were assigned to the
  luminaries: Leo (masculine) to the Sun, Cancer (feminine) to the Moon `[P I.17]`.
- The remaining signs were allocated outward from the luminaries in keeping
  with planetary spheres and natures: Saturn took Capricorn and Aquarius (cold,
  wintry, opposite the luminaries); Jupiter Sagittarius and Pisces (trine to
  the luminaries); Mars Aries and Scorpio; Venus Taurus and Libra; Mercury
  Gemini and Virgo `[P I.17]`.
- `[Synth]` This is the classical rationale for the rulership scheme already in
  the rulership modules; cite when enriching rulership with *why* a planet rules
  a sign.

### Exaltations and depressions (falls)

- Sun: exalted in Aries, depressed in Libra `[P I.19]`.
- Moon: exalted in Taurus, depressed in Scorpio `[P I.19]`.
- Jupiter: exalted in Cancer, depressed in Capricorn `[P I.19]`.
- Mercury: exalted in Virgo, depressed in Pisces `[P I.19]` (inferred from the
  pattern; Ptolemy states the rationale for each).
- Venus: exalted in Pisces, depressed in Virgo `[P I.19]`.
- Mars: exalted in Capricorn, depressed in Cancer `[P I.19]`.
- Saturn: exalted in Libra, depressed in Aries `[P I.19]`.
- Exaltations are rationalized by seasonal/latitudinal extremes (e.g., the Sun
  begins to climb northward at the Aries equinox) `[P I.19]`.

### Triplicities (triangles) — Ptolemy's two-lord system

Ptolemy uses a **two-lord** day/night triplicity system, distinct from the
later three-lord Dorothean system; keep them separate in modules.

- Aries-Leo-Sagittarius (masculine): Sun by day, Jupiter by night; Mars
  excluded as not of the solar sect `[P I.18]`.
- Taurus-Virgo-Capricorn (feminine): Moon by night, Venus by day `[P I.18]`.
- Gemini-Libra-Aquarius (masculine): Saturn by day, Mercury by night `[P I.18]`.
- Cancer-Scorpio-Pisces (feminine): Mars by house (Scorpio), with the Moon by
  night and Venus by day as co-rulers `[P I.18]`.
- The three-lord (Dorothean) triplicity system with a participating lord is a
  later tradition `[M]`, `[V]`; modules that use triplicity should name which
  system the supplied data implies and not mix them.

### Terms (bounds) and faces (decanates)

- Two term systems circulate: the **Egyptian** (based on houses, triplicities,
  exaltations) and the **Chaldaean** (based on triplicity governance)
  `[P I.20]`, `[P I.21]`. Ptolemy also describes his own monomorphic term
  scheme elsewhere in the tradition.
- "Places and degrees" finer divisions are reported by Ptolemy but judged
  unfounded `[P I.22]`.
- Faces/chariots/decanates are a minor dignity of image and role `[P I.23]`.
- `[Synth]` Do not assert a specific terms scheme or face unless the supplied
  data names one. The skill does not derive minor dignities from partial data
  (`dignities.md`).

### Detriment, fall, and peregrine

- Detriment: a planet in the sign opposite its domicile — the classical basis
  for the detriment assignments already in the modules `[P I.17]` (implied by
  the domicile scheme), `[L]`.
- Fall (depression): a planet in the sign opposite its exaltation `[P I.19]`.
- Peregrine: a planet lacking any essential dignity in its sign `[L]`,
  `[M]`. Treat as dependence on other testimony, not as damage `[Synth]`.

### Accidental strength and debility

- Planets are strongest when angular or approaching midheaven, next strongest
  on the horizon or in a succedent place; cadency weakens `[P I.24]`.
- Oriental/direct/fast motion adds power; occidental/slow/retrograde motion
  diminishes it `[P I.24]`.
- `[L]` Lilly's "Table of the Essential Dignities" (Book I, ~p.104 of the 1659
  edition) and "A Table to examine the Fortitudes and Debilities" give the
  classic scoring framework; paraphrase the *categories* (essential vs.
  accidental) rather than reproducing the table or its numeric weights.

## Sect

Core sect mechanics are summarized in `traditions/classical/sect.md`; the
following supplies the source grounding and terminology.

- A chart is diurnal when the Sun is above the horizon in the chart and
  nocturnal when below — but **do not infer sect** unless the supplied data
  states the Sun's horizon position or the chart sect `[Synth]`.
- Sect membership: diurnal sect = Sun, Jupiter, Saturn; nocturnal sect = Moon,
  Venus, Mars; Mercury variable `[P I.7]`.
- Sect moderation: the malefic in its own sect (Saturn by day, Mars by night)
  operates with more containment and constructive aim; the malefic contrary to
  sect (Saturn by night, Mars by day) operates with sharper friction `[P I.7]`,
  `[Synth]`.
- The benefic of sect (Jupiter by day, Venus by night) is the more reliably
  constructive support; the benefic contrary to sect helps less consistently
  `[Synth]`.
- `[V]` Valens and later Hellenistic practice develop sect into time-lord and
  trigon-lord mechanics; use those only when timing factors are supplied.

## Visibility and solar condition

### Combustion, cazimi, under the beams

Lilly's public-domain English gives the standard orb conventions; cite the
family and the chapter, paraphrase the doctrine, and keep the skill's
"do not derive orbs from missing data" rule.

- **Combustion**: a planet close enough to the Sun to be "burned"; Lilly gives
  the working orb as about 8° (with a 17° moeity convention implied in the
  8°30' half-orb tradition); a combust significator shows fear, overpowering,
  or loss of independent voice `[L Book I/II, Combustion; ~p.115, ~p.123]`.
- **Under the beams**: a planet within roughly 8.5°-17° of the Sun — weakened
  by invisibility but not fully combust; recovering as it elongates toward 17°
  `[L ~p.115, ~p.123]`.
- **Cazimi** ("in the heart of the Sun"): a planet within about 17' (seventeen
  minutes of arc) of the Sun's body; "all Authors doe hold a Planet in Cazimi
  to be fortified thereby" — concentrated, authorized, specially focused
  `[L ~p.115, ~p.123]`. Quote Lilly sparingly and attribute.
- `[Synth]` Distinguish the three conditions when the supplied data states an
  orb or a visibility label; when the data only says "combust" without an orb,
  treat as combustion and do not assert cazimi or under-beams.

### Morning and evening stars; oriental/occidental

- A planet rising before the Sun (morning star, oriental) is more masculine and
  more powerful; one setting after the Sun (evening star, occidental) is more
  feminine and weaker `[P I.6]`, `[P I.8]`, `[P I.24]`.
- `[Synth]` Especially relevant for Mercury and Venus, whose morning/evening
  star status is a meaningful condition modifier when the data provides it.

## Reception

Reception is primarily a medieval and early modern doctrine (Ptolemy does not
use the term); source families are Lilly (public-domain English) and the
medieval Arabic/Latin tradition (paraphrase).

- A planet is **received** when the ruler of the sign (or exaltation,
  triplicity, term, or face) it occupies aspects or attends to it; reception
  shows welcome, access, and negotiated cooperation `[L]`, `[M]`.
- Strong reception can make a difficult aspect more workable; lack of reception
  in a hard configuration intensifies friction `[L]`, `[M]`.
- **Mutual reception**: two planets in each other's dignities; it links their
  topics and can show exchange, alliance, dependency, or mixed obligations
  `[L]`, `[M]`.
- `[L]` Lilly treats mutual reception between the querent's and quesited's
  significators as able to perfect a matter that the applying aspect alone
  would not — paraphrase the *mechanism*, do not import horary perfection rules
  into natal modules without the reading type.
- `[Synth]` Reception modifies the *delivery* of a planet's testimony, not its
  existence. A received malefic still signifies its topics; reception changes
  how constructively those topics can be expressed. This is already in
  `dignities.md`; enrich with concrete mutual-reception examples per the gap
  matrix.

## Bonification and maltreatment

Source grounding for `bonification_maltreatment.md`.

- **Bonification** = constructive configuration, especially from a benefic or
  the benefic of sect; enclosure by benefics; reception from a constructive
  ruler; being overcome by a benefic where the tradition supports that judgment
  `[V]`, `[M]`.
- **Maltreatment** = hard configuration from a malefic (especially the malefic
  contrary to sect); malefic enclosure/hemming; being overcome by a malefic in
  poor condition; combustion or destructive solar condition; lack of reception
  in a harsh configuration `[V]`, `[M]`.
- Mixed testimony is normal: support may arrive with pressure, and pressure may
  force discipline, separation, or realism `[Synth]`.
- `[Synth]` Preserve the skill's non-fear-based tone: describe maltreatment as
  strain, exposure, blockage, or volatility, not doom.

## Lots and timing (only as supported by supplied chart data)

Timing and lot doctrine is **only** used when the supplied chart data provides
the lot, time-lord, profection year, direction, or timing factor explicitly.
The skill does not calculate any of these (`SKILL.md`).

### Lots

- `[V]` Valens gives extensive practical use of the Lot of Fortune and many
  specialized lots (Spirit, Necessity, Eros, Basis, and others) as significator
  placements and topic anchors — paraphrase the *function* (a lot marks a
  topic point that is then read like a house/planet), not the construction
  formulas.
- `[P]` Ptolemy uses the Lot of Fortune as a derived point for material
  fortune, dignity, and action judgments in Book IV.
- `[Synth]` When a lot is supplied, read it with the dignity, house, and aspect
  rules applied to its position and ruler; when no lot is supplied, do not
  invent one.

### Time-lords, profections, directions, length of life

- `[V]` Valens is the principal Hellenistic source for time-lord techniques —
  decennials, distributions, chronocrators, and profection-style annual
  rulers — used to identify active periods and their planetary character.
  Paraphrase the *concept* (a sequence of ruling planets governing periods),
  never the modern translator's tables or worked examples.
- `[P]` Ptolemy treats length of life as a distinct, technically demanding
  inquiry using the prorogator and directing primary motion (`Tetrabiblos`
  III.10); he treats annual returns, ingresses, lunations, and transits as
  activation methods in Book IV.
- `[L]` Lilly gives profections, directions, solar returns, and transits in the
  natal book (Book III).
- `[Synth]` For `annual_profection` and timing-synthesis modules, name the
  active time-lord or profected house/ruler only when the supplied data
  identifies it; otherwise describe timing testimony as conditional.

## Natal topic routing (source grounding for synthesis modules)

Ptolemy Book III-IV gives the classical topic structure that the synthesis
pattern modules already use implicitly; the chapter map is provided here for
citation during enrichment.

- Parents: III.4. Siblings: III.5. Male/female nativity: III.6. Twins: III.7.
  Monsters: III.8. Children not reared: III.9. Length of life: III.10 `[P]`.
- Material fortune: IV.2. Fortune of dignity: IV.3. Quality of action:
