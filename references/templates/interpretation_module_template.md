# Module Title

Use this structure for new interpretation modules. Keep each module compact,
specific, and composable with other retrieved modules.

## Module creation checklist

Before adding a module, confirm:

- Core significations name the essential function, topic, style, condition, or
  relationship covered by this module.
- Classical notes capture relevant traditional factors such as rulership,
  condition, sect, dignity, debility, reception, house strength, or concrete
  topics.
- Modern psychological notes capture lived experience, developmental patterning,
  archetypal symbolism, or integration themes.
- Constructive expression describes how the factor can operate with agency,
  support, skill, or coherence.
- Shadow expression describes likely distortions, stress patterns, excesses, or
  avoidance without fatalistic language.
- Synthesis instructions explain how this module should modify a reading when
  combined with house placement, ruler condition, aspects, sect, dignity,
  debility, and the user's question.
- Every section earns its place. Prefer short bullets over exhaustive prose, and
  avoid repeating material that belongs in a broader planet, sign, house,
  aspect, tradition, or synthesis module.

Sections may be intentionally omitted when they do not apply to the module's
scope, when the relevant information belongs in another more specific module, or
when including the section would make the module less compact and composable.
If omitting a section could surprise a future maintainer, add one short note
under the nearest relevant heading explaining why.
