# {Planet} as House Ruler

Use this structure for planet-led rulership modules named
`references/rulerships/{planet}.md`. Each module describes how a specific
planet carries the topics of any house it rules. For example, use the Mars
module for "Mars ruling the 1st house," then combine it with Mars's sign,
house placement, dignity, sect, aspects, and reception.

## Module creation checklist

Before adding a module, confirm:

- The filename uses the pattern `references/rulerships/{planet}.md`.
- The module is organized by planet first, then by the house the planet rules.
- House rulership is based on the supplied chart's sign ruler or explicitly
  supplied rulership data.
- The module does not assume natural house rulers such as "Mars rules the 1st"
  unless the chart data actually says Mars rules the 1st.
- The module describes the planet's symbolic way of administering a house, not
  a guaranteed event.
- House placement is handled by planet-in-house modules or broader house
  modules, not baked into the rulership meaning.
- Dignity, debility, sect, reception, and aspects modify the ruler's condition
  and delivery rather than replacing the basic house topic.
- The module stays compact enough to combine with planet, sign, house, aspect,
  condition, tradition, and topic-synthesis modules.

## Core formula

- Ruling planet: `{planet}`.
- Ruled house: `{house}`.
- Topics carried: the concrete and symbolic topics of `{house}`.
- Planetary administration: `{planet}` manages those topics through its own
  functions, motives, temperament, and condition.
- Manifesting arena: the ruler's actual house placement and configured planets,
  read from separate placement, house, aspect, and condition modules.

Avoid collapsing rulership into placement. "Mars ruling the 1st" describes how
identity, body, vitality, and self-direction are administered by Mars. "Mars in
the 5th" describes the arena where Mars operates. A full reading needs both
when both are supplied.

## Planetary rulership baseline

Name the planet's core way of governing house topics:

- What the planet seeks, protects, builds, joins, separates, illuminates,
  organizes, questions, expands, or limits.
- What kinds of actors, motives, or situations the planet tends to signify.
- How the planet's temperament changes the tone of the house it rules.
- What the planet tends to make visible, urgent, relational, private, practical,
  reflective, or developmental.

## House-by-house sections

Create one section for each house the planet might rule:

```markdown
## {Planet} ruling the {house} house

Use when {planet} is explicitly supplied as ruler of the {house} house.

### Core topic carried

- Name the house topics being administered by {planet}.
- Distinguish the ruled house topic from the ruler's placement house.
- Note when the house is especially relevant to common reading questions.

### Planetary expression

- Describe how {planet}'s function shapes those house topics.
- Include concrete, relational, psychological, or practical expressions when
  they naturally follow from the planet-house combination.
- Keep the interpretation symbolic and conditional, not event-certain.

### Constructive expression

- Describe skillful ways the person can work with the ruled house through
  {planet}'s strengths.
- Emphasize agency, timing, support, practice, relationship, or discernment.

### Shadow expression

- Describe likely distortions, excesses, omissions, fears, or misuses of the
  planet's function in relation to the ruled house.
- Use non-fatalistic language and preserve room for other chart factors.
```

## Classical notes

- Use traditional domicile rulers by default in classical or blended mode unless
  the input explicitly supplies a different rulership scheme.
- Treat house rulership as accidental and chart-specific: a planet rules a house
  because it rules the sign on that house in the supplied chart.
- For angular houses, note stronger visibility and capacity to act when relevant.
- For succedent houses, note stabilization, consolidation, maintenance, and
  resource continuity when relevant.
- For cadent houses, note mediation, movement, learning, service, hiddenness, or
  diffusion when relevant.
- Do not confuse planetary joys, natural house affinities, or sign-house
  correspondences with the actual ruler of a house.

## Modern psychological notes

- Describe how the person may experience the house topic through the planet's
  lived pattern: desire, attachment, cognition, boundary, confidence, grief,
  belonging, growth, or integration.
- Keep psychological language grounded in supplied chart factors.
- Avoid making house rulership a fixed personality verdict.

## Condition modifiers

Use these modifiers only when the chart data explicitly supplies the condition.
Do not derive missing dignity, sect, reception, or aspects from incomplete data.

### Dignity and debility

- Dignified ruler: the ruled house topics have more coherent access to the
  planet's resources, authority, and preferred style of operation.
- Debilitated ruler: the ruled house topics may require adaptation, support,
  practice, negotiation, or alternate channels.
- Peregrine or mixed dignity: emphasize dependence on house placement, aspects,
  reception, sect, and repeated testimony.
- Reception: show whether another planet or house lord welcomes, supports,
  redirects, or complicates the ruler's work.

### Sect

- Planet of the sect: expression may be more moderated, visible, or aligned with
  the chart's operating climate.
- Planet contrary to sect: expression may be less consistent, more effortful, or
  sharper in tone, especially for benefics and malefics.
- Mercury: judge sect relevance only from supplied visibility, phase, speed, or
  association data.

### Aspects

- Supportive aspects: describe channels of cooperation, opportunity, testimony,
  or easier integration for the ruled house topics.
- Hard aspects: describe pressure, conflict, urgency, friction, or necessary
  adjustment without making the outcome deterministic.
- Benefic testimony: note help, protection, sweetness, access, or mediation when
  supported by condition.
- Malefic testimony: note constraint, severing, heat, delay, pressure, or
  discipline, then qualify by sect, dignity, reception, and aspect condition.
- Applying, separating, or exact aspects should modify timing, intensity, or
  immediacy only when those details are supplied.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- The ruled house and its topics.
- The ruling planet's core function and temperament.
- The ruler's sign, dignity, and reception.
- The ruler's house placement, angularity, and visibility.
- Sect status and whether benefics or malefics are moderated or intensified.
- Aspects to the ruler, especially from planets relevant to the user's question.
- Planets in the ruled house and planets in the ruler's placement house.
- Whether the user's question emphasizes the ruled house, the ruler's placement,
  or the relationship between them.
- Repeated testimony from planet, sign, house, aspect, condition, tradition, and
  synthesis-pattern modules.
