# Ruler of {source_house} in {target_house}

Use this structure for house-ruler placement modules named
`ruler_of_{source_house}_in_{target_house}`. These modules describe how the
topics of one house are carried by its ruler into another house's arena.

## Module creation checklist

Before adding a module, confirm:

- The filename uses the pattern
  `references/placements/planet_as_house_ruler/ruler_of_{source_house}_in_{target_house}.md`.
- `{source_house}` and `{target_house}` use ordinal house labels such as `1st`
  through `12th`.
- The source house is treated as the topic being carried, not as the place where
  events automatically happen.
- The target house is treated as the arena, context, or outlet where the source
  house topics manifest.
- The module can be applied to any planet that rules the source house; planet,
  sign, dignity, sect, and aspect details should modify the module rather than
  be baked into the base meaning.
- Dignity, debility, sect, and aspect modifiers describe condition and delivery,
  not guaranteed outcomes.
- The module stays compact and composable with house, planet, sign, aspect,
  condition, tradition, and reading-type modules.

## Core formula

- Topic carried: `{source_house}` topics.
- Arena of manifestation: `{target_house}` topics.
- Interpretive bridge: The ruler of the `{source_house}` directs, relocates, or
  expresses `{source_house}` matters through `{target_house}` circumstances.
- Avoid collapsing the two houses. The source house says what is being carried;
  the target house says where, through whom, or by what activity it becomes
  active.

## Source house topics

- Name the essential topics, people, obligations, desires, or questions belonging
  to the `{source_house}`.
- Identify which of those topics are most relevant to common reading questions.
- Note any common overextensions or misreadings of the source house.

## Target house arena

- Name the practical and symbolic arena of the `{target_house}`.
- Describe how the source-house topics may become visible, useful, complicated,
  or developed through that arena.
- Include a short caution against treating the target house as the only possible
  outcome.

## Classical notes

- Describe the concrete house-topic relationship without assuming a specific
  planet, sign, dignity, or aspect.
- Consider angularity, succedency, or cadency of the target house when relevant.
- Note whether the target house supports visibility, stability, effort, privacy,
  dependence on others, or withdrawal.
- If the source and target houses are configured by whole-sign aspect, note the
  ease or friction of that house-to-house relationship when relevant.

## Modern psychological notes

- Describe the lived pattern of carrying one life topic into another sphere.
- Include developmental, relational, identity, or integration themes when they
  naturally follow from the source-target relationship.
- Keep psychological language grounded in the supplied chart factors and avoid
  treating the placement as a fixed personality verdict.

## Constructive expression

- Describe how the person can work skillfully with the source-house topic in the
  target-house arena.
- Emphasize agency, support, timing, negotiation, or practical channels.

## Shadow expression

- Describe likely distortions, avoidance patterns, displacement, excess, or
  confusion between the two houses.
- Use non-fatalistic language and preserve room for other chart factors to
  modify the expression.

## Condition modifiers

Use these modifiers only when the chart data explicitly supplies the condition.
Do not derive missing dignity, sect, reception, or aspects from incomplete data.

### Dignity and debility

- Dignified ruler: the source-house topic has more coherent resources,
  authority, or capacity to operate through the target house.
- Debilitated ruler: the source-house topic may need adaptation, support,
  negotiation, or practice to operate cleanly in the target house.
- Peregrine or mixed dignity: emphasize dependence on house placement, aspects,
  reception, sect, and repeated testimonies.
- Reception: show whether another planet or house lord welcomes, supports,
  redirects, or complicates the ruler's work.

### Sect

- Ruler of the sect: expression may be more moderated, visible, or aligned with
  the chart's operating climate.
- Ruler contrary to sect: expression may be less consistent, more effortful, or
  sharper in tone, especially when the ruler is a benefic or malefic.
- Mercury: judge sect relevance only from supplied visibility, phase, speed, or
  association data.

### Aspects

- Supportive aspects: describe channels of cooperation, opportunity, testimony,
  or easier integration between the source-house topic and other factors.
- Hard aspects: describe pressure, conflict, urgency, friction, or necessary
  adjustment without making the outcome deterministic.
- Aspecting benefics: note help, protection, sweetness, access, or mediation
  when supported by condition.
- Aspecting malefics: note constraint, severing, heat, delay, pressure, or
  discipline, then qualify by sect, dignity, reception, and aspect condition.
- Applying, separating, or exact aspects should modify timing, intensity, or
  immediacy only when those details are supplied.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- The planet ruling the source house and its core function.
- The sign and dignity of the ruler.
- The ruler's target house, angularity, and visibility.
- Sect status and whether benefics or malefics are moderated or intensified.
- Aspects to the ruler, especially from planets relevant to the user's question.
- Reception between the ruler, its dispositor, planets in the source house, and
  planets in the target house.
- Whether the user's question emphasizes the source-house topic, the target-house
  arena, or the relationship between them.
- Repeated testimonies from planet-in-house, house, aspect, condition, tradition,
  and synthesis-pattern modules.
