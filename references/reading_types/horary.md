# Horary Readings

Horary readings interpret a chart cast for the moment a clear, sincere question
is received and understood. They are best suited to focused questions with a
defined matter, relevant parties, and reliable time and location data. Horary
does not replace practical judgment, consent, medical, legal, financial, or
safety advice, and it should not be used to over-answer vague, speculative, or
poorly timed questions.

## Baseline factors

- The exact question, the querent's role, the relevant other party or object,
  and the life area being asked about.
- Chart time, timezone, location, house system, and whether the chart is cast
  for the astrologer's receipt of the question or another explicitly stated
  method.
- Querent significator: usually the Ascendant ruler, with the Moon as
  co-significator unless the chosen tradition or chart context assigns it
  differently.
- Quesited significator: the ruler of the house that best describes the matter,
  with planets in that house and natural significators used as supporting
  testimony.
- Moon condition: sign, dignity or debility when used, house, aspects,
  void-of-course status, speed, light, combustion or proximity to the Sun, and
  whether its next aspect describes movement in the matter.
- House relevance: the house selected for the question, its ruler, planets in
  the house, turned houses when appropriate, and whether the chosen house
  actually matches the user's stated concern.
- Applying and separating aspects between significators, especially perfection
  by conjunction, sextile, square, trine, or opposition when supported by
  reception and chart context.
- Reception between significators, including dignity-based willingness,
  support, mismatch, indifference, or strain.
- Prohibition, frustration, collection of light, translation of light, and
  other perfection modifiers when the tradition and supplied data support them.
- Radicality cautions: early or late Ascendant, Saturn in the 7th, Moon
  void-of-course, unclear intent, repeated questions, missing data, or a chart
  that does not appear to describe the matter.

## Question clarity and scope

- Begin by restating the question in practical terms. A horary chart can only
  answer the question it was asked, not every adjacent anxiety or hidden issue.
- If the question combines several matters, separate them and avoid forcing one
  chart to answer all of them. Prioritize the question that was most clearly
  formed at the chart moment.
- If the question is vague, hypothetical, asked on behalf of someone without a
  clear relationship to the querent, or mainly seeks private knowledge about a
  third party, narrow the scope before judging.
- Do not use horary to bypass consent, boundaries, privacy, or professional
  expertise. For third-party questions, focus on the querent's choices,
  observable circumstances, and ethical limits.
- If the question, time, timezone, or location data is unclear, do not produce a
  firm yes/no answer. State the missing data and give only provisional symbolic
  observations that do not depend on the uncertain factor.

## Significators and houses

- Assign the querent to the 1st house and its ruler unless the question clearly
  requires a different role-based assignment.
- Assign the quesited by topic: the 7th for partners, opponents, contracts, and
  one-to-one others; the 10th for career, authority, public outcomes, and
  reputation; the 4th for home, land, endings, and roots; the 2nd for the
  querent's money or possessions; the 8th for another person's money, debt,
  taxes, or shared entanglement; the 6th for employees, illness routines, and
  small animals; the 9th for travel, law, study, and divination; the 11th for
  friends, groups, and hopes.
- Use turned houses when the matter belongs to another party, but name the
  turning logic so the reading remains transparent.
- Planets in the relevant house can describe people, conditions, resources, or
  obstacles in the matter. They should support or modify the ruler testimony,
  not replace it automatically.
- Natural significators can add context, such as Venus for affection, Mercury
  for messages or documents, Mars for conflict, Jupiter for support or growth,
  Saturn for delay or constraint, and the Moon for motion and changing
  circumstances.

## Moon condition

- Treat the Moon as a primary descriptor of flow, timing, emotional weather,
  and how the matter develops.
- The Moon's next applying aspect often shows the next meaningful development,
  especially when it connects to a significator or relevant house ruler.
- A void-of-course Moon can describe stalled movement, lack of immediate
  change, or a matter that may not develop as expected. It should not be framed
  as doom or permanent failure.
- A debilitated, combust, slow, or cadent Moon can show fatigue, reduced
  visibility, delay, uncertainty, or a need for patience and care.
- A well-supported Moon can show coherence, responsiveness, help, or easier
  movement, especially when it applies to a relevant significator with
  reception.

## Perfection and reception

- Applying aspects between the querent and quesited significators are central
  testimony. Separating aspects often describe what has already happened,
  recently passed, or is losing force.
- Favor clear perfection when significators apply by major aspect within the
  relevant bounds of the chart and without stronger intervening testimony.
- Squares and oppositions can still perfect, but they may describe effort,
  negotiation, cost, confrontation, delay, or a result that requires adjustment.
  Avoid treating difficult aspects as automatic denial.
- Reception describes the quality of willingness, fit, access, or support
  between significators. Strong reception can help difficult aspects work more
  constructively; poor reception can weaken an otherwise easy path.
- If significators do not aspect, look for translation or collection of light,
  relevant house testimony, and the Moon's motion before concluding that the
  matter cannot connect.
- Repetition increases confidence: a relevant house ruler, Moon aspect,
  reception pattern, and angular testimony all pointing to the same answer
  outweigh an isolated indication.

## Radicality and caution

- Radicality cautions are reasons to slow down and qualify the reading, not
  reasons to frighten the user or discard the chart automatically.
- An early Ascendant may suggest the matter is still forming, the user lacks
  enough information, or it is too soon to judge firmly.
- A late Ascendant may suggest the matter is already far developed, the answer
  may be known, or the chart is describing a situation with little room to
  change.
- Saturn in the 7th can caution the astrologer about judgment, method, or
  clarity, especially if the question or data is confused.
- A chart that does not describe the people, topic, or circumstances in a
  recognizable way should be treated cautiously. State the mismatch and avoid
  forcing a verdict.
- Repeatedly asking the same question can muddy interpretation. Prefer the
  first clear chart unless materially new information or a new situation has
  emerged.

## Avoiding fear-based answers

- Frame difficult testimony as delay, constraint, mismatch, repair work,
  reduced access, unclear timing, or a need for support rather than punishment,
  danger, fate, or inevitable loss.
- Do not predict death, serious illness, accidents, legal outcomes, pregnancy,
  financial ruin, relationship endings, or other high-stakes outcomes as
  horary-derived certainty.
- If the chart suggests a "no" or a strained path, explain the symbolic basis
  briefly and name constructive next steps, boundaries, timing considerations,
  or information the querent may need.
- Prefer language such as "the chart does not show an easy connection yet,"
  "this looks delayed or conditional," "the testimony is mixed," and "the
  answer depends on clarifying the question or data."
- Keep yes/no answers proportional to the data quality and testimony strength.
  When testimony is mixed or the chart is not radical, give a conditional
  answer rather than a dramatic conclusion.

## Retrieval guidance

- Always retrieve this horary module for `reading_type: horary`, then retrieve
  foundations, the requested tradition module, and ethics and scope.
- Retrieve the selected question house, querent significator, quesited
  significator, Moon condition, relevant dignity or reception modules, and
  aspect type modules for applying aspects between significators when available.
- For relationship, contract, conflict, career, home, money, travel, legal, or
  third-party questions, retrieve the corresponding house and ruler resources
  before adding minor testimony.
- For yes/no questions, prioritize significators, applying aspects, reception,
  Moon's next aspect, relevant house condition, and radicality cautions before
  timing or descriptive details.
- For timing questions, use perfection, the Moon's motion, angularity, and
  sign modality only when exact degrees and chart context are supplied. State
  uncertainty when timing indicators conflict.
- When exact modules are missing, fall back to broader planet, house, aspect
  type, tradition, ethics, and synthesis resources rather than inventing
  missing horary rules.
- If the question, time, timezone, location, house assignment, or radicality is
  unclear, state the confidence limit in the reading plan and avoid
  over-answering.
