# Synastry Exemplar Modules

Use these examples when a synastry reading includes the named inter-chart
contacts. They are examples of synthesis language, not verdicts. Combine them
with each person's natal relationship pattern, the exact aspect type, orb,
house overlays when reliable, dignity, sect, reception, and the user's stated
relationship context.

## Shared framing

- Treat each contact as a dynamic between two full agents rather than a claim
  about what either person secretly wants, feels, or intends.
- Name constructive and challenging possibilities without ranking the
  relationship as compatible, incompatible, destined, doomed, safe, or unsafe.
- Keep consent, communication, lived behavior, values, timing, and repair
  separate from symbolic chemistry or friction.
- Describe intensity as activation, not obligation. No contact requires anyone
  to pursue, endure, forgive, escalate, sexualize, or formalize a connection.
- If the relationship type is unclear, do not assume romance; these contacts
  can appear in family, friendship, creative, client, colleague, and other
  one-to-one bonds.

## Moon-Saturn

Use for one person's Moon in major aspect to the other person's Saturn.

### Constructive expression

- This contact can describe emotional steadiness, loyalty, practical care, and
  a willingness to take the relationship seriously over time.
- The Saturn person may help create structure, reliability, accountability, or
  containment around the Moon person's needs.
- The Moon person may soften Saturn's reserve by bringing feeling, memory,
  habit, and everyday care into the bond.
- With mutual consent and warmth, this can become a pattern of dependable care:
  showing up, keeping agreements, and making space for both vulnerability and
  limits.

### Challenging expression

- The same contact can feel heavy if responsibility replaces tenderness or if
  caution becomes criticism, withholding, or emotional control.
- The Moon person may feel protected, judged, stabilized, lonely, or deprived
  depending on context; the Saturn person may feel committed, burdened,
  cautious, or pressured to manage emotional needs.
- Age, authority, dependency, scarcity, or power differences need especially
  careful handling. Do not romanticize endurance when boundaries or consent are
  unclear.
- A useful synthesis names the work: warmth, repair, realistic commitments, and
  explicit permission to have needs without making either person responsible
  for the other's entire emotional life.

## Venus-Mars

Use for one person's Venus in major aspect to the other person's Mars.

### Constructive expression

- This contact can describe attraction, creative heat, play, flirtation,
  artistic collaboration, and a vivid exchange between pleasure and pursuit.
- The Mars person may activate desire, initiative, protection, or directness;
  the Venus person may draw, receive, soften, delight, or clarify what feels
  good and worthwhile.
- When handled skillfully, the pair can learn how to keep aliveness connected
  to respect: clear invitations, enthusiastic consent, and room for different
  tempos of desire.
- In non-romantic contexts, the same symbolism can support creative momentum,
  aesthetic courage, productive competition, or shared passion for making
  something tangible.

### Challenging expression

- Venus-Mars tension can feel magnetic without automatically being compatible,
  caring, mutual, or sustainable.
- The Mars person may come on too strongly, while the Venus person may feel
  excited, pressured, irritated, desired, or objectified depending on behavior
  and context.
- Differences around flirtation, sex, money, aesthetics, conflict, and pacing
  need direct negotiation rather than assumptions.
- Avoid wording that treats attraction as entitlement. A careful reading
  distinguishes chemistry from consent and passion from volatility.

## Sun-Moon

Use for one person's Sun in major aspect to the other person's Moon.

### Constructive expression

- This contact can describe recognition between one person's identity, purpose,
  or vitality and the other's emotional body, habits, and need for belonging.
- The Sun person may feel visible as an organizing presence; the Moon person
  may feel warmed, noticed, oriented, or invited into daily-life closeness.
- Constructively, the pair may support a sense of home around purpose: one
  person's direction and the other's responsiveness can help the relationship
  feel coherent.
- In friendship, family, or collaboration, this can show a natural rhythm
  between leadership and care, visibility and support, or initiative and
  responsiveness.

### Challenging expression

- Sun-Moon contact can become lopsided if the Sun person's agenda overrides the
  Moon person's changing needs or if the Moon person's moods become the measure
  of the Sun person's identity.
- The Moon person may feel cherished, exposed, dependent, or eclipsed; the Sun
  person may feel nourished, needed, watched, or emotionally obligated.
- A tense aspect can show mismatched rhythms between life direction and
  emotional security. Even an easy aspect still needs reciprocity and respect
  for difference.
- Keep the language non-deterministic: this contact may support belonging, but
  it does not prove that the relationship is meant to last or that either
  person must orient around the other.

## Mars-Mercury

Use for one person's Mars in major aspect to the other person's Mercury.

### Constructive expression

- This contact can describe quick conversation, debate, mental stimulation,
  tactical problem-solving, direct requests, and the courage to say what needs
  to be said.
- The Mars person may energize Mercury's thinking, speech, humor, or decisions;
  the Mercury person may give language, strategy, questions, or flexibility to
  Mars's desire and action.
- At its best, the pair can sharpen each other: clearer arguments, faster
  decisions, lively banter, and practical movement from idea to action.
- Consent-sensitive communication matters here too. Directness works best when
  both people agree on the tone, timing, privacy, and stakes of difficult
  conversations.

### Challenging expression

- Mars-Mercury tension can correlate with sharp words, interrupting,
  defensiveness, argument as stimulation, or decisions made before enough
  information is gathered.
- The Mars person may feel impatient with Mercury's questions; the Mercury
  person may feel pressured, provoked, criticized, or energized by Mars's
  urgency.
- This contact benefits from explicit rules for conflict: pausing before
  escalation, checking facts, separating critique from attack, and allowing
  either person to end or postpone a conversation.
- Avoid claiming that conflict proves passion or that hurtful speech is just
  chemistry. The chart can describe activation; the people choose the behavior.
