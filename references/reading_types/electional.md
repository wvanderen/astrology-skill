# Electional Readings

Electional readings compare possible start times for a chosen action and assess
which chart best supports the user's stated goal. They are used for timing
events, launches, agreements, ceremonies, applications, publications, and other
beginnings where the user has some practical choice about date, time, or
location. Electional astrology optimizes symbolic conditions for a beginning;
it does not guarantee outcomes, override preparation, remove risk, or replace
legal, medical, financial, safety, or logistical judgment.

## Baseline factors

- The concrete goal, action, participants, location, deadline, and practical
  constraints that define the election.
- The relevant house or houses for the matter, their rulers, planets placed
  there, and whether the elected Ascendant supports the topic.
- The Ascendant, chart ruler, and condition of the chart ruler: sign, dignity
  or debility when used, house, angularity, speed, retrograde condition,
  combustion, aspects, reception, and relationship to the goal.
- Moon condition: sign, dignity or debility when used, house, phase, speed,
  void-of-course status, combustion or proximity to the Sun, applying aspects,
  and the Moon's relationship to the topic ruler.
- Angular planets, especially planets close to the Ascendant, MC, Descendant,
  or IC, and whether they help or burden the elected beginning.
- Benefic support from Venus or Jupiter by dignity, angularity, house
  placement, reception, or close helpful aspects to the Ascendant, chart ruler,
  Moon, or topic ruler.
- Malefic mitigation for Mars and Saturn through sect, dignity, cadency when
  appropriate, reception, separating aspects, avoidance of close angularity, or
  constructive placement in houses where their nature serves the goal.
- Applying aspects among the Moon, chart ruler, topic ruler, benefics, and
  relevant natural significators.
- Avoidance or containment of severe afflictions to the Ascendant, chart
  ruler, Moon, topic ruler, and the house representing the matter.
- Repetition across house focus, ruler condition, Moon testimony, angularity,
  benefic support, and malefic containment.

## Goal definition and house relevance

- Begin by naming the electional objective in plain terms. A chart for "launch
  a product," "sign a lease," "hold a wedding," or "submit an application"
  should be judged by the houses and rulers that actually describe that action.
- Identify the primary house before optimizing the chart. Use the 1st for the
  actor or initiator, the 2nd for money and assets, the 3rd for messages and
  local movement, the 4th for homes and land, the 5th for creative releases or
  pleasure, the 6th for labor and routines, the 7th for contracts and
  partnerships, the 9th for publishing, travel, law, or study, the 10th for
  public launches and career visibility, and the 11th for groups, patrons, and
  audiences.
- Use the 8th or 12th only when the matter genuinely belongs there, such as
  debt, shared assets, taxes, hidden work, withdrawal, or confinement. Do not
  force every difficult topic into these houses.
- Keep the chosen house assignment transparent. If two houses are relevant,
  name the primary and secondary topic rather than blending them without
  explanation.
- The elected Ascendant should fit the goal when possible. Favor an Ascendant
  and chart ruler that can carry the action, support the relevant house, and
  avoid placing the matter in contradiction with the user's practical intent.

## Moon condition

- Treat the Moon as a primary indicator of flow, development, timing, and how
  the elected matter moves after it begins.
- Favor a Moon that is not void of course, not combust, not severely afflicted,
  and applying to a helpful aspect with the chart ruler, topic ruler, benefic,
  or relevant natural significator.
- The Moon's next applying aspect often describes the next development after
  the beginning. Prefer applications that support the goal and avoid making a
  difficult or irrelevant planet the first major contact when better options
  exist.
- A waxing Moon can support growth, visibility, and increase; a waning Moon can
  suit endings, release, reduction, or private work when that matches the goal.
  Do not treat phase as more important than the Moon's actual condition and
  aspects.
- A Moon in the relevant house or connecting well with the topic ruler can
  strengthen the election, especially when its condition is otherwise sound.
- If every available chart has lunar problems, describe the tradeoff plainly
  and choose the least conflicting option rather than presenting the election
  as ideal.

## Chart ruler and angularity

- The chart ruler describes the elected action's agency, durability, and
  ability to carry the matter forward. Prioritize its condition before adding
  minor refinements.
- Favor a chart ruler that is dignified or otherwise supported, direct when
  possible, visible when appropriate, free from close combustion, and connected
  to the relevant house or topic ruler.
- Angularity makes planets loud. Place helpful, relevant, or well-conditioned
  planets on angles when their symbolism supports the action.
- Avoid putting poorly conditioned malefics, debilitated topic rulers, or
  planets contrary to the goal tightly on angles unless the action specifically
  requires their symbolism and the chart contains mitigation.
- Planets in the 1st, 10th, 7th, or 4th should be interpreted as foreground
  conditions of the election, not background details.
- A strong MC or 10th ruler is especially important for public launches,
  career actions, reputation, visibility, ceremonies, announcements, and
  anything intended to be seen.

## Malefic mitigation and benefic support

- Do not aim for a chart with no difficulty; electional work usually manages
  tradeoffs. The goal is to reduce avoidable conflict and strengthen the parts
  of the chart that matter most.
- Mitigate Mars and Saturn by keeping them away from the Ascendant, Moon,
  chart ruler, topic ruler, and key angles when their symbolism does not serve
  the goal.
- A malefic can be more manageable when dignified, in sect, received by a
  relevant ruler, separating from key significators, cadent from the main
  action, or placed where discipline, severance, heat, courage, boundaries, or
  endurance are useful.
- Benefics are most helpful when they can actually contact the election's
  significators by dignity, reception, aspect, house placement, or angularity.
  A benefic that is isolated from the matter may be pleasant but less relevant.
- Favor Venus for harmony, attraction, agreement, art, pleasure, hospitality,
  relationship, and social ease when those support the goal.
- Favor Jupiter for growth, protection, legitimacy, teaching, publication,
  abundance, patronage, law, travel, and confidence when those support the
  goal.
- If a benefic is debilitated, combust, cadent, or under strong affliction, do
  not overstate its help. Describe the support as limited or conditional.

## Comparing candidate charts

- When multiple candidate charts are supplied, compare them against the same
  goal and house assignment so the ranking does not drift.
- Rank candidates by the factors most central to the matter: topic house and
  ruler condition, Moon condition and next aspect, chart ruler condition,
  angular planets, benefic support, malefic mitigation, and practical
  feasibility.
- Name each candidate's strongest support, main liability, and best use case.
  Avoid declaring a single chart "perfect" when every option contains
  tradeoffs.
- Prefer a chart with coherent repeated support for the goal over one that has
  one spectacular feature but damages the Moon, chart ruler, or topic ruler.
- If two charts are close, use practical constraints, the user's priorities,
  and the chart's most relevant repeated testimony to break the tie.
- If none of the candidates are acceptable, say so and identify the least
  problematic option only if the user must act within the supplied window.

## Optimization, not guarantee

- Frame electional astrology as choosing a more supportive symbolic beginning,
  not as control over the future.
- Strong elections still require preparation, consent, resources, skill,
  timing logistics, and follow-through. Weak elections do not doom an action.
- Do not promise success, safety, profit, fertility, legal victory, medical
  results, permanent relationship stability, or other high-stakes outcomes from
  an elected chart.
- Prefer language such as "this chart better supports," "this option reduces,"
  "this timing emphasizes," "the main tradeoff is," and "the election can help
  align the beginning with the goal."
- When the chart is mixed, separate what can be optimized from what cannot be
  known astrologically or controlled by timing alone.

  missing electional rules.
