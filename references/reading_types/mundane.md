# Mundane Readings

Mundane readings interpret charts for collective entities and shared conditions:
nations, cities, markets, institutions, weather, agriculture, and large public
events. They work from charts the user has already calculated for a moment and a
location — an ingress, lunation, eclipse, great conjunction, outer-planet
ingress, or a foundation/inception chart for a nation, city, or institution.
Mundane symbolism describes the collective *weather* of a period — which themes,
pressures, tensions, and capacities are foregrounded for a place or a group. It
does not predict political outcomes, market moves, disasters, conflict, public
health events, or the fates of leaders. Treat every mundane chart as symbolic
emphasis, not as foreknowledge of events.

## Baseline factors

- The entity or field the reading is about: a nation, city, region, market,
  commodity sector, institution, religion, political body, or the collective
  itself. If the entity is unclear, name that before judging.
- The chart type and the moment it was cast for: Aries ingress or another
  cardinal ingress, a lunation, an eclipse, a Jupiter-Saturn great conjunction,
  an outer-planet ingress or station, a transit contact, a synodic cycle, or a
  foundation/inception chart for the entity.
- Location the chart is set for, including whether it is the capital, the city
  affected, the market center, or a stated place; whether the Ascendant, houses,
  MC/IC, and angular planets depend on that location.
- Chart time, timezone, and date reliability, since a mundane chart set for the
  wrong location or time cannot support angle-, house-, or angularity-based
  claims.
- Ascendant of the chart as the collective entity, and the chart ruler as the
  entity's significator and condition for the period.
- Angular planets, especially planets close to the Ascendant, MC, Descendant, or
  IC, and whether their symbolism matches the entity's situation.
- Relevant mundane houses for the topic: the 10th for the government or
  leadership, the 2nd for the treasury, economy, and resources, the 4th for
  land, agriculture, and the homeland, the 7th for foreign relations and open
  conflict, the 9th for religion, law, and ideology, the 11th for the
  legislature and allies, the 12th for hidden or covert matters, and the 6th for
  public health and labor.
- Sign and triplicity correspondences for nations, regions, cities, and
  commodities when supplied by the source data or the user's stated tradition;
  do not invent or impose a correspondence list.
- The ruling planet or planets of the ingress, eclipse, or lunation sign, their
  condition, and the houses they govern.
- Lunations and eclipses contacting the chart's angles, the entity's
  foundation-chart factors, or relevant mundane house rulers.
- Jupiter-Saturn great conjunction phase: the current triplicity (element) and
  the time since the last conjunction, when the user supplies that context.
- Outer-planet positions, sign ingresses, stations, and major outer-planet
  aspects when the tradition is modern or blended and the data supplies them.
- The entity's foundation chart (nation/institution/company inception) when
  available, and contacts between the period chart and the foundation chart's
  angles, luminaries, ruler, and topic rulers.
- Repetition across chart type, sign emphasis, house focus, ruler condition,
  angularity, lunation or eclipse contacts, and the user's stated concern.

## Entity, chart type, and location context

- Begin by stating plainly which entity or field the chart describes and what
  kind of chart it is. A reading that mixes "the Aries ingress for London" with
  "the foundation chart of a company" without naming each will drift.
- Confirm the location the chart is set for. Ingress, lunation, eclipse, and
  return mundane charts are set for a place; their Ascendant, houses, MC/IC, and
  angular planets are location-dependent. If the location is missing, those
  factors cannot be judged and the reading must fall back to location-stable
  factors (see Missing location and entity context).
- Confirm the chart moment and timezone. Ingress and lunation charts are read
  for the moment of the astronomical event in the local time of the chosen
  location. A wrong timezone shifts the Ascendant and the whole house structure.
- Separate a foundation/inception chart (the entity's "natal" chart) from a
  period chart (an ingress, lunation, eclipse, great conjunction, or outer-planet
  contact). The first describes the entity's enduring collective temperament and
  capacity; the second describes a season of emphasis, pressure, or change within
  that structure. See Natal-like versus ingress or return-based mundane charts.
- Keep the entity's stated scope. If the user asks about a city, do not silently
  generalize to a nation; if the user asks about a market sector, do not
  generalize to a political outcome.

## Houses for collective entities

The mundane house assignments are the same twelve-topic structure used in other
reading types, read for a collective subject instead of an individual. Treat
these as topic routing, not as assertions about a specific chart.

- 1st: the people, the public mood, the general state of the entity.
- 2nd: the treasury, national or institutional wealth, revenue, currency, trade
  balance, and material resources.
- 3rd: neighboring nations and regions, transit, communications, the press,
  roads, and local movement.
- 4th: the land, agriculture, weather, crops, the homeland, real estate, and (in
  some traditions) the opposition or the roots of the polity.
- 5th: birth rate, children, public entertainment and spectacle, ambassadors and
  emissaries, schools, and speculative markets.
- 6th: public health, the labor force and working population, public servants,
  and routine collective operations.
- 7th: foreign relations, treaties, alliances, international trade, diplomacy,
  and open adversaries or conflict.
- 8th: mortality and death rates, shared or inherited obligations, national
  debt, taxes, and the partner's or counterpart's resources.
- 9th: religion, ideology, philosophy, the judiciary, higher education, long-
  distance travel and trade, and foreign affairs conducted through law or
  belief.
- 10th: the government, the executive, the leadership or head of state, public
  reputation, and the administration in power.
- 11th: the legislature, parliament, institutional allies, the entity's hopes
  and supporters, and organized civic groups.
- 12th: hidden enemies, secret or covert institutions, espionage, prisons,
  hospitals, large-scale confinement or suffering, and matters conducted out of
  public view.

Commodity, market, and sign correspondences are a traditional layer of mundane
work (signs and their rulers associated with goods, regions, and sectors). Use
them only when the source data or the user's stated tradition supplies the
correspondence. Do not invent a sign-to-nation or sign-to-commodity list, and do
not assert a region belongs to a sign from memory: classical lists are
geographically ancient and vary across traditions.

## Ingress charts and the Aries ingress

- An ingress chart is cast for the moment the Sun enters a cardinal sign: Aries
  (vernal equinox), Cancer (summer solstice), Libra (autumnal equinox), or
  Capricorn (winter solstice), set for the location of the entity.
- The Aries ingress is the primary annual mundane chart in the classical
  tradition. The moment the Sun enters Aries opens the symbolic year for the
  place; the chart's Ascendant, ruler, angular planets, and emphasized houses
  describe the tone and pressures of the coming year for that entity.
- The lunation immediately preceding the Aries ingress — the "new moon of the
  year" in the classical universal doctrine — is read alongside the ingress as
  the year's opening syzygy when the data supplies it.
- The quarterly cardinal ingresses are read as updates or refinements of the
  Aries ingress: the Cancer, Libra, and Capricorn ingresses describe seasonal
  developments within the annual theme.
- Read the ingress Ascendant as the entity's posture for the period, the chart
  ruler as the entity's operating significator, and the Sun as the year's
  vitality-and-purpose center. The house the ingress Sun occupies and the
  houses of any angular planets name where attention and energy collect.
- Ingress effects are symbolic emphasis for a period, not dated events. Where the
  user supplies a timing window (a fiscal year, a legislative session, a season),
  interpret the ingress within that window rather than asserting when something
  will occur.

## Lunations and eclipses

- Lunations (new and full moons) are read in mundane work as monthly markers of
  collective focus, activation, or culmination. The lunation's sign, the house it
  falls in for the location, and its aspects to angles and rulers describe the
  short-period collective theme.
- Eclipses are the strongest universal trigger in the classical mundane doctrine:
  solar and lunar eclipses are read by the sign and house of the eclipse, the
  regions associated with that sign, the ruling planet of the eclipse sign, and
  the mundane houses contacted.
- The path of the eclipse shadow (totality track) is a modern observational layer
  that locates where an eclipse is geographically most intense; use it only when
  the source data supplies it, and never as proof that a specific event will
  strike that region.
- Read the ruling planet of the eclipse sign for the quality, sector, and tone of
  what the eclipse emphasizes: its dignity, sect, house, reception, and aspects
  describe the condition of that period's pressures, not a verdict on outcomes.
- Classical convention scales an eclipse's symbolic influence to its magnitude
  and to the luminaries' condition, treating solar eclipses as longer-period and
  lunar eclipses as shorter-period. Apply any duration convention only when the
  data supplies the magnitude, and frame it as traditional symbolism rather than
  a dated prediction.
- Eclipses are especially prone to fear-based framing. Describe them as
  emphasis, exposure, culmination, or reorientation of collective attention, not
  as omens of disaster.

## Jupiter-Saturn great conjunctions

- The Jupiter-Saturn conjunctions occur roughly every twenty years and are read
  in mundane work as markers of longer collective cycles: shifts in economic,
  political, religious, and institutional order.
- The conjunctions stay within one element (triplicity) for about two hundred
  years before shifting to the next — the "great mutation" or triplicity shift —
  and that longer arc is read as a background framing for generations, not as a
  single event.
- The sign and house of the conjunction, the ruling planets, the location's
  Ascendant, and any close aspects describe the tone of the cycle. Read the
  planets' condition — Jupiter for growth, legitimacy, expansion, and belief;
  Saturn for contraction, structure, authority, limits, and consolidation.
- The great conjunction is grounded in the medieval Arabic/Latin mundane
  tradition; treat its timing conventions as symbolic period-framing, and never
  as a dated forecast of regime change, economic turn, or conflict.
- Where the user supplies the current triplicity and time since the last
  conjunction, use that as background context. Do not calculate conjunction
  dates, triplicity transitions, or "grand mutations" from partial data.

## Outer-planet ingresses and transits

- The modern mundane layer adds Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto: their sign changes
  (ingresses), stations, retrograde passes, and major mutual aspects are read as
  long collective arcs and generational pressures.
- Uranus is read for innovation, disruption, technology, revolt, and sudden
  shifts in collective organization; Neptune for collective idealism, mass
  movements, dissolved or blurred boundaries, the sea, oil, contagion of mood,
  and idealism or confusion; Pluto for concentrations of power, transformation,
  collapse and renewal, large capital, and covert or underworld collective
  forces.
- Outer-planet aspects to each other (for example, a Saturn-Pluto conjunction, a
  Uranus-Pluto square, a Uranus-Neptune conjunction) and outer-planet ingresses
  into a new sign are read as background pressures that color a period, not as
  specific dated events.
- Outer-planet symbolism belongs to modern or blended tradition modes. Do not
  impose Uranus, Neptune, or Pluto on a strictly classical reading. When the
  tradition is classical, restrict the long-arc layer to Jupiter, Saturn, the
  great conjunction, and the fixed-star and eclipse material the tradition
  supports.
- Read outer-planet contacts to the entity's foundation-chart factors, to ingress
  angles, or to mundane house rulers as activations of those factors, in the same
  way transits activate a natal chart. Keep the activation symbolic and
  non-deterministic.

## Chart ruler and angular planets

- In a mundane chart set for a location, the Ascendant is the collective entity
  itself, and the chart ruler is the entity's significator for the period.
- Assess the chart ruler's sign, house, dignity or debility when used, sect,
  reception, motion, combustion, and aspects to judge the condition, capacity,
  and cooperativeness of the entity through the period.
- Angular planets are foregrounded in mundane charts just as in natal and
  electional work. Planets close to the Ascendant, MC, Descendant, or IC become
  prominent forces, figures, or conditions for the period and should be read as
  part of the headline rather than as background detail.
- A strong, well-placed chart ruler with constructive angular planets can
  describe coherence, capacity, support, or visibility for the entity. A stressed
  chart ruler or afflicted angles can describe strain, exposure, instability, or
  pressure — framed as conditions to navigate, not as inevitable outcomes.
- When the entity has a foundation chart, the period chart's chart ruler and
  angular planets gain weight when they contact the foundation chart's angles,
  ruler, luminaries, or topic rulers.

## Moon condition

- Treat the Moon as a primary indicator of collective mood, public receptivity,
  flow, and how a period's themes move after the chart moment, paralleling its
  role in solar return, annual profection, electional, and horary work.
- In an ingress or foundation chart, note the Moon's sign, house, phase, speed,
  dignity or debility when used, combustion or proximity to the Sun, and
  applying aspects. A waxing Moon can support increase, visibility, and growth
  of collective matters; a waning Moon can suit consolidation, release, or
  withdrawal. Do not treat phase as more important than the Moon's actual
  condition and aspects.
- The Moon's next applying aspect often describes the next collective
  development after the chart moment, especially when it contacts the chart
  ruler, a mundane house ruler, an angle, or the entity's foundation-chart
  factors. Prefer applications that support the period's themes.
- A void-of-course, combust, slow, or afflicted Moon can describe stalled
  collective movement, reduced visibility, fatigue, or uncertainty — framed as
  a condition to navigate, not as failure. A well-supported Moon can describe
  coherence, responsiveness, or easier collective flow.
- In some classical mundane traditions the Moon signifies the common people and
  the public mood, complementing the Sun's link to leadership. Use that
  assignment only when the stated tradition supports it; otherwise read the
  Moon as collective receptivity and flow without forcing a specific
  signification.

## Classical path

In classical mode, begin with the chart type and concrete structure.

- Identify the chart type (Aries ingress, another cardinal ingress, the year's
  opening lunation, an eclipse, a Jupiter-Saturn great conjunction) and the
  location it is set for. The chart type governs the period the symbolism
  describes.
- Identify the sign and triplicity correspondences supplied for the entity or
  region, and the regions associated with the eclipse or ingress sign, using only
  correspondences the source data or the stated tradition provides.
- Read the Ascendant as the entity and the chart ruler as its significator.
  Assess the ruling planet of the ingress or eclipse sign for the quality of the
  period: its dignity, sect, house, reception, and close aspects.
- Route the topic through the mundane houses — 10th for the government, 2nd for
  the treasury and resources, 4th for land and agriculture, 7th for foreign
  relations and conflict, 9th for religion, law, and ideology — and judge their
  rulers and any planets placed in them.
- Treat eclipses as the strongest universal trigger. Read the eclipse sign, its
  ruling planet, the regions associated with that sign, the houses contacted, and
  the "colours" and quality of the event through the ruling planet's condition.
- Use the Jupiter-Saturn great conjunction for longer-arc framing: sign,
  triplicity, ruling planets, and condition of Jupiter and Saturn.
- Weigh sect, dignity, reception, combustion, bonification, and maltreatment of
  the significators before judging how reliably a period's forces can act.
- Use repetition across chart type, eclipse or ingress sign, mundane house
  rulers, angular planets, and the entity's foundation factors before making any
  strong claim. One striking placement should not outweigh converging testimony.
- Keep every classical judgment non-deterministic. The universal doctrine
  describes conditions, pressures, and tendencies for a place and period, not
  fated events.

## Modern path

In modern mode, translate the same structure into collective, systemic, and
archetypal language and add the outer planets.

- Read ingress, lunation, and eclipse charts as symbolic weather for collective
  attention: which themes, tensions, integrations, and developmental pressures a
  group is working with for the period.
- Add Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto as long-arc collective forces. Read their sign
  ingresses, stations, mutual aspects, and contacts to the chart's angles, the
  entity's foundation factors, and mundane house rulers as generational and
  systemic pressures coloring the period.
- Read the mundane houses as collective functions rather than fated sectors: the
  10th as how authority and governance are experienced, the 2nd as shared
  resources and economic mood, the 4th as land, ecology, and belonging, the 7th
  as relations across borders, the 11th as civic hopes and organized groups.
- Treat difficult configurations as meaningful collective material to integrate —
  exposure, correction, transformation, reorganization — rather than as doom.
- Keep the modern layer non-deterministic and analytical. Describe collective
  process, tension, and possibility; do not predict political, market, or
  conflict outcomes.

## Resolving mixed testimony

- If the ingress chart is constructive but a concurrent eclipse or outer-planet
  contact is stressful, name the tension: a broadly supported period may still
  pass through an exposed or pressured passage, and the timing of the two layers
  matters.
- If the chart ruler is strong but an angular malefic is afflicted, distinguish
  the entity's underlying capacity from the foregrounded pressure of the period.
- If classical testimony (eclipse sign, ingress ruler) and modern testimony (an
  outer-planet ingress) point to different sectors, present both as concurrent
  pressures rather than forcing a single verdict.
- If the entity's foundation chart is strong but the period chart is stressed,
  frame the period as a capable entity meeting temporary pressure. If the
  foundation chart is stressed and the period chart is supportive, frame it as an
  opening to work with difficult structural material.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence plainly. Collective periods often
  carry mixed pressures simultaneously; a reading that flattens them into a
  single prediction has overreached.

## Natal-like versus ingress or return-based mundane charts

- Some mundane readings use a foundation or inception chart for the entity — a
  nation's independence or founding chart, a city's charter, a company's
  incorporation, an institution's founding. Treat these like natal charts: they
  describe the entity's enduring collective temperament, capacity, topic
  structure, and recurring patterns. They should not be read as period weather.
- Other mundane readings use a period chart — an ingress, lunation, eclipse,
  great conjunction, outer-planet ingress, or transit contact — that describes a
  season of emphasis within the entity's longer structure. Treat these like solar
  returns or transits in individual work: foreground, activation, pressure, or
  opportunity for a period, anchored in the entity's foundation chart when one
  exists.
- When both are available, anchor period-chart claims in the foundation chart. A
  period chart can activate, spotlight, stress, support, or redirect a
  foundation-chart theme; it should not be read as creating a collective topic
  from nothing.
- When only a period chart is available, read the period's symbolism without
  making foundation-style claims about the entity's enduring character. Note in
  the reading plan that the entity's structural baseline is unknown.
- When only a foundation chart is available, read the entity's enduring patterns
  without asserting that a specific period will unfold. Offer period-level
  framing only when the user also supplies a timing factor.

## Missing location and entity context

- If the chart location is missing or unclear, you cannot judge the Ascendant,
  MC/IC, houses, house rulers, angularity, or planets near angles. State the
  limit, then fall back to location-stable factors: the eclipse or lunation sign,
  planetary sign placements, interplanetary aspects, the ruling planet of the
  ingress or eclipse sign, outer-planet positions and aspects, and the great
  conjunction's sign.
- If the entity is unspecified (which nation, city, market, or institution),
  do not assign one. Describe the chart's symbolic content generically and ask
  which entity or field it applies to before making entity-specific claims.
- If the entity is named but no foundation chart is supplied, restrict the
  reading to period-chart symbolism and avoid claims about the entity's enduring
  structure, natal-like patterns, or recurring destiny.
- If the chart time, timezone, or date is missing or approximate, avoid firm
  claims about the Ascendant, angles, houses, angularity, or the chart ruler.
  Treat the period thematically and name the missing data.
- If sign/region or sign/commodity correspondences are not supplied, do not
  fabricate them. Offer the period's symbolism without pinning it to a specific
  region or market.
- If house system is unspecified, use the chart's supplied houses without
  treating them as method-neutral. Whole-sign houses are common in traditional
  mundane work; quadrant systems can shift emphasis near cusps and at high
  latitudes. Name the method rather than silently switching systems.

## Avoiding event certainty and fear-based language

- Frame mundane charts as collective themes, symbolic weather, foregrounded
  pressures, and developmental conditions for a place or group — never as
  foreknowledge of events.
- Do not predict election results, the fall or death of leaders, war,
  revolution, terror or attack, market crashes or rallies, currency moves,
  commodity prices, natural disasters, famines, epidemics or pandemics, or other
  high-stakes collective outcomes from a mundane chart.
- Do not present eclipses, great conjunctions, Saturn-Pluto or Uranus-Pluto
  contacts, outer-planet ingresses, or fixed-star alignments as omens of
  catastrophe. These are the most fear-prone mundane symbols; describe them as
  emphasis, pressure, exposure, reorientation, or transformation of collective
  attention.
- Defer to the user's supplied timing. If the user gives a window (a fiscal
  year, a quarter, a season, a session), interpret the chart within that window.
  Do not assert when a collective event will occur if the user has not supplied a
  timing frame; describe the period's themes instead.
- Prefer language such as "this ingress emphasizes," "the chart foregrounds
  themes around," "one collective pressure in this period is," "this can
  correspond with attention to," and "this is better treated as symbolic weather
  for the period than as a forecast."
- Separate observation from interpretation. Describe what the chart shows (the
  signs, houses, rulers, aspects) before offering any symbolic reading, and keep
  the symbolic reading proportional to the data quality and the strength of
  repeated testimony.
- Keep agency and choice intact for the people and institutions the reading
  describes. A collective under pressure is not fated to a specific outcome, and
  a reading should not be used to justify fatalism, blame, panic, or
  astrology-driven political or financial decisions.
- Event-certainty and high-stakes claims must stay within the Output Guardrails
  in `SKILL.md` and the always-loaded `references/foundations/ethics_and_scope.md`.

## Source notes

- The classical universal (mundane) doctrine paraphrases Ptolemy, *Tetrabiblos*
  Book II (Robbins Loeb edition, Harvard University Press, 1940, hosted by
  LacusCurtius and stated there to be public domain in the U.S. via
  non-renewal): the "general" branch of astrology relating to "whole races,
  countries, and cities" (Book II introduction); familiarities between countries
  and the triplicities and stars (II.3); the examination of countries affected by
  an eclipse and the quality, class, and time of the predicted events (II.5-8);
  the colours of eclipses and comets (II.9); the new moon of the year as the
  Aries opening syzygy (II.10); and the signs' effect on weather and seasons
  (II.11-13). The Jupiter-Saturn great-conjunction cycle and its triplicity arc
  follow the medieval Arabic/Latin mundane tradition represented by Abu Ma'shar,
  *De Magnis Conjunctionibus*, and Bonatti, *Liber Astronomiae* (original and
  early Latin printings public domain; modern English translations copyrighted —
  paraphrased, not quoted). William Lilly, *Christian Astrology* (1647/1659,
  public domain), preserves ingress, lunation, and weather material in early
  modern English practice. The modern outer-planet layer (Uranus, Neptune, Pluto
  ingresses, stations, and mutual aspects as collective arcs) is modern
  synthesis grounded in the public-domain modern corpus in
  `references/modern_language_notes.md`; no copyrighted modern mundane-astrology
  text, tables, region lists, or examples are reproduced. See
  `references/classical_doctrine_notes.md` and
  `references/source_canon_and_rights_ledger.md` for the source pass, provenance
  tags, and edition rights.
