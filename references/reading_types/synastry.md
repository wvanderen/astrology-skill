# Synastry Readings

Synastry readings compare two natal charts to describe relational dynamics,
points of resonance, likely friction, mutual growth themes, and how each person
may experience the connection differently. Synastry does not determine whether
a relationship is "good," "bad," destined, doomed, or compatible by score. It is
best used as a reflective map of contact, contrast, needs, projections,
boundaries, and repair patterns.

## Baseline factors

- Each person's natal relationship pattern: 7th house, 7th ruler, Venus, Mars,
  Moon, relevant 5th, 8th, and 11th house factors, and repeated natal testimony
  about attachment, desire, trust, conflict, commitment, friendship, and repair.
- Inter-chart aspects between personal planets, luminaries, angles, chart
  rulers, Venus, Mars, Saturn, and the rulers of relationship-relevant houses.
- House overlays: where one person's planets fall in the other person's houses,
  especially angular houses and the 5th, 7th, 8th, and 11th houses when birth
  times are reliable.
- Repeated relational themes across both charts, such as several contacts to the
  same planet, house, axis, modality, element, or relationship significator.
- Asymmetry between partners: the same contact may feel bonding, activating,
  confronting, stabilizing, intrusive, or inspiring in different ways to each
  person.
- Reception, dignity, sect, and natal condition of contacted planets when the
  chosen tradition uses those factors.
- Data quality: birth time confidence, house system, exact degrees, orbs,
  applying/separating status, and whether angles or overlays are usable.

## Consent and scope

- Keep language consent-sensitive. Do not claim what either person secretly
  wants, feels, intends, or will do from the charts alone.
- Treat both partners as full agents. Avoid framing one person as the problem,
  savior, pursuer, victim, teacher, or karmic obligation unless the user has
  explicitly provided that lived context and wants it explored reflectively.
- Do not tell a user to begin, end, escalate, forgive, pursue, or tolerate a
  relationship solely because of synastry factors.
- Do not use synastry to override stated boundaries, uncertainty, no-contact
  choices, safety concerns, or lack of consent from either person.
- For third-party questions, keep the reading focused on the user's reflections,
  observable dynamics, and boundaries rather than private claims about the other
  person's inner life.

## Inter-chart aspects

- Prioritize close aspects involving the Sun, Moon, Ascendant, Descendant, chart
  ruler, Venus, Mars, Saturn, and relationship house rulers.
- Conjunctions and oppositions often describe strong mutual activation; squares
  often describe friction, urgency, attraction-through-difference, or recurring
  work; trines and sextiles often describe ease, recognition, or supportive
  flow. None are inherently proof of success or failure.
- Read the natal condition of each contacted planet before assigning meaning.
  A partner's planet may activate a theme the person already carries; it does
  not create the entire pattern from outside.
- Distinguish attraction from compatibility, intensity from care, familiarity
  from safety, and stability from emotional fulfillment.
- Saturn contacts can correlate with commitment, accountability, inhibition,
  duty, age or power differences, endurance, or heaviness. Assess consent,
  maturity, and mutual responsibility before framing them as binding.
- Outer-planet contacts can describe fascination, disruption, idealization,
  surrender, transformation, or collective-scale themes, but they require natal
  and relational context before being treated as central.

## House overlays

- Read overlays as the area of life where one person's presence may become
  especially visible to the other, not as proof of the planet person's intent.
- Angular overlays can feel immediate or hard to ignore when birth times are
  reliable. Planets near the other person's Ascendant, Descendant, MC, or IC
  often describe strong activation of identity, partnership, visibility, or
  private life.
- 5th house overlays may emphasize romance, play, creativity, pleasure, or
  validation. 7th house overlays may emphasize partnership, mirroring,
  projection, negotiation, or one-to-one expectation.
- 8th house overlays may emphasize vulnerability, trust, shared resources,
  intimacy, grief, fear, or psychological entanglement; use especially careful
  non-coercive language here.
- 11th house overlays may emphasize friendship, social belonging, shared aims,
  networks, and support beyond romance.
- If birth times are missing, approximate, rounded, or uncertain, label overlays
  and angular contacts as provisional or omit them. Prefer planet-to-planet
  aspects and whole-chart relational themes.

## Repeated relational themes

- Repetition increases relevance: multiple contacts to the same planet, axis,
  house, or ruler matter more than an isolated contact.
- Look for themes that appear in both natal charts and in the synastry, such as
  repeated Saturn-Venus, Moon-Mars, Mercury-Jupiter, 1st/7th, 5th/11th, or
  2nd/8th patterns.
- Weigh supportive and challenging testimony together. A relationship can show
  ease in communication and difficulty around security, or deep attraction and
  uneven emotional pacing.
- Separate recurring symbolic themes from deterministic outcomes. Repetition
  points to what deserves attention, not what must happen.
- When themes conflict, name the ambivalence directly: for example, "there is
  both strong emotional recognition and a need for clearer boundaries."

## Asymmetry between partners

- Interpret each direction separately. Person A's Moon in Person B's 10th house
  is not the same experience as Person B's Moon in Person A's 10th house.
- Note who is being activated and by what. The planet person may express a
  function; the house person may experience that function in a life area. In an
  aspect, each planet carries a different need, pace, and symbolic role.
- Avoid assuming equal intensity. One person may feel a contact as reassuring
  while the other feels exposed, pressured, inspired, or unsettled.
- Check whether synastry repeats one person's natal pattern more strongly than
  the other's. The relationship may be more central to one person's existing
  developmental themes.
- Use asymmetry to support empathy and boundaries: "this may land differently
  for each of you" is usually more useful than "you are compatible" or "you are
  incompatible."

## Avoiding compatibility scoring

- Do not produce deterministic compatibility percentages, rankings, soul-mate
  verdicts, red-flag verdicts, or claims that a relationship is astrologically
  meant to last or fail.
- If the user asks for a score, offer a qualitative synthesis instead: points
  of ease, points of effort, repeated themes, data limits, and practical
  reflection questions.
- Avoid counting harmonious versus difficult aspects as a verdict. Difficult
  contacts can support growth when handled with care; easy contacts can still
  be passive, avoidant, or insufficient for a real relationship.
- Keep the conclusion focused on dynamics, choices, communication, consent,
  shared values, timing, and lived behavior.

## Data quality limits

- If either birth time is uncertain, avoid confident claims about Ascendants,
  Descendants, MC/IC, house overlays, angularity, or house rulers that depend on
  exact angles.
- If exact degrees or orbs are missing, avoid ranking aspects as central unless
  the chart data clearly identifies them as close.
- If only one chart has reliable houses, use overlays only in the direction
  supported by reliable data and state the limitation.
- If the relationship type is unclear, avoid assuming romance. Synastry can
  apply to friends, family, creative collaborators, colleagues, clients, and
  other one-to-one bonds.

## Retrieval guidance

- Always retrieve this synastry module for `reading_type: synastry`, then
  retrieve foundations, the requested tradition module, and the natal module for
  each person's baseline relationship pattern.
- Retrieve each person's relevant natal factors before inter-chart synthesis:
  7th house, 7th ruler, Venus, Mars, Moon, chart ruler, luminaries, angular
  planets, and repeated natal aspects tied to relationships.
- For synastry contacts, prioritize exact inter-chart aspects involving
  luminaries, personal planets, angles, chart rulers, Venus, Mars, Saturn, and
  relationship house rulers.
- For house overlays, retrieve the relevant house modules and natal planet
  modules only when the receiving person's birth time is reliable enough to use
  houses.
- When exact modules are missing, fall back to broader planet, house, aspect
  type, tradition, ethics, and synthesis resources rather than inventing missing
  compatibility rules.
- For questions about whether to pursue, trust, leave, or escalate a
  relationship, retrieve ethics and scope first, then frame the reading around
  reflection, boundaries, and practical discernment.
