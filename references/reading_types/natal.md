# Natal Readings

Natal readings synthesize enduring chart patterns around life topics, temperament, motivation, relationships, vocation, resources, home, health routines, spirituality, and development.

## Baseline factors

- Ascendant, ascendant ruler, and angular planets.
- Sun and Moon condition.
- Sect and luminary relevance when provided.
- Houses and rulers tied to the user's question.
- Repeated aspect patterns or dignity themes.
- Birth time confidence, house system, and whether angles or houses are usable.

## Data quality limits

- If birth time is missing, approximate, rounded, or uncertain, prioritize
  planets by sign, aspects, dignity, sect if known, and whole-chart patterns;
  avoid confident claims about Ascendant, MC, houses, house rulers, angularity,
  or topics that depend on exact angles. Retrieve
  `references/foundations/birth_time_uncertainty.md` for standard scope
  language.
- If birth time confidence is low, label house-based interpretation as
  provisional and prefer "may be emphasized if the house placement is accurate"
  over firm topic claims.
- If no house system is specified, use the chart's supplied houses without
  treating them as method-neutral. Note that quadrant systems can shift house
  placement, especially near cusps, high latitudes, intercepted houses, and
  planets close to angles.
- Do not resolve house-system disputes inside the reading unless the user asks.
  Name the limit, then synthesize from factors that remain stable across
  systems.

## Common focus areas

For self-understanding, prioritize Ascendant, Ascendant ruler, Sun, Moon, chart
ruler condition, angular planets, sect light, Mercury for perception and
learning style, repeated elemental or modal emphasis, and close aspects to the
luminaries or chart ruler. Use signs for temperament and houses for where the
identity pattern becomes visible.

For vocation, prioritize the 10th house, MC, 10th ruler, planets in the 10th,
Sun, Saturn, 2nd and 6th house links, angularity, dignity and reception of work
rulers, and repeated testimony about work, skill, authority, service, reputation,
or visibility.

For vocation without a reliable birth time, do not substitute sign-based
interpretation for the missing MC, 10th house, 2nd house, 6th house, chart
ruler, or angular testimony. Name those unavailable factors first, then use
stable non-house factors such as Sun, Mercury, Saturn, Jupiter, Venus, Mars,
supplied dignity, close interplanetary aspects, and repeated sign or modality
themes as provisional vocational style rather than concrete career placement.

For relationships, prioritize the 7th house, 7th ruler, Venus, Mars, Moon,
relevant aspects to relationship significators, reception between rulers, and
repeated testimony about attachment, conflict, commitment, desire, repair,
reciprocity, and partnership patterns. Use the 5th for romance and pleasure, the
8th for shared vulnerability and entanglement, and the 11th for friendship or
community bonds when the question points there.

For resources, prioritize the 2nd house, 2nd ruler, planets in the 2nd, Jupiter,
Venus, Saturn, the 8th house and 8th ruler for shared resources, 10th and 6th
links for earned income, dignity and condition of resource rulers, and repeated
testimony about earning, saving, scarcity, support, stewardship, or material
priorities.

For home, prioritize the 4th house, IC, 4th ruler, Moon, planets in the 4th,
Saturn for roots and structures, Venus for comfort and belonging, the 10th/4th
axis for public-private tension, and repeated testimony about family patterns,
ancestry, dwelling, privacy, relocation, or emotional grounding.

## Retrieval guidance

- Always retrieve this natal module for `reading_type: natal`, then retrieve
  foundations and the requested tradition module.
- For broad natal questions, retrieve modules for the Ascendant or chart ruler
  when available, Sun, Moon, angular planets, and the strongest repeated aspect
  or dignity pattern.
- For topic questions, retrieve the relevant house module, its ruler placement
  module, planets in that house, exact aspects to the topic ruler, and any
  condition modules affecting the ruler. If exact modules are missing, fall back
  to the broader planet, house, aspect type, tradition, and synthesis resources.
- For questions about vocation, relationships, resources, or home, retrieve the
  focus-area factors above before retrieving minor points. Add minor points only
  when the user requests them or when they are supplied, exact, angular, and
  clearly tied to the question.
- For birth-time-sensitive questions, first check whether Ascendant, MC, house
  cusps, and house placements are reliable. If not, retrieve non-house-dependent
  resources and `references/foundations/birth_time_uncertainty.md` first, then
  state the confidence limit in the reading plan.
