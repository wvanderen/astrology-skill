# Mundane Exemplar Modules

Use these examples when a mundane reading is built around one of the named
chart types. They are synthesis models for collective *symbolic weather*, not
event predictions. Combine them with the entity the chart is set for, the
location, the chart moment and timezone, the supplied houses and angular
planets, the ruling planets' condition, and the user's stated collective
question. Always load `references/reading_types/mundane.md` first; it governs
the baseline factors, retrieval priorities, and the event-certainty and
fear-based-language limits these exemplars must respect.

## Shared framing

- Name the entity and the chart type before synthesizing. An Aries ingress "for
  London" and a great conjunction "for a stated nation" read differently from a
  foundation chart of a company; conflating them drifts.
- Confirm the location, date, and timezone before judging any angle-, house-, or
  angularity-based factor. The Ascendant, MC/IC, houses, house rulers, and
  planets near angles are all location- and time-dependent in ingress and
  conjunction charts. If any is missing or approximate, say so and fall back to
  location-stable testimony (sign, ruling planet, interplanetary aspects,
  outer-planet positions).
- Separate the entity's foundation chart from a period chart. A foundation or
  inception chart describes the entity's enduring collective temperament; an
  ingress, lunation, eclipse, great conjunction, or outer-planet contact
  describes a season of emphasis within that structure. Anchor period claims in
  the foundation chart when one exists; otherwise restrict the reading to the
  period's symbolism.
- Treat every mundane symbol as emphasis, pressure, exposure, reorientation, or
  capacity for a period — never as foreknowledge of an event. Do not predict
  election results, the fate of leaders, war, revolution, market moves,
  currency or commodity prices, disasters, epidemics, or other high-stakes
  collective outcomes from these charts.
- Keep agency intact for the people and institutions the reading describes. A
  collective under pressure is not fated to a specific result. Prefer language
  such as "this ingress foregrounds themes around," "one collective pressure in
  this period is," "this can correspond with attention to," and "this is better
  treated as symbolic weather for the period than as a forecast."

## Aries Ingress Reading

Use when the supplied chart is the Aries ingress (the moment the Sun enters
Aries, the vernal equinox) set for a location. This is the primary annual
mundane chart in the classical universal doctrine; its Ascendant, ruler,
angular planets, and emphasized houses describe the tone and pressures of the
symbolic year for that entity. The Cancer, Libra, and Capricorn cardinal
ingresses are read as seasonal refinements of this annual chart.

### Core synthesis

- Read the ingress Ascendant as the entity's posture for the year and the chart
  ruler as the entity's operating significator. A strong, well-placed chart
  ruler can describe coherence, capacity, or support; a stressed ruler can
  describe strain, exposure, or pressure to navigate — framed as conditions,
  not verdicts.
- Read the ingress Sun as the year's vitality-and-purpose center. The house it
  occupies for the location names where collective attention and energy collect.
- Foreground angular planets close to the Ascendant, MC, Descendant, or IC; they
  become prominent forces or figures for the year and belong in the headline
  rather than the background.
- Route the entity's stated concern through the mundane houses — 10th for the
  government and leadership, 2nd for the treasury and resources, 4th for land
  and agriculture, 7th for foreign relations and open conflict, 9th for
  religion, law, and ideology — and judge their rulers and any planets placed
  in them.
- Weigh the Moon as an indicator of collective mood, public receptivity, and
  how the year's themes move. Its phase, speed, condition, and next applying
  aspect add short-period texture; a waxing Moon can support increase while a
  waning Moon can suit consolidation, without overriding the chart's main
  testimony.
- Look for repetition across ingress sign, emphasized houses, angular planets,
  ruler condition, and (when supplied) the year's opening lunation and the
  entity's foundation-chart factors before making any strong claim.

### Classical path

- Identify the ingress sign (Aries by definition) and its ruling planet (Mars by
  domicile). Assess that ruler's sign, house, dignity or debility when used,
  sect, reception, motion, combustion, and aspects for the quality of the
  period's pressures.
- Read the Ascendant as the entity and the chart ruler as its significator;
  judge the chart ruler's capacity and cooperativeness through the period from
  its condition.
- Weigh sect, dignity, reception, combustion, bonification, and maltreatment of
  the significators before judging how reliably the period's forces can act.
- When the year's opening lunation (the new moon preceding the ingress) is
  supplied, read it alongside the ingress as the year's opening syzygy; treat
  its sign and ruling planet as additional testimony, not as a separate
  verdict.
- Use sign/region correspondences only when the source data or the stated
  tradition supplies them; do not invent a sign-to-nation or sign-to-region
  mapping.

### Modern path

- Translate the same structure into collective, systemic, and archetypal
  language. Read the ingress as symbolic weather for collective attention:
  which themes, tensions, integrations, and developmental pressures the entity
  is working with for the year.
- Add Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto as long-arc collective forces when the
  tradition is modern or blended. Read their contacts to the ingress angles,
  the entity's foundation factors, and mundane house rulers as generational
  pressures coloring the year — not as dated events.
- Treat difficult configurations as meaningful collective material to integrate
  — exposure, correction, transformation, reorganization — rather than as doom.
- Keep the modern layer non-deterministic and analytical. Describe collective
  process, tension, and possibility; do not predict political, market, or
  conflict outcomes.

### Resolving mixed testimony

- If the ingress chart is broadly constructive but a concurrent eclipse or
  outer-planet contact is stressful, name the tension: a supported year may
  still pass through an exposed or pressured passage, and the timing of the two
  layers matters.
- If the chart ruler is strong but an angular malefic is afflicted, distinguish
  the entity's underlying capacity from the foregrounded pressure of the period.
- If the entity's foundation chart is strong but the ingress is stressed, frame
  the year as a capable entity meeting temporary pressure. If the foundation is
  stressed and the ingress is supportive, frame it as an opening to work with
  difficult structural material.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence plainly. Collective years often
  carry mixed pressures simultaneously; a reading that flattens them into a
  single prediction has overreached.

### Grounded uncertainty and scope language

- "This ingress foregrounds themes around [house/sign topics] for the year, but
  it does not predict an election, a policy outcome, a market move, a conflict,
  or a disaster."
- "Because the Ascendant, houses, and angular planets depend on the chart
  location and time, I can only judge them if the location, date, and timezone
  are reliable; otherwise I'll read the location-stable factors (sign, ruling
  planet, aspects) thematically."
- "A strong chart ruler suggests capacity and support for the period, not a
  guaranteed result; a stressed one describes pressure to navigate, not failure."
- "Where the entity has a foundation chart, this ingress activates or stresses
  existing patterns rather than creating them; without a foundation chart I can
  only describe the period's symbolism, not the entity's enduring structure."
- Defer to the user's supplied timing window (a fiscal year, a legislative
  session, a season). Do not assert when a collective event will occur if the
  user has not supplied a timing frame; describe the period's themes instead.

## Jupiter-Saturn Great Conjunction

Use when the supplied chart is a Jupiter-Saturn great conjunction (the roughly
twenty-year synodic conjunction of Jupiter and Saturn) set for a location, or
when the user frames a period around the current great-conjunction cycle. This
is a longer-arc framing tool grounded in the medieval Arabic/Latin mundane
tradition; treat its timing conventions as symbolic period-framing, never as a
dated forecast of regime change, economic turn, or conflict.

### Core synthesis

- Read the conjunction's sign and the ruling planet(s) of that sign for the tone
  of the cycle: its dignity, sect, house, reception, and aspects describe the
  condition of the period's pressures.
- Read Jupiter and Saturn's condition together — Jupiter for growth,
  legitimacy, expansion, and belief; Saturn for contraction, structure,
  authority, limits, and consolidation. A well-disposed pair can describe
  ordered development and durable institutional capacity; a stressed pair can
  describe tension between expansion and constraint to navigate, not collapse.
- Foreground the conjunction's house for the location and any close aspects to
  the Ascendant, MC, or mundane house rulers. Where the conjunction contacts
  the entity's foundation-chart factors, read it as activation of existing
  structure rather than a force arriving from nowhere.
- Use the current triplicity (element) and the time since the last conjunction
  as background framing only when the user supplies them. The conjunctions stay
  within one triplicity for roughly two hundred years before shifting — the
  "great mutation" or triplicity shift is a generational arc, read as context,
  not as a single event.

### Classical path

- Identify the conjunction sign and its ruling planet; assess that ruler's
  condition for the quality of the cycle's pressures.
- Weigh Jupiter and Saturn's individual dignity, sect, reception, and mutual
  condition; judge whether the period's forces can act coherently or meet
  friction.
- Read the conjunction as a longer-arc complement to the ingress, eclipse, and
  lunation testimony already in the chart. It frames generations and decades,
  while ingresses and lunations frame seasons and years.
- Use region/triplicity correspondences only when the source data or the stated
  tradition supplies them; do not calculate conjunction dates, triplicity
  transitions, or "grand mutations" from partial data.

### Modern path

- Read the conjunction as a generational pressure on collective order: how
  growth and constraint, belief and authority, expansion and limits interact
  across a long period.
- Add the outer planets when the tradition is modern or blended. A concurrent
  Saturn-Pluto, Uranus-Pluto, or Uranus-Neptune contact colors the conjunction's
  cycle with transformation, disruption, or dissolution of collective
  structures — described as background pressure, not as a dated event.
- Keep the language systemic and non-deterministic. Describe collective
  process, structural tension, and developmental possibility; do not predict
  political, economic, or institutional outcomes.

### Resolving mixed testimony

- If the great conjunction is broadly constructive but a concurrent ingress or
  eclipse is stressful, name the layering: a long supportive cycle can still
  pass through pressured seasons, and the shorter-period chart locates the
  pressure within the longer arc.
- If Jupiter and Saturn testify differently (for example, a strong Jupiter with
  an afflicted Saturn), distinguish expansion capacity from structural strain
  rather than averaging them into one verdict.
- If classical testimony (conjunction sign, ruler) and modern testimony (an
  outer-planet contact) point to different sectors, present both as concurrent
  pressures rather than forcing a single reading.
- If testimony conflicts, name the ambivalence plainly; collective cycles often
  carry mixed pressures across their span.

### Grounded uncertainty and scope language

- "This conjunction frames a long collective cycle around [sign/house topics],
  but it does not predict regime change, an economic turn, a market move, a
  conflict, or the fate of a leader."
- "The current triplicity and time since the last conjunction are background
  context only; I won't date a 'mutation' or turn from partial data, and the
  cycle's symbolism is better read as a generational arc than a single event."
- "Where the conjunction contacts the entity's foundation chart, it activates
  existing structure; without a foundation chart I can describe the cycle's
  symbolism but not its effect on the entity's enduring character."
- "Jupiter-Saturn cycles describe the interplay of growth and constraint over
  time, not a guaranteed outcome; describe conditions to navigate and
  capacities to build, not fated turns."

## Retrieval guidance

- Retrieve this module after `references/reading_types/mundane.md` when the
  supplied chart is an Aries ingress (or another cardinal ingress treated as a
  seasonal refinement of it) or a Jupiter-Saturn great conjunction.
- Also retrieve the solar return module (`references/reading_types/solar_return.md`)
  for the period-emphasis framing of an ingress, and the relevant mundane house
  and ruler modules for the entity's stated concern.
- For a great conjunction, retrieve the Jupiter and Saturn planet modules
  (`references/planets/jupiter.md`, `references/planets/saturn.md`), the
  conjunction aspect module (`references/aspects/conjunction.md`), and the
  rulership modules for any houses Jupiter and Saturn govern in the chart.
- For the modern path, retrieve
  `references/traditions/modern/outer_planets.md` and the Uranus, Neptune, and
  Pluto planet modules when outer-planet contacts are supplied; restrict this
  layer to modern or blended tradition modes.
- For any high-stakes framing, load `references/foundations/ethics_and_scope.md`
  and keep claims within the event-certainty and fear-based-language limits in
  `references/reading_types/mundane.md`.
- Keep the final reading proportional to location and time reliability, entity
  clarity, foundation-chart availability, ruler condition, angularity, repeated
  testimony, and the user's stated collective question.
