# Opposition

Use this module when two chart factors are supplied in opposition. Combine it
with the relevant planet, sign, house, dignity, sect, reception, reading type,
and exact planet-pair aspect modules when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- An opposition places two factors across the zodiac from one another, creating
  polarity, confrontation, mirroring, awareness, and the need to hold both ends
  of an axis.
- It often describes tension between complementary needs, people, roles,
  houses, or perspectives that cannot be resolved by choosing only one side.
- Oppositions can manifest through relationship, projection, negotiation,
  comparison, culmination, visibility, and the pressure to integrate difference.
- The aspect can be difficult because the planets face each other directly, but
  it also offers clarity: each side makes the other more visible.

## Aspect dynamics

- Interpret the opposition as an axis. The planets occupy different positions
  but are tied into one field of meaning, often across paired house topics.
- One side may be over-identified while the other is projected onto partners,
  rivals, circumstances, or external demands.
- Integration usually requires alternation, negotiation, boundaries, and
  conscious relationship between the two factors rather than forced merger.
- The opposition can become skillful when the person learns to name the polarity
  clearly and create structures that honor both ends.

## Strength and orb

- Tight oppositions are strong and often highly visible, especially when they
  involve luminaries, angular houses, chart rulers, malefics, benefics, or
  planets tied to the user's question.
- A close orb intensifies the felt polarity and gives the two planets a stronger
  claim on one another's interpretation.
- Wider oppositions should be weighted according to the reading's orb standard,
  sign-based testimony, house relevance, and repetition elsewhere in the chart.
- If an orb is supplied, state whether the opposition is exact, close, moderate,
  or wide according to the reading context. Do not invent missing orb data.

## Applying and separating

- Applying oppositions show a polarity coming to a head: confrontation,
  awareness, culmination, decision, or encounter is building.
- Separating oppositions show a confrontation or realization already made, with
  the work moving toward response, integration, cooling, or consequence.
- In natal readings, applying oppositions may feel like an active developmental
  tension; separating oppositions may describe a more familiar pattern of
  comparison, projection, or negotiation.
- In timing work, application usually marks the lead-up to contact, while
  separation marks the period after exactness when the meaning is processed.

## Ease and tension

- The opposition is a hard aspect because it requires awareness of difference,
  distance, and competing claims.
- Its tension may appear as interpersonal conflict, inner contradiction,
  oscillation, projection, or being pulled between two houses or life topics.
- Its gift is perspective: the person can learn balance, mediation, strategic
  alternation, and mature relationship to an opposing need.
- Ease increases through reception, compatible dignity, benefic support,
  practical agreements, and the ability to recognize both sides as belonging to
  the same system.

## Classical aspect theory

- Opposition configures two signs of equal power across the zodiac (180°),
  including the commanding/obeying equinoctial pairs.
- Because the signs face each other directly, the opposition carries the clearest
  seeing and hearing of any aspect: each side makes the other fully apparent.
- Judgment can be by body (exact degree) or by sign (whole-sign opposition);
  sign-based opposition still ties the two houses and topics into one axis.
- Testimony across an opposition is mutual: each planet witnesses the other, and
  reception strongly shapes whether that witnessing is cooperative or
  adversarial.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House axis: interpret both occupied houses and the houses ruled by the
  planets. Oppositions often speak through paired life topics, relationship
  fields, and choices requiring balance.
- Planetary condition: identify which planet has more strength, dignity, sect
  support, angularity, or topical relevance when supplied. A stronger planet may
  dominate the axis or provide the more workable route.
- Projection and relationship: in relational questions, consider how one side
  may be carried by another person or externalized into circumstances.
- Orb and exactness: give close oppositions more interpretive weight; treat wide
  contacts as background unless reinforced.
- Applying or separating status: when supplied, describe whether the polarity is
  building toward confrontation or moving into aftermath and integration.
- User question: translate the polarity into the specific decision, pattern,
  relationship, or life area being asked about.
