# Sextile

Use this module when two chart factors are supplied in a sextile. Combine it
with the relevant planet, sign, house, dignity, sect, reception, reading type,
and exact planet-pair aspect modules when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- A sextile links two factors by a sixty-degree relationship, creating
  opportunity, cooperation, exchange, and workable support.
- It is a soft aspect, but usually more active and participatory than the trine:
  it offers an opening that benefits from choice, skill, effort, or response.
- Sextiles often describe resources, allies, techniques, conversations, habits,
  or adjacent life topics that can help one another when deliberately connected.
- The aspect is generally constructive, but its results depend on planetary
  condition, house topics, dignity, reception, and whether the opportunity is
  actually used.

## Aspect dynamics

- Interpret the sextile as an invitation. The planets can collaborate, but the
  person may need to notice the opening and act on it.
- Sextiles often connect compatible elements: fire with air, and earth with
  water. This creates practical cooperation without the automatic sameness of
  the trine.
- The aspect can show learning, coordination, mutual usefulness, supportive
  timing, and the ability to turn a potential into a concrete option.
- Sextiles are especially helpful for problem-solving because they offer a
  route around difficulty without denying that effort is still required.

## Strength and orb

- Tight sextiles are stronger and more usable, especially when involving
  luminaries, angular houses, chart rulers, benefics, malefics, or planets tied
  to the user's question.
- A close orb makes the opportunity clearer and more reliable.
- Wider sextiles should usually be treated as background support unless
  reinforced by house rulership, reception, angularity, repeated testimony, or
  timing triggers.
- If an orb is supplied, state whether the sextile is exact, close, moderate,
  or wide according to the reading context. Do not invent missing orb data.

## Applying and separating

- Applying sextiles show opportunity, contact, conversation, or cooperation
  building toward exactness.
- Separating sextiles show a supportive contact already made, with the work
  moving toward follow-through, use, or integration.
- In natal readings, applying sextiles may describe resources developed through
  initiative; separating sextiles may describe familiar skills or supportive
  patterns already available.
- In timing work, application points to an opening approaching, while separation
  points to the period after the opening has occurred and needs to be used well.

## Ease and tension

- The sextile is generally easy, but it is not passive. It often works best
  when the person takes the offered route, asks the question, practices the
  skill, or makes the connection.
- Its constructive expression is opportunity, cooperation, clever adjustment,
  useful relationships, and practical ways to mobilize resources.
- Its shadow is missed opportunity, underdeveloped talent, treating support as
  too minor to use, or assuming ease will become results without action.
- Tension may come from weak planetary condition, difficult house topics, poor
  reception, or the need to activate the aspect under pressure.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Planetary nature: describe the specific opportunity created by the planets
  involved and whether one planet can practically assist the other.
- House placement: connect the opportunity to the occupied and ruled houses.
  Note where resources, allies, skills, or adjacent topics can be linked.
- Sign element: when signs are supplied, use fire-air or earth-water
  compatibility to describe the style of cooperation.
- Orb and exactness: give close sextiles meaningful weight; treat wide sextiles
  as subtle support unless reinforced.
- Applying or separating status: when supplied, describe whether opportunity is
  building toward contact or moving into follow-through after contact.
- User question: translate the sextile into a concrete route, option, support,
  or practical next step relevant to the matter being asked about.
