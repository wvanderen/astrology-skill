# Conjunction

Use this module when two chart factors are supplied in conjunction. Combine it
with the relevant planet, sign, house, dignity, sect, reception, reading type,
and exact planet-pair aspect modules when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- A conjunction joins two factors in the same zodiacal place or very close by
  degree, blending their topics, functions, and timing into one shared field.
- It is the most immediate major aspect: the planets do not negotiate from a
  distance so much as co-present, merge, compete for expression, or amplify one
  another.
- Conjunctions can show intensity, focus, identification, fusion, emphasis,
  beginning, embodiment, and difficulty separating one factor from the other.
- The aspect is not inherently easy or hard. Its quality depends strongly on
  the planets involved, sign condition, dignity, sect, house topics, reception,
  speed, and support or pressure from other testimony.

## Aspect dynamics

- Interpret the conjunction as a shared agenda. Each planet modifies the other:
  Mars heats what it touches, Saturn concentrates or constrains, Venus softens
  or attracts, Jupiter expands, and so on.
- When planets have compatible natures or dignities, the conjunction may act as
  cooperation, focus, and mutual reinforcement.
- When planets have incompatible natures, poor condition, or competing house
  topics, the conjunction can feel crowded, compulsive, conflicted, or difficult
  to separate cleanly.
- The faster planet often carries the slower planet's symbolism into immediate
  experience, while the slower planet gives the faster planet a larger pattern,
  pressure, or developmental theme.

## Strength and orb

- Tight conjunctions are strong. The closer the orb, the more fused, visible,
  and difficult to ignore the combined significations become.
- Very close conjunctions can dominate interpretation, especially when angular,
  involving luminaries, chart rulers, malefics, benefics, or planets tied to the
  user's question.
- Wider conjunctions still matter when supported by sign-based co-presence,
  repeated testimony, house rulership, angularity, or a relevant reading type,
  but should be weighted less than close degree-based contact.
- If an orb is supplied, state whether the conjunction is exact, close,
  moderate, or wide according to the orb standards used by the reading context.
  Do not invent an orb when it is not provided.

## Applying and separating

- Applying conjunctions show an active coming-together: the matter is building,
  seeking contact, or moving toward a more exact union.
- Separating conjunctions show a contact already made: the symbolism may be
  integrated, remembered, cooling, or showing consequences and aftermath.
- In natal readings, applying contacts often feel more anticipatory or
  developmental; separating contacts may feel more established or already
  patterned. Keep this as a nuance, not a rigid rule.
- In timing work, applying contacts are usually more active for future
  development, while separating contacts describe recent activation or waning
  influence when the reading method supports that judgment.

## Ease and tension

- The conjunction concentrates rather than harmonizes. It can be powerful,
  efficient, and coherent, but it can also reduce perspective because the
  planets are too close to distinguish easily.
- Ease comes from compatible planetary natures, dignity, reception, benefic
  testimony, and clear outlets through house topics.
- Tension comes from conflicting planetary natures, debilitated condition,
  maltreatment, combustion, poor reception, or crowded significations that make
  one planet dominate or compromise another.
- Avoid calling a conjunction automatically fortunate or difficult. Judge the
  quality from the planets' condition and the user's actual question.

## Classical aspect theory

- Conjunction is co-presence rather than a ray cast across signs: the planets
  share one zodiacal place and therefore one topical and elemental field.
- Judgment can be by body (exact degree, the strongest form) or by sign
  (whole-sign co-presence); sign-based conjunction still configures the planets
  even when the degrees are loose.
- A planet casts its rays most intensely through bodily conjunction; combustion,
  cazimi, and under-the-beams are the solar forms of this co-presence.
- Witnessing and testimony: a benefic bodily present with a planet is the
  strongest constructive testimony; a malefic bodily present is the strongest
  form of pressure.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Planetary nature: describe how each planet alters the other's function, and
  note which planet has more condition, speed, dignity, sect support, or topical
  relevance when that information is supplied.
- House placement: place the fused significations in the occupied house and in
  the houses ruled by both planets. Angular houses increase visibility and
  agency; cadent houses may internalize, disperse, or specialize the contact.
- Sign and dignity: use sign condition to describe the shared style, resources,
  and constraints of the conjunction. Include dignity or debility only when
  supplied.
- Orb and exactness: weight close conjunctions heavily and wide conjunctions
  more lightly unless other testimony reinforces them.
- Applying or separating status: when supplied, use application to describe
  building contact and separation to describe aftermath, integration, or waning
  emphasis.
- User question: focus the conjunction on the relevant life topic rather than
  listing every possible expression of the planets involved.
