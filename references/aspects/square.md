# Square

Use this module when two chart factors are supplied in a square. Combine it with
the relevant planet, sign, house, dignity, sect, reception, reading type, and
exact planet-pair aspect modules when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- A square links two factors by a ninety-degree relationship, creating friction,
  pressure, obstacle, effort, and the need for active adjustment.
- It is a hard aspect that tends to externalize tension through conflict,
  urgency, blockage, repeated effort, or circumstances that demand action.
- Squares can be stressful, but they are also productive: they create movement,
  skill, resilience, and problem-solving when the person engages the tension
  consciously.
- The square shows where two planetary functions are trying to operate at the
  same time but do not naturally move in the same direction.

## Aspect dynamics

- Interpret the square as dynamic pressure. The planets push, irritate,
  challenge, or provoke one another into response.
- The aspect often describes a pattern that cannot remain theoretical. It asks
  for practice, correction, confrontation, discipline, or a change in strategy.
- Cardinal squares tend to act quickly and initiate conflict or change; fixed
  squares tend to hold pressure and resist movement; mutable squares tend to
  scatter, adapt, revise, or repeat the issue through changing circumstances.
- Planetary condition determines whether the square becomes constructive
  effort, chronic frustration, reactive conflict, or disciplined mastery.

## Strength and orb

- Tight squares are strong and should be weighted heavily, especially when they
  involve luminaries, angular houses, chart rulers, malefics, benefics, or
  planets central to the user's question.
- A close orb makes the friction more immediate and less optional.
- Wider squares can still matter through sign-based relationship, house
  rulership, repeated testimony, angularity, or timing triggers, but should be
  weighted less than close degree-based contacts.
- If an orb is supplied, state whether the square is exact, close, moderate, or
  wide according to the reading context. Do not infer missing orb data.

## Applying and separating

- Applying squares show active pressure building toward exact conflict,
  decision, effort, or correction.
- Separating squares show that the main contact has already occurred and the
  chart is describing response, consequence, repair, or integration.
- In natal readings, applying squares may feel more developmental and urgent;
  separating squares may feel more familiar, embodied, or patterned. Keep this
  distinction flexible.
- In timing work, applying squares often describe the lead-up to action or
  confrontation, while separating squares describe the aftermath and adjustment
  after exactness.

## Ease and tension

- The square is primarily tense. It describes incompatibility, effort, and
  friction rather than natural ease.
- Its constructive side is competence under pressure: the person learns through
  repeated engagement, boundaries, technique, and disciplined response.
- Its shadow is reactivity, blockage, burnout, unnecessary conflict, or trying
  to force one planetary need to erase the other.
- Ease increases through reception, dignity, benefic support, good timing,
  practical outlets, and willingness to work with the conflict directly.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- Planetary nature: describe the specific type of pressure created by the
  planets involved, especially when malefics, benefics, luminaries, or chart
  rulers participate.
- House placement: connect the friction to the occupied and ruled houses.
  Angular houses make the square visible and active; cadent houses may
  internalize, delay, or specialize the work.
- Sign modality and element: use modality to describe how the conflict moves
  and element to describe its style of expression when signs are supplied.
- Orb and exactness: close squares should shape the main interpretation; wide
  squares should qualify rather than dominate unless strongly reinforced.
- Applying or separating status: when supplied, describe whether pressure is
  building toward action or moving through consequence and repair.
- User question: frame the square as the specific work, conflict, decision, or
  skill-building process relevant to the question.
