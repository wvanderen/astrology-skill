# Mars Square Saturn

Use this module when Mars and Saturn are explicitly supplied in a square.
Combine it with the aspect type, house, sign, dignity, sect, reception, and
reading type modules when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- Mars signifies assertion, heat, conflict, speed, pursuit, defense, and action.
- Saturn signifies limits, structure, delay, fear, duty, endurance, and
  consequence.
- A square between Mars and Saturn describes friction between force and
  restraint.
- This can show blocked action, disciplined struggle, frustration, endurance
  under pressure, conflict with authority, or skill forged through repeated
  resistance.

## Natal guidance

- In natal readings, interpret this as a high-pressure pattern around will,
  anger, discipline, fear, and timing.
- The person may learn to act despite delay, build strength through constraint,
  or struggle with harsh internal authority and suppressed anger.
- Avoid fatalism. Judge severity by sect, dignity, reception, benefic support,
  house topics, and whether either malefic is strongly mitigated.

## Transit guidance

- In transit readings, this aspect can time hard work, delays, resistance,
  conflict with rules, injury-prevention themes, or the need to conserve force.
- Transiting Saturn square natal Mars is a major timing factor; transiting Mars
  square natal Saturn is shorter but can trigger frustration or confrontation.
- Counsel strategic pacing, realistic timelines, and avoiding unnecessary tests
  of strength.

## Synastry guidance

- In synastry, one person's Mars square the other's Saturn can feel binding,
  frustrating, serious, or demanding.
- The Saturn person may block or discipline the Mars person's initiative; the
  Mars person may provoke the Saturn person's control, fear, or boundaries.
- Constructive expression requires clear roles, consent, and respect. Without
  repair skills, the contact can become criticism, resentment, or stalemate.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: identify where action meets delay or resistance.
- Sect and dignity: weigh malefic condition before judging intensity.
- Reception: note whether restraint can guide action constructively.
- User question: frame the square as pressure requiring strategy, not proof of
  failure.
