# Venus Opposition Mars

Use this module when Venus and Mars are explicitly supplied in an opposition.
Combine it with the aspect type, house, sign, dignity, sect, reception, and
reading type modules when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- Venus signifies attraction, pleasure, affection, union, beauty, and value.
- Mars signifies desire, assertion, conflict, separation, pursuit, and heat.
- An opposition between Venus and Mars polarizes attraction and action. Desire
  may be experienced through another person, a competing value, or a visible
  tension between harmony and conflict.
- This contact can create magnetism and creative contrast, but may split
  softness and assertiveness into opposing roles.

## Natal guidance

- In natal readings, interpret this as a relational and creative polarity.
- The person may alternate between pleasing and pursuing, yielding and fighting,
  or seeking peace and provoking aliveness.
- Integration involves owning both attraction and assertion instead of assigning
  one side to partners or circumstances.

## Transit guidance

- In transit readings, this aspect can time relational confrontation,
  attraction, creative contrast, conflict about pleasure or money, or decisions
  about desire.
- Transiting Mars opposite natal Venus is sharper and more externalized;
  transiting Venus opposite natal Mars may bring Mars topics into relational or
  aesthetic awareness.
- Emphasize mirroring, negotiation, and the difference between tension that
  clarifies and tension that harms.

## Synastry guidance

- In synastry, one person's Venus opposite the other's Mars can feel magnetic,
  polarizing, and highly responsive.
- The Mars person may embody pursuit or friction; the Venus person may embody
  attraction, invitation, or values.
- This contact benefits from mutual ownership of desire so one person is not
  cast only as pursuer and the other only as prize or peacekeeper.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House axis: name where attraction and assertion face each other.
- Sign polarity: describe complementary styles and projection risks.
- Dignity and reception: assess whether the planets can negotiate.
- User question: center consent, reciprocity, and productive contrast.
