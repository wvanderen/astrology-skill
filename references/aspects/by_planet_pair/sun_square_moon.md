# Sun Square Moon

Use this module when the Sun and Moon are explicitly supplied in a square.
Combine it with the aspect type, house, sign, dignity, sect, reception, and
reading type modules when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- The Sun signifies identity, vitality, purpose, visibility, and the will to
  organize life around a coherent center.
- The Moon signifies body, emotion, instinct, habit, memory, and the need for
  safety, care, and belonging.
- A square between the luminaries describes friction between conscious direction
  and embodied need.
- This can show a life pattern where public purpose, family inheritance,
  emotional rhythm, and self-definition require active negotiation.

## Classical notes

- The two lights in square describe ongoing tension between the life-side
  (solar) and form-side (lunar) principles. Square aspects require adjustment
  and active management rather than passive ease.
- Sect still decides the leading light (Sun by day, Moon by night); the
  non-sect light is read as the modifying, less consistent voice.
- A Sun-Moon square falls near a quarter Moon phase (first or last quarter),
  which classically modifies the Moon's active quality — waxing-to-first-quarter
  moist and building, waning-to-last-quarter cold and releasing — when the phase
  is supplied.
- Reception by sign helps: if either light receives the other, the friction has
  a route for cooperation; without reception, the square tends to feel more
  internally divided.

## Modern psychological notes

- This aspect often describes a person whose conscious goals and embodied needs
  pull at different tempos, so that pursuing one can feel like neglecting the
  other.
- The pattern can show mixed messages early in life between being seen and being
  safe, or between autonomy and attachment, leaving a felt sense that self and
  need are at odds.
- Development is integrative rather than curative: the task is to translate the
  friction into practiced alignment between what one is becoming and what one's
  body and history require.

## Constructive expression

- Resilience and self-knowledge forged from having to reconcile competing inner
  currents rather than following a single one.
- Deliberate habits that protect both purpose and need: pacing, rest, and
  check-ins before major public or family commitments.
- Naming the tension aloud so that visibility and safety can both be on the
  table instead of alternating silently.

## Shadow expression

- Oscillating between self-assertion and emotional retreat, so that neither
  purpose nor need is consistently met.
- Treating emotional discomfort as proof that a chosen direction is wrong, or
  treating drive as permission to override bodily signals.
- Re-enacting family or parental friction (visibility versus care) in current
  relationships and roles without recognizing the pattern.

## Natal guidance

- In natal readings, interpret this as developmental tension rather than a
  defect. The person may need practice aligning what they are trying to become
  with what their body and history need.
- The aspect can describe mixed parental messages, inner conflict between
  visibility and safety, or alternating loyalty to autonomy and attachment.
- Strong testimony can produce resilience, self-knowledge, and the ability to
  make deliberate choices under emotional pressure.

## Transit guidance

- In transit readings, this contact can time tension between role and need,
  public obligation and private life, or a decision that exposes emotional
  ambivalence.
- Transiting Sun square natal Moon may illuminate a pattern that needs
  adjustment; transiting Moon square natal Sun is brief unless tied to a larger
  timing sequence.
- Emphasize pacing, bodily information, and concrete adjustment rather than
  treating the transit as a crisis by itself.

## Synastry guidance

- In synastry, one person's Sun square the other's Moon can create attraction
  and recognition with mismatched timing.
- The Sun person may press on the Moon person's needs; the Moon person may
  challenge the Sun person's preferred identity or direction.
- This can become constructive when both people distinguish personal purpose
  from emotional caretaking and build habits that respect both.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House placement: identify where purpose and need compete for priority.
- Sign modality: describe whether the tension initiates, holds, or adapts.
- Luminary condition: weigh dignity, sect, phase, angularity, and support.
- Reception: note whether either light receives the other by sign dignity.
- User question: frame the square as a specific integration task, not a fixed
  incompatibility.
