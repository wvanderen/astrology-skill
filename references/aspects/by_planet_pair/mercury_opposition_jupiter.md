# Mercury Opposition Jupiter

Use this module when Mercury and Jupiter are explicitly supplied in an
opposition. Combine it with the aspect type, house, sign, dignity, sect,
reception, and reading type modules when those factors are available.

## Core significations

- Mercury signifies thought, speech, messages, analysis, commerce, and
  translation.
- Jupiter signifies growth, belief, wisdom, teaching, law, abundance, and
  meaning.
- An opposition between Mercury and Jupiter polarizes detail and vision. The
  person or situation may learn through debate, counsel, contrast, or opposing
  interpretations.
- This contact can show intellectual range and persuasive dialogue, but also
  overstatement, contradiction, or outsourcing judgment to another viewpoint.

## Natal guidance

- In natal readings, interpret this as a polarity between fact and meaning,
  local information and broad principle.
- The person may be drawn to teaching, debate, advising, writing, languages, or
  systems of knowledge, especially when the house axis supports it.
- Integration involves holding the big picture without losing the terms,
  evidence, or practical limits.

## Transit guidance

- In transit readings, this aspect can time negotiations, announcements, study
  culminations, legal or advisory conversations, travel decisions, or a need to
  compare competing claims.
- Transiting Jupiter opposite natal Mercury is longer and may expand options;
  transiting Mercury opposite natal Jupiter is brief and often works through
  news or conversation.
- Encourage verification and proportional commitments.

## Synastry guidance

- In synastry, one person's Mercury opposite the other's Jupiter can create
  lively dialogue, teaching, debate, and mutual enlargement.
- The Jupiter person may offer meaning or correction; the Mercury person may
  offer questions, facts, and flexibility.
- The contact needs humility so difference of scale does not become condescension
  or nitpicking.

## Synthesis instructions

Modify interpretation by:

- House axis: name the areas where detail and worldview face each other.
- Dignity and reception: assess whether debate improves judgment.
- Timing context: treat brief Mercury contacts differently from slow Jupiter
  cycles.
- User question: emphasize dialogue, evidence, and wise proportion.
